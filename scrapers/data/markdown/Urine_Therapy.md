## Overview

Urine therapy refers to use of urine to maintain health, to prevent or cure sickness, to enhance beauty, or to promote meditation and spiritual enlightenment ( 21088 ).

## History

Urine therapy can be traced back at least 5,000 years to the early civilizations of the Aztecs, ancient Egyptians, ancient Chinese, and Native Americans. It is mentioned in the Bhava Prakasha, an ancient Ayurvedic text. Subcutaneous urine injections were used in the early 20th century under the name of auto-pyotherapy for urinary tract infections ( 21088 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, urine therapy is used for abdominal pain, aging, HIV/AIDS, atopic disease, anemia of chronic disease, infections, poisoning, asthma, atherosclerosis, androgenic alopecia, cognitive function, common cold, constipation, coronary heart disease (CHD), cough, dyspepsia, congestive heart failure (CHF), otitis media, fatigue, fever, flatulence, influenza, nephrotic syndrome,  rheumatoid arthritis (RA), myocardial infarction (MI), sickle cell disease, hypertension, infertility, insomnia, leprosy, liver disease, migraine headache, pain, parasites, pruritus, pulmonary embolism, Raynaud syndrome, scabies, sexual dysfunction, toothache, tuberculosis, cancer, urinary tract infections (UTIs), and vitiligo. Topically, urine therapy is used for burns, varicella zoster, atopic dermatitis (eczema), psoriasis, urticaria, and wound healing.

#### General

In a healthy person, urine is a sterile fluid containing 95% water, with a mixture of minerals, salts, hormones, enzymes, and urea.

#### Anti-cancer effects

Cancer antigens found in urine may activate the intestinal lymphatic system to produce antibodies. These antibodies may enter the bloodstream and attack cancer cells ( 21089 ).

#### Central nervous system effects

Melatonin and its conjugated esters are present in morning urine. Drinking the first morning urine leads to deconjugation of the ester metabolites in the gut and absorption of melatonin. This is proposed to produce night-time levels of melatonin in the plasma, and potential changes in the sleep-wake cycle ( 21095 ). Urine also contains muramyl dipeptide which has similar actions to serotonin. It induces slow-wave sleep in rats, rabbits, and cats ( 21096 , 21097 ).

#### Hormonal effects

Urine therapy has been proposed to have a role in the treatment of infertility. Urine from women contains the gonadotropins luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) ( 21094 ).
