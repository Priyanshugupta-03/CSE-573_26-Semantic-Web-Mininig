## Overview

Applied kinesiology is a chiropractic diagnostic and treatment technique used by integrative medical practitioners ( 98893 ). Applied kinesiology should not be confused with the practice of kinesiotherapy or general kinesiology, which does not claim to diagnose or treat disease.

## History

In the 1940s, American physiotherapists Henry and Florence Kendall developed procedures to test the strength of a specific muscle independently from surrounding muscles. Two decades later, George J. Goodheart, Jr., a chiropractor from Michigan, started using this approach to treat and diagnose disease and redefined it as applied kinesiology ( 98893 ).

## People Use This For

Applied kinesiology is a chiropractic technique used to diagnose and treat medical conditions such as mastalgia, Developmental coordination disorder (DCD), dyslexia, and attention-deficit hyperactivity disorders in children.

Possibly Safe when applied kinesiology is used as therapy or treatment. Applied kinesiology has been used as a method of treatment in clinical research without reports of serious adverse effects ( 15916 , 88720 , 98893 ). However, potential for harm exists when applied kinesiology is used as a method for diagnosis, as it could delay time to appropriate and accurate medical diagnosis and treatment.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Applied kinesiology is generally well tolerated. No adverse effects have been reported in clinical research ( 88722 , 98892 , 98893 ).

Likely Ineffective
Disease diagnosis.

While some preliminary clinical research shows that applied kinesiology might have a weak correlation to conventional diagnostic approaches in patients with thyroid dysfunction ( 88719 ), most research shows that kinesiology does not reliably identify or diagnose disease ( 15913 , 15914 , 15915 , 15917 , 88721 , 88722 , 98892 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Child development.

Preliminary clinical research shows that using a chiropractic treatment protocol including applied kinesiology might improve concentration, attention, impulsivity and performance when compared to baseline in children with developmental delay syndromes such as dyslexia, dyspraxia, and attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder ( 98892 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a control group.

Dyslexia.

Preliminary clinical research in children with dyslexia shows that applied kinesiology, along with physical manipulation, massage, homeopathy, herbal remedies, and neuro-linguistic programing, might increase academic and reading self-esteem, but does not improve cognition or literacy test performance, when compared to baseline ( 88720 ). Other preliminary clinical research in this population shows that using a chiropractic treatment protocol including applied kinesiology might improve concentration, attention, impulsivity, and school performance when compared to baseline ( 98892 ). However, the validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a control group. It is also unclear if these effects are due to applied kinesiology, the other interventions, or the combination.

Mastalgia.

Preliminary open-label research shows that applied kinesiology treatments might reduce breast pain when compared to baseline in women with mastalgia ( 15916 ). The validity of this finding is limited by the lack of a control group.

More evidence is needed to rate applied kinesiology for these uses.

During applied kinesiology, patients hold their arms extended from the shoulder and parallel to the floor. Patients resist the practitioner's downward pressure while holding or being exposed to a test substance. Diagnosis is based on the difference in arm strength with and without the test substance. Muscle weakness typically means the test substance is not advisable for the patient. Sometimes the practitioner presses "trigger points" to determine if they produce muscle weakness. In this case, muscle weakness is typically interpreted to indicate a buildup of toxins around the trigger point ( 98893 ). Certification as an Applied Kinesiologist is offered by the Touch for Health organization and the International College of Applied Kinesiology. Usually certification involves over 300 hours of instruction, proficiency exams, and submission of original research ( 98893 ).

Applied kinesiology is a chiropractic technique used to diagnose and treat conditions based on the belief that buildup of toxins around muscles results in weakness in that area. By identifying muscle weakness, the practitioner is able to identify areas of disease. However, there is no reliable scientific evidence that applied kinesiology is an accurate indicator of medical conditions or disease ( 15913 , 15914 , 15915 , 15917 ).
