# European Buckthorn
Scientific Name Rhamnus cathartica Family Rhamnaceae Other Common Names Buckthorn, Espino Cerval Europeo, Hartshorn, Highwaythorn, Kreuzdornbeeren, Nerprun, Nerprun Cathartique, Nerprun Commun, Nerprun Purgatif, Ramsthorn, Rhamni Cathartica Fructus, Waythorn
|

---

Caution

European Buckthorn should not be confused with the similarly named Alder Buckthorn , Sea Buckthorn , or Cascara Sagrada (California Buckthorn) .

## Overview

European buckthorn is a plant native to Europe, Northern Africa, and Western Asia, although it has also been naturalized in North America ( 102537 ). Traditional uses of the berry include constipation ( 2 , 90893 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used orally, long-term ( 90893 ). European buckthorn berry can cause potassium depletion, possibly leading to muscle weakness and disturbed heart function ( 2 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of European buckthorn when used short-term.

CHILDREN: Likely Unsafe when used orally in children younger than 12 years of age ( 90893 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally ( 90893 ); avoid using.

|

#### General

Orally, European buckthorn might have adverse effects.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Abdominal pain, cramps, watery diarrhea.

#### Serious Adverse Effects (Rare)

Orally : Albuminuria, hematuria, potassium depletion.

#### Cardiovascular

Orally, high doses or prolonged use of European buckthorn berry can cause potassium depletion, possibly leading to disturbed heart function ( 2 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, European buckthorn can cause abdominal pain, cramps, or watery diarrhea ( 90893 ). Also, prolonged use of anthraquinone-containing laxatives such as European buckthorn can cause pseudomelanosis coli (pigment spots in the intestinal mucosa) which usually reverses with discontinuation ( 2 , 6138 ). Pseudomelanosis coli is not associated with an increased risk of developing colorectal adenoma or carcinoma ( 6138 ).

#### Hematologic

Orally, high doses or prolonged use of European buckthorn berry can lead to hematuria ( 2 ).

#### Musculoskeletal

Orally, high doses or prolonged use of European buckthorn berry can cause potassium depletion, possibly leading to muscle weakness ( 2 ).

#### Renal

Orally, high doses or prolonged use of European buckthorn berry can lead to albuminuria and hematuria ( 2 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Constipation.

It is unclear if oral European buckthorn is beneficial in patients with constipation.

European buckthorn has a history of use for its laxative effects in patients with constipation ( 3 , 4 , 7 , 90893 ).

More evidence is needed to rate European buckthorn for this use.

#### Adult
Oral:

Research is limited; typical dosing is unavailable.

#### Standardization & Formulation

There is insufficient reliable information available about the standardization of European buckthorn.

#### DIGOXIN (Lanoxin)
Interaction Rating Major Do not take this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, overuse or abuse of European buckthorn might exacerbate the adverse effects of cardiac glycosides, including digoxin.

European buckthorn has stimulant laxative effects. High doses or prolonged use could lead to potassium depletion, increasing the risk of adverse cardiac effects ( 2 ).

---

#### DIURETIC DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, overuse or abuse of European buckthorn could increase the risk of hypokalemia associated with some diuretics.

European buckthorn has stimulant laxative effects. Overuse or abuse may cause potassium loss, compounding the hypokalemia occurring with some diuretics ( 19 ).

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVES
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

European buckthorn might have additive effects and side effects with other stimulant laxatives.

European buckthorn has stimulant laxative effects. Taking other stimulant laxatives concurrently might compound fluid and electrolyte loss ( 19 ).

---

#### WARFARIN (Coumadin)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, overuse or abuse of European buckthorn could increase the effects of warfarin and the risk of bleeding.

European buckthorn has stimulant laxative effects and can cause diarrhea ( 19 ). This might increase the effects of warfarin, which can increase the international normalized ratio (INR) and the risk of bleeding.

|

#### CARDIAC GLYCOSIDE-CONTAINING HERBS

Theoretically, overuse or abuse of European buckthorn can cause hypokalemia which increases the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity ( 19 ). See products that contain cardiac glycosides here .

---

#### HORSETAIL

Theoretically, concomitant use of European buckthorn berry with horsetail increases the risk of potassium depletion ( 19 ).

---

#### LICORICE

Theoretically, concomitant use of European buckthorn berry with licorice increases the risk of potassium depletion ( 19 ).

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVE HERBS

Theoretically, concomitant use of European buckthorn with other stimulant laxative herbs can increase the risk of side effects including potassium depletion ( 19 ). See other products with laxative effects here .

#### GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) CONDITIONS

European buckthorn is contraindicated in individuals with intestinal obstruction; abdominal pain of unknown origin; and intestinal inflammation, including appendicitis, Crohn disease, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), and ulcerative colitis ( 90893 ).

#### COLORIMETRIC TESTS

Due to its anthraquinone content, European buckthorn can discolor urine (pink, red, purple, orange, rust), interfering with diagnostic tests that depend on a color change ( 275 , 90893 ).

#### Presentation

Orally, European buckthorn berry, especially if taken chronically or in higher doses, can cause potassium depletion, possibly leading to disturbed heart function and muscle weakness, as well as albuminuria and hematuria ( 2 ).

#### Treatment

There is insufficient reliable information available about the treatment of overdose with European buckthorn.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of European buckthorn.

#### General

The applicable part of European buckthorn is the berry. Constituents of European buckthorn include anthraquinones ( 2 ).

#### Laxative effects

The anthraquinones in the European buckthorn berry can increase intestinal motility by inhibiting stationary contractions and stimulating propulsive contractions. Stimulation of active chloride secretion increases the water and electrolytes in intestinal contents, increasing the risk of electrolyte loss with overuse or misuse of the berry ( 2 ).
