Caution

Do not confuse cat's foot with Cat's Claw . Cat's foot is sometimes called Cudweed and should not be confused with Cudweed (Gnaphalium uliginosum).

## Overview

Cat's claw is a perennial herb. The flower has been traditionally used as a diuretic and for the treatment of gastrointestinal disorders ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of cat's foot.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### Immunologic

Cat's foot can cause an allergic reaction in individuals sensitive to the Asteraceae/Compositae family. Members of this family include ragweed, chrysanthemums, marigolds, daisies, and many other herbs.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of cat's foot.

#### Adult

Research is limited; typical dosing unavailable.
