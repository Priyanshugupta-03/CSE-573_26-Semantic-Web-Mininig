# Shirodhara
Other Common Names Ayurvedic Shirodhara, Jaladhara, Ksheeradhara, Massage Ayurvédique, Shiro Dhara, Shirodhara Therapy, Thakradhara, Thyladhara
|

---

Caution

Shirodhara is a technique used in Ayurvedic medicine, but it should not be confused with Ayurveda .

## Overview

Shirodhara is a technique used in Ayurvedic medicine. The word shirodhara means "head flow." Shirodhara involves the patient laying face up in a relaxing environment while a variety of liquids are gently flowed over the forehead ( 95860 , 95861 , 95862 ). Shirodhara therapy is becoming a popular service at day spas in North America.

Possibly Safe when used appropriately. Shirodhara has been administered in clinical studies with apparent safety for up to 21 days ( 95860 , 95861 , 95862 , 95863 , 106843 ). However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; however, there is no reason to suspect safety issues when used appropriately.

#### General

Shirodhara seems to be well tolerated. No adverse effects reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Allergic rhinitis (hay fever).

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for allergic rhinitis, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Anxiety.

Small clinical studies suggest that receiving shirodhara as an adjunct to other Ayurvedic therapies does not improve anxiety.

A small clinical study in patients with generalized anxiety disorder and social phobia shows that receiving shirodhara therapy with warmed Brahmi tail oil for 45 minutes daily for 7 days and taking Manasamitra Vataka tablets 100 mg twice daily for 30 days reduces anxiety when compared to baseline, but not when compared with taking Manasamitra Vataka alone ( 95863 ). Another small clinical study in young adults with generalized anxiety, social phobia, or panic disorder shows that receiving shirodhara therapy with buttermilk containing purple nut sedge and Indian gooseberry for 40 minutes daily for 7 days along with yoga does not improve scores related to anxiety, other psychiatric symptoms, or sleep quality when compared with yoga training alone ( 106843 ).

Attention deficit-hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).

It is unclear if shirodhara is beneficial in children with ADHD.

A small clinical study in children with ADHD shows that receiving shirodhara with milk for 45 minutes daily for 2 weeks in addition to taking an Ayurvedic herb mixture containing ashwagandha, bacopa, and valerian 1 mL/kg daily for 3 months improves visual and auditory reaction time when compared to baseline. The combination of shirodhara plus the Ayurvedic herbal mixture also improves total reaction time compared with taking the Ayurvedic mixture alone ( 95862 ). It is unclear if shirodhara improves other symptoms of ADHD.

Cognitive function.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for improving cognitive function, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Hearing loss.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for hearing loss, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Hypertension.

It is unclear if shirodhara is beneficial in patients with hypertension.

A small clinical study in patients with hypertension shows that receiving shirodhara 30 minutes daily for 3 weeks modestly reduces blood pressure when compared to baseline ( 95860 ). The validity of these findings is limited by a lack of control group.

Insomnia.

It is unclear if shirodhara is beneficial in patients with insomnia.

A small clinical study in patients with self-reported sleep problems shows that receiving shirodhara with sesame oil for seven 30-minute sessions over 2 weeks modestly improves sleep quality when compared to baseline and when compared with shirodhara with warm water ( 95861 ).

Psychological well-being.

It is unclear if receiving shirodhara is beneficial for psychological well-being.

A small, open-label clinical study in adults with breast cancer and symptoms of psychological distress shows that receiving shirodhara with the herb Jatamansi Taila for 20-30 minutes daily for 7 days in combination with shamana chikitsa, an Ayurvedic palliative treatment, does not improve anxiety and depression scores when compared with shamana chikitsa alone.  The validity of these findings is limited by the inclusion of patients with normal anxiety and depression scores at baseline.

Rhinosinusitis.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for rhinosinusitis, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Stress.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for stress, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Tinnitus.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for tinnitus, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

Vertigo.

Although there has been interest in using shirodhara for vertigo, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of shirodhara for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate shirodhara for these uses.

Shirodhara is a technique of pouring liquid such as water, oil, or milk on the forehead of a patient laying face up in a relaxing environment. It is administered by a trained practitioner such as an Ayurvedic doctor. It is often administered for 30-45 minutes daily for several days up to a few weeks. The liquid is poured in a specific pattern and with an even stream from about 3 inches above the forehead. Different medicated and non-medicated liquids are used depending on the conditions being treated. Commonly used liquids include water, various oils, buttermilk, or coconut water ( 95860 , 95861 , 95862 , 95863 , 106843 ).

Shirodhara literally means "head flow." It is a technique used in Ayurvedic medicine for "Urdhwa Jatrugata Vikaras" or disorders related to areas above the collar bone. The process of flowing medicated or non-medicated liquid on the forehead of patients is believed to improve circulation, relax the body, relax the mind, and reduce stress ( 106843 ).
