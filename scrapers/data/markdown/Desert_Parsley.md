## Overview

Desert parsley is the common name used to describe a genus of 70 to 80 perennial herbs native to western North America. Lomatium dissectum (fernleaf biscuitroot) is the most commonly used species of desert parsley. Desert parsley has been used by many Native American tribes to treat a wide variety of infections, particularly pulmonary complaints ( 12702 , 103804 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Traditionally used for asthma, influenza, other lung problems, as well as for wound healing, but there is no strong evidence to support its use for any condition.
- There is insufficient reliable evidence available about the safety of desert parsley when used orally or topically.
- No known major interactions.

## History

Historically, desert parsley has been used by Native American tribes ( 12702 ). Historical and traditional sources report that the desert parsley species Lomatium dissectum was used during the influenza pandemic of 1917 ( 103804 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, desert parsley is used for asthma, colds, cough, influenza, lung hemorrhages, pneumonia, tuberculosis, viral infections. Topically, desert parsley is applied as a poultice to treat sores, cuts, boils, bruises, sprains, and broken bones. The powdered root is applied to the skin to treat burns, boils, and other skin wounds. It is added to a steam bath to treat rheumatism, sprains, pain, and pneumonia. Desert parsley is also eaten as food. The root of desert parsley can be dried, ground into a powder, and mixed with cereal flours or added as a flavoring to soups. The roots have also been boiled to make a drink.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of any species of desert parsley.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, desert parsley may cause a painful rash ( 103804 ). Topically, no adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### Dermatologic

Orally, in a case report of a 74-year-old woman, the desert parsley species Lomatium dissectum caused a painful rash that was not improved with glucocorticoids. The rash resolved after discontinuing the product. Traditionally, desert parsley products with the resin removed are thought to prevent the development of rash ( 103804 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of any species of desert parsley.

#### General

The applicable part of desert parsley is the root. Desert parsley contains the coumarins osthol, jatamansin, pteryxin, lomatin, columbianin, and nuttallin ( 26161 ). Falcarindiol, coniferyl ferulate, ferulic acid, ligustilide, senkyunolide I, and trans-neocnidilide have also been identified ( 26162 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

In vitro, desert parsley constituents falcarindiol, coniferyl ferulate, ferulic acid, and ligustilide, have antibacterial activity against Bacillus subtilis and Staphylococcus aureus ( 26162 ).

#### Antiviral effects

In vitro, desert parsley inhibits the cytopathic effects of rotavirus, which causes gastroenteritis ( 12702 ).
