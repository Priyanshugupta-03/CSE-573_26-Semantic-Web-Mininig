## Overview

Columbine is a perennial herb indigenous to northern Africa and central and southern Europe. It is also found in the eastern US and in Asia ( 96561 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, columbine is used for general gastrointestinal disorders, to stimulate bile flow, and to regulate gallbladder contraction. It is also used for scurvy, jaundice, dermatitis, as a tranquilizer in patients with agitation, and as a diaphoretic.

#### General

The applicable parts of columbine are the leaves and stems, harvested during the flowering season. Columbine contains tannins, flavonoids, phenolic acids, triglycerides, and anthocyanins. The main flavonoid present is isocytisoside (4-methoxy-5,7-dihydroxyflavone 6-C-glucoside) ( 96561 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

In vitro, an ethanol extract of columbine leaves and stems has demonstrated activity against Staphylococcus aureus, Staphylococcus epidermidis, and Aspergillus niger ( 96561 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

In vitro, an ethanol extract of columbine leaves and stems inhibits superoxide anion formation and demonstrates antioxidant activity in a microsomal lipid peroxidation assay ( 96562 ).

#### Hepatoprotective effects

Columbine extracts have a protective effect against carbon tetrachloride-induced liver damage in mice ( 96562 ).
