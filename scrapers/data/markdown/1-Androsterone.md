Caution

Do not confuse 1-androsterone with similarly named compounds such as Androstenediol , Androstenedione , 4-Androsterone , Androsterone , Epiandrosterone and 19-nor-DHEA .

## Overview

1-Androsterone is a prohormone that is included as an ingredient in a variety of supplements marketed to athletes and bodybuilders. Prohormones are typically designed to be metabolized to anabolic compounds ( 91094 , 91095 ). Although there is very little known about these compounds, they may cause serious anabolic steroid-like side effects including stroke, kidney failure, and liver injury.

It is unlawful for anabolic steroids to be added to dietary supplements. The current definition of an anabolic steroid went into effect in the US in January 2005, under the Anabolic Steroid Control Act of 2004 legislation ( 8639 ). Anabolic steroids are also prohibited by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) ( 10164 , 102469 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used orally. 1-androsterone might have adverse cardiovascular, liver, and kidney effects ( 91095 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used orally. 1-Androsterone is converted to androgens in the body ( 90399 ). These might adversely affect pregnancy or nursing infants.

#### General

There is currently a limited amount of information on the adverse effects of 1-androsterone. A thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Dyslipidemia, transaminitis, renal dysfunction.

#### Cardiovascular

Significant increases in LDL cholesterol of around 29 mg/dL and significant decreases in HDL cholesterol of around 19 mg/dL have been reported in patients taking 1-androsterone for 4-weeks ( 91095 ).

#### Hepatic

Significant increases in AST of around 15.4 IU/L have been reported in patients taking 1-androsterone daily for 4 weeks. Despite the elevation, average AST levels did not go much beyond 40 IU/L, the upper limit of normal for AST ( 91095 ).

#### Renal

Significant increases in serum creatinine of around 0.2 mg/dL and significant decreases in GFR of around 16.4 mL/min/1.72m 2 have been reported in patients taking 1-androsterone for 4 weeks. Serum creatinine remained within normal range despite the elevation, GFR dropped below 89 mL/min/1.72m2, which is suggestive of renal impairment ( 91095 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Athletic performance.

It is unclear if oral 1-androsterone is beneficial for athletic performance.

Preliminary clinical research in healthy males undergoing resistance training shows that taking 1-androsterone 330 mg orally daily for 4 weeks moderately improves back squat performance when compared with placebo. Taking a-androsterone also decreased body fat mass by 3 kg and increase lean body mass by 5 kg when compared with placebo ( 91095 ).

More evidence is needed to rate 1-androsterone for this use.

#### HYPERLIPIDEMIA

Theoretically, taking 1-androsterone might also worsen lipid levels in patients with hyperlipidemia. Preliminary clinical research shows that 1-androsterone adversely affects lipid levels by increasing low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol by around 29 mg/dL and decreasing high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol levels by around 19 mg/dL in healthy men ( 91095 ).

#### KIDNEY DISEASE

Theoretically, 1-androsterone might worsen renal function in patients with kidney disease. Preliminary clinical research shows that 1-Androsterone increases serum creatinine by around 0.2 mg/dL and decrease glomerular filtration rate (GFR) by around 16.4 mL/min/1.72m2 in healthy men ( 91095 ).

#### LIVER DISEASE

Theoretically, 1-androsterone might worsen liver function in patients with liver disease. Preliminary clinical research shows that 1-Androsterone increases aspartate transaminase (AST) levels by around 15.4 IU/L, potentially worsening liver function in healthy men ( 91095 ).

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the presentation or treatment of overdose with 1-androsterone.

#### Metabolism

1-Androsterone is enzymatically converted to 1-androstenediol, 1-androstenedione, and 1-testosterone upon ingestion ( 90399 , 91095 ). Other hormones may ultimately result as byproducts of 1-androsterone metabolism. These include androsterone, epiandrosterone, 3alph-androstanediol, and 3beta-androstanediol ( 90339 ).

#### Excretion

1-Androsterone appears to be detectable in urine up to 2 days after taking a single 115 mg dose. Metabolites of 1-androsterone appear in the urine for up to 7 days post-dose. Some of these metabolites include 1-testosterone, 1-androstenedione, and 1-androstenediol ( 90399 ).

#### General

1-Androsterone is a prohormone which is enzymatically converted to 1-androstenediol, 1-androstenedione, and 1-testosterone upon ingestion ( 90399 , 91095 ). 1-Testosterone is an anabolic steroid shown to be twice as potent as endogenous testosterone; it is likely responsible for 1-androsterone's effects on body composition and muscle strength ( 91095 ). Other byproducts of 1-androsterone metabolism might also contribute to its physiological effects. These include androsterone, epiandrosterone, 3alpha-androstanediol, and 3beta-androstanediol ( 90339 ).
