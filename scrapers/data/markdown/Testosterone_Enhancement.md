## Overview

Testosterone enhancement involves the use of various substances to increase testosterone levels in the body, typically for the purposes of enhancing athletic performance. Testosterone enhancers are commonly marketed as dietary supplements and may be available as individual ingredients or in combination products containing multiple ingredients ( 101202 ). Refer to our lists of Testosterone Enhancers and Anabolic Steroids to find information on specific ingredients.

Many ingredients in testosterone enhancement supplements fall under the legal definition of a dietary supplement in the United States and are permissible for use before and during sporting events. However, several substances that are marketed as testosterone enhancers, including anabolic steroids, are banned by major sporting organizations and/or are considered illegal in the United States. In addition to allowing the user an unfair advantage, these chemicals may be unsafe ( 101008 , 101202 ).

Testosterone enhancers represent a wide group of herbs and supplements that are intended to increase testosterone levels in the body. The proposed mechanism of action for testosterone enhancement varies by chemical. Some testosterone enhancers are believed to increase testosterone levels by stimulating production of testosterone by the body ( 93419 , 94499 ). Other supplements are proposed to contain testosterone, which could increase testosterone levels through direct supplementation ( 95933 ). Anabolic steroids increase testosterone levels by increasing the synthesis of testosterone in the body while simultaneously blocking the receptor that inactivates testosterone ( 91095 , 97845 ). However, anabolic steroids also have a number of additional effects on the body. These chemicals block the effects of cortisol, a stress hormone, on muscle tissue, increase the rate of protein synthesis, and increase the rate of bone remodeling and growth. Together, this results in an abnormally rapid increase in muscle mass and strength ( 91095 , 101201 ).
