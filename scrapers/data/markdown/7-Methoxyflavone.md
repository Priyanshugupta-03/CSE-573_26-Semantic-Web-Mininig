## Overview

7-methoxyflavone is a methylated flavonoid that can be chemically synthesized but has also been isolated from extracts of Zornia brasiliensis and from plants in families including Meliaceae and Rutaceae plants ( 88785 , 88788 ). Athletes are interested in 7-methoxyflavone for bodybuilding due to its proposed effect on testosterone levels.  7-methoxyflavone is purposed to inhibit the aromatase enzyme.  Aromatase is responsible for converting testosterone and other androgens to estrogens. Therefore, by inhibiting aromatase, 7-methoxyflavone is thought to increase testosterone levels ( 88785 ). For this reason, some supplement manufacturers have added 7-methoxyflavone to supplements promoted for boosting testosterone and improving athletic performance.

## People Use This For

Orally, 7-methoxyflavone is used in supplements promoted for boosting testosterone and improving athletic performance.

#### AROMATASE INHIBITORS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

In vitro evidence shows that 7-methoxyflavone inhibits aromatase ( 88785 ). Theoretically, 7-methoxyflavone might have an additive effects when used with other aromatase inhibitors, potentially increasing the therapeutic effects and adverse effects associated with these drugs. Some aromatase inhibitors include anastrozole (Arimidex), exemestane (Aromasin), and letrozole (Femara).

---

#### TESTOSTERONE
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

In vitro evidence shows that 7-methoxyflavone inhbits aromatase ( 88785 ). Inhibiting aromatase reduces the conversion of testosterone and other androgens to estrogens. Theoretically, use of 7-methoxyflavone with testosterone might increase the effects and side effects of testosterone therapy.

#### CHRYSIN

In vitro evidence suggests that both 7-methoxyflavone and chrysin act as an aromatase inhibitors ( 7506 , 88785 ). Theoretically, use of 7-methoxyflavone with chrysin might increase the risk for adverse effects due to aromatase inhibition, including aggressive behavior, excessive sweating, adrenal insufficiency, kidney failure, and liver dysfunction.

#### Benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH)

In vitro evidence suggests that 7-methoxyflavone acts as an aromatase inhibitor ( 88785 ). Inhibiting aromatase reduces the conversion of testosterone and other androgens to estrogens, resulting in higher androgen levels. Androgens might worsen symptoms of BPH. Theoretically, use of 7-methoxyflavone might worsen BPH symptoms in some patients.

Insufficient available evidence.

#### Metabolism

Methylated flavones, including 7-methoxyflavone, have been shown to be more resistant to hepatic metabolism and have a higher intestinal transport capacity compared to their non-methylated counterparts in vitro. This suggests increased bioavailability and the potential for aromatase inhibition in vivo ( 88787 ).

#### General

7-methoxyflavone inhibits aromatase in vitro and is slightly less potent than one of the most potent flavone aromatase inhibitors, 7-hydroxyflavone ( 88785 ). Aromatase is the enzyme responsible for converting testosterone and other androgens to estrogens. Inhibiting aromatase increases testosterone levels. Because 7-methyoxyflavone has shown in vitro inhibition of aromatase ( 88785 ) supplement manufacturers have added 7-methoxyflavone to products marketed to boost testosterone.  However, no evidence has evaluated whether taking 7-methoxyflavone affects testosterone in humans.

#### Analgesic effects

Some animal research suggests that 7-methoxyflavone might have analgesic properties that are peripheral, not central, in nature ( 88786 ).
