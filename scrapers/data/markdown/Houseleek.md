## Overview

Houseleek is a succulent evergreen plant with fleshy, juicy leaves growing directly from the root. It is native to Europe ( 18 , 100621 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, houseleek is used for diarrhea, peptic ulcers, cancer, and diabetes. Topically, houseleek is used for burns, pressure ulcers, warts, insect bite, gout, otitis externa, and otitis media. It is also used as a gargle for oral mucositis.

#### General

The applicable parts of houseleek are the fresh leaves before flowering, and their juice ( 18 ). The juice contains glycosides of flavonols including kaempferol and quercetin. It also contains organic acids, primarily malic acid, with smaller amounts of citric, succinic, oxalic, ascorbic, and fumaric acids ( 100621 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

The juice from houseleek leaves has antimicrobial effects in vitro against Proteus mirabilis, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Staphylococcus aureus ( 100621 ).
