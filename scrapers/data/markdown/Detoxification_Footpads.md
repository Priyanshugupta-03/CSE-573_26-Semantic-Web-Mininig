## Overview

Detoxification footpads are adhesive pads that are promoted for removing toxins from the body ( 103105 , 103106 ). The most common components of detoxification footpads are tourmaline (a gemstone commonly mined in the United States) and bamboo or wood vinegars. Detoxification footpads have also included magnets, magnetic powder, chitosan, loquat leaf, vegetable fiber, and various herbs ( 103105 , 103106 ).

A number of sources have declared detoxification footpads to be a hoax. In 2009, the US Federal Trade Commission (FTC) charged the makers of Kinoki Foot Pads for false advertising. The FTC stated that the company used deceptive and false claims, including that the footpads would remove toxins from the body, lower blood pressure, treat depression, and promote weight loss ( 103103 ). In 2010, a judge ruled that Kinoki Foot Pads would be banned from sale in the US ( 103104 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of detoxification footpads. Detoxification footpads contain various ingredients. Some individuals may be allergic to some of these ingredients.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted. Detoxification footpads contain various ingredients. Some people may be allergic to some of these ingredients.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of detoxification footpads.

Detoxification footpads are typically applied to the soles of the feet and are worn for about 12 hours. They contain a special adhesive that allows them to remain in place throughout the night. The pads can also be applied to other parts of the body, including the arms, back, hips, or neck, although the manufacturers of the pads often recommend the feet for best effect ( 103105 , 103106 ).

#### BROKEN, DAMAGED SKIN

Detoxification footpads contain adhesive and a number of active ingredients ( 103105 , 103106 ). Avoid administering to infected areas, rashes, or open wounds, which may worsen broken or damaged skin.

Manufacturers of detoxification footpads claim that the products remove heavy metal toxins from the feet such as lead, arsenic, cadmium, copper, nickel, and zinc. These elements normally circulate in the human body and are excreted through sweat and urine. The manufacturers conclude that removing any excess heavy metals may be beneficial since heavy metal toxicity adversely affects multiple organ systems ( 103105 , 103106 ). Detoxification footpads claim to be based on the Japanese and Chinese philosophy of reflexology, where areas of the feet correspond to areas of the body. Theoretically, the toxins that may be afflicting an area of the body will also be present on the corresponding area of the foot. In this theory, removing the toxin from an area of the foot should detoxify the corresponding area of the body. Manufacturers claim that the pads work by removing toxins through osmosis. After use, pads are supposed to appear discolored, which is claimed to be due to the toxins ( 103105 , 103106 ). However, there is no evidence to support any of these claims.
