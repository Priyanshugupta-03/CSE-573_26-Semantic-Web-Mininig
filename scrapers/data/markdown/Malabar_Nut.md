## Overview

Malabar nut is a dense evergreen shrub growing to a height of 1-2.5 meters. It grows commonly in Southeastern Asia, including India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Burma, Nepal, and Malaysia. The leaves have been used in Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for 3000 years ( 48569 , 99091 , 99095 , 113561 , 113562 , 113563 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, Malabar nut is used for the upper respiratory tract infections, common colds, coughs, phlegm, asthma, bronchitis, and tuberculosis. It is used as an expectorant and secretory agent. It is also used as a bronchodilatory agent and mild spasmolytic. Topically, Malabar nut is used on the teeth to inhibit the development of dental plaque.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Malabar nut.

PREGNANCY: Unsafe when used orally ( 18 ); contraindicated.

LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Dental plaque.

Preliminary clinical research in adults with or without chronic gingivitis shows that using an Ayurvedic toothpaste (Sudantha, Link Natural Products) containing nine herbs, including Malabar nut leaf, twice daily for 12-24 weeks can reduce dental plaque compared with baseline or an unknown control toothpaste ( 99090 , 43570 ). Due to poor study methodology, and the use of nine different herbs in the toothpaste formulation, further study is needed to determine the effect of Malabar nut leaf dental health.

Upper respiratory tract infection (URTI).

Preliminary clinical research in patients with acute URTIs shows that taking a specific combination product (Kan Jang, Swedish Herbal Institute) containing extracts from Echinacea purpurea root, Malabar nut leaf, and Siberian ginseng root 30 mL orally once daily for 3-4 days does not reduce the severity or frequency of cough compared to bromhexine hydrochloride or placebo ( 95648 ). Other preliminary clinical research in patients with acute URTIs shows that taking this product 30 mL orally three times daily for 6 days does not improve the frequency or severity of symptoms compared to taking bromhexine hydrochloride or a combination of only Echinacea purpurea root and Siberian ginseng root ( 48569 ).

More evidence is needed to rate Malabar nut for these uses.

#### General

The applicable part of Malabar nut is the leaf. Constituents include pyrroquanazoline alkaloids, including vasicine, vasicinone, vasicol, pregnane, adhatodinine, adhavasinone, vasicinol, and vasicinolone ( 99091 , 99092 , 99095 , 113563 ). It also contains saponins, tannins, sterols, phenols, anthraquinones, phytosterols, triterpenoids, and flavonoids ( 113561 , 113563 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

Malabar nut leaves are traditionally thought to have antibacterial effects, and they are used to treat tuberculosis. The constituent vasicine has some antibacterial effects, but the derivative vasicine acetate, found in very low amounts in the leaf, is more active. Vasicine acetate and another constituent, 2-acetyl benzylamine, have antibacterial effects against Mycobacterium tuberculosis ( 99091 , 99096 ). In vitro research shows that alcoholic extracts of Malabar nut leaves have activity against Escherichia coli, and lesser activity against Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Staphylococcus aureus, and Bacillus subtilis. They also have activity against some fungi, including Aspergillus niger ( 113561 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

In vitro, Malabar nut leaf extract acts as an antioxidant and scavenges free radicals ( 105512 ).

#### Coagulation effects

An ethanolic extract of Malabar nut leaf, given by intraperitoneal injection, reduces the bleeding time in rodents with warfarin- or aspirin-induced coagulopathy from about 6 minutes to 2 minutes. The platelet count is increased in those pretreated with aspirin. The extract has no effect on coagulation parameters. The extract also increases aggregation of human platelets in vitro in a dose-dependent manner, possibly due to release of prostaglandins ( 113563 ).

#### Dermatologic effects

In a mouse model of atopic dermatitis, the alkaloid vasicine from Malabar nut decreases histopathological changes, restores epidermal thickness, and improves symptoms. It inhibits infiltration and activation of mast cells and T2 helper cells in the dermis, reducing levels of interleukin (IL)-4, IL-5, IL-9, IL-13, and immunoglobulin E in the serum and skin tissues ( 113564 ). In mice, bromhexine, a synthetic molecule derived from the alkaloid vasicinone present in Malabar nut, alleviates acute itch caused by histamine release induced by a mast cell destabilizer. It reduces histamine-induced transcription of NF-kappa-B, reduces activation of the kynurenine pathway, and inhibits the enzyme transmembrane protein serine protease 2 ( 113565 ).

#### Lipid effects

There is interest in using Malabar nut for hyperlipidemia. Research in rats with hyperlipidemia shows that consuming Malabar nut leaf extract 200 mg/kg daily for 14 days reduces triglyceride and low-density lipoprotein cholesterol levels and increases high-density lipoprotein cholesterol levels when compared with control. Taking Malabar nut leaf extract also reduces lactate dehydrogenase levels and adipose tissue weight ( 105512 ).

#### Respiratory effects

Malabar nut leaf is used for respiratory symptoms, such as cough and phlegm. The constituents vasicine and vasicinone have expectorant and bronchodilating effects ( 48569 , 113562 ). Isolated polysaccharides from Malabar nut leaves have antitussive effects in an animal model ( 99095 ). In a rodent model of acute allergic asthma, an aqueous extract of Malabar nut leaves reduces airway resistance, inflammation, mucus metaplasia, and subepithelial collagen deposition. It reduces levels of inflammatory mediators including interleukin (IL)-4, IL-5, IL-8, IL-13, IL-17A, and hypoxia-inducible factor-1-alpha. Aqueous extracts also reverse cellular hypoxia-induced mitochondrial function in human bronchial epithelial cells ( 113562 ).

#### Uterine effects

Malabar nut is contraindicated during pregnancy ( 18 ). The constituent vasicine is thought to have uterine stimulating effects ( 99091 ).
