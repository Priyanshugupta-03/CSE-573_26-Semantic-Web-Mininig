Caution

Scurvy grass is sometimes referred to as watercress, and should not be confused with Watercress (Nasturtium officinale).

## Overview

Scurvy grass is a flowering plant that is native to central and northern Europe, Asia, and North America. The dried leaves have been traditionally used to prevent vitamin C deficiency and scurvy, as well as for purported antiseptic, diuretic, and mild laxative effects ( 18 , 7122 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of scurvy grass.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

|

#### General

There is limited reliable information available about the adverse effects of oral or topical scurvy grass.

#### Dermatologic

Topically, scurvy grass may cause skin irritation ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, large amounts of scurvy grass can cause symptoms of gastrointestinal irritation ( 18 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Gout.

Although there has been interest in using oral scurvy grass for gout, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of scurvy grass for this purpose.

Vitamin C deficiency.

Although there has been interest in using oral scurvy grass for vitamin C deficiency, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of scurvy grass for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate scurvy grass for these uses.

#### General

The applicable parts of scurvy grass are the leaves and aerial parts or pressed juice. Scurvy grass contains glucosilinates, an unidentified volatile oil and bitter principle, tannin, and vitamin C ( 7122 ).

#### Irritant effects

The mustard oils in scurvy grass can irritate the mucous membranes ( 18 ).
