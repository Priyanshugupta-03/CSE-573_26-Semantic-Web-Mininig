## Overview

Herb Paris is a plant native to Europe, Russia, and the Caucasus ( 102538 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Traditionally used for pain, including headache, nerve pain, and rheumatoid arthritis (RA), but there is insufficient reliable evidence to rate its effectiveness for these uses.
- Likely unsafe when used orally. Side effects may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and respiratory paralysis.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, Herb Paris is traditionally used for headache, neuralgia, pain, rheumatoid arthritis (RA), genital tumors, arrhythmia, constipation, and spasms. It is also used as an emetic.

Likely Unsafe when used orally. The plant and berry are poisonous ( 18 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Unsafe when used orally ( 18 ).

|

#### General

Orally, Herb Paris can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, miosis, and respiratory paralysis ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, Herb Paris can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea ( 18 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, Herb Paris can cause headache ( 18 ).

#### Ocular/Otic

Orally, Herb Paris can cause miosis. Paristyphnin, a constituent of Herb Paris, is thought to be responsible for this effect ( 18 ).

#### Pulmonary/Respiratory

Orally, Herb Paris can cause respiratory paralysis. Paristyphnin, a constituent of Herb Paris, is thought to be responsible for this effect ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of Herb Paris.

#### General

The applicable part of Herb Paris is the whole plant with ripe fruit. Herb Paris contains triterpene saponins (pennogenintetra glycosides), also referred to as parissaponins, which cause local irritation. This increases absorption of the toxic constituent, paristyphnin ( 18 ).
