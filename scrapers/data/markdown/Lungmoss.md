## Overview

Lungmoss is a type of lichen commonly found on old trees in Europe. It is traditionally used as medicine ( 18 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, lungmoss is used for bronchitis, asthma, tuberculosis, and for coughs including irritable cough and smoker's cough. It is also used for stomach pain, atopic dermatitis (eczema), arthritis, diarrhea, menorrhagia, to promote sweating, and as an expectorant, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of lungmoss.

#### General

Constituents in lungmoss include rhizonaldehyde and rhizonyl alcohol ( 102767 ).

#### Gastroprotective effects

Lungmoss is traditionally used for conditions such as stomach pain and diarrhea. The gastroprotective effects of lungmoss might be related to the antioxidant effects of the constituent rhizonyl alcohol ( 102767 ).
