# Prolotherapy
Other Common Names Nonsurgical Reconstructive Therapy, Proliferative Therapy, Reconstructive Therapy
|

---

## Overview

Prolotherapy is based on the idea that chronic pain is often caused by loose ligaments or tendons. Ligaments connect bone to bone and tendons connect muscles to bone. When ligaments or tendons are lax, the muscles must work extra hard to stabilize the joint. Consequently, muscle pain and muscle spasm occur. Prolotherapy treatment involves injections of a dextrose (a simple sugar) solution near joints to promote growth and thickening of the ligaments.

## People Use This For

Prolotherapy is used to treat back pain, osteoarthritis, fibromyalgia, plantar fasciitis, sciatica, sports injuries, temporomandibular joint disorder (TMJ), tendinitis and tension headaches.

No serious side effects have been reported in clinical trials.

#### General

Patients usually experience tenderness or stiffness near the injection site for a few minutes to a few days after treatment.

Osteoarthritis.

The results of two double-blind human trials suggest that prolotherapy may effectively treat osteoarthritis.

A medical doctor of doctor of osteopathic medicine generally practices prolotherapy. Physicians specializing in orthopedics or physical medicine and rehabilitation are most likely to practice prolotherapy. Usually a series of injections of dextrose every few weeks is required to maximize results.

Human and animal studies have found that prolotherapy injections increase strength and thicken ligaments and tendons. The injections inflame the area causing the blood supply to increase and allowing nutrients to stimulate the tissue.
