Caution

Canadian hemp should not be confused with the similarly named Hemp and Hemp Agrimony . Also, Canadian hemp is sometimes called milk ipecac and should not be confused with the similarly named Ipecac and German Ipecac . Canadian hemp should not be confused with Gentian (also known as bitter root).

## Overview

Canadian hemp is a bushy shrub. The tough, fibrous bark has been used as a substitute for hemp ( 18 ).

Likely Unsafe when used orally. Contains cardiac glycosides that can cause toxicity ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Canadian hemp when used topically.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally due to the toxic potential of its cardiac glycoside constituents ( 18 ); avoid using.

|

#### General

Orally, Canadian hemp can cause adverse effects due to its cardiac glycoside content.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, vomiting.

#### Serious Adverse Effects (Rare)

Orally : Bradycardia, heart palpitations, hypotension, reflex hypertension.

#### Cardiovascular

Orally, Canadian hemp might cause bradycardia with low blood pressure, increased heart contractions, and reflex hypertension ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, Canadian hemp can cause topical irritation of the gastrointestinal mucous membranes. This irritation can result in a higher incidence of nausea and vomiting than with other cardiac glycoside-containing plants (such as digitalis) ( 18 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Androgenic alopecia.

Although there has been interest in using topical Canadian hemp for androgenic alopecia, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Canadian hemp for this condition.

Cough.

Although there has been interest in using oral Canadian hemp for cough, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Canadian hemp for this condition.

Heart failure.

Although there has been interest in using oral Canadian hemp for heart failure, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Canadian hemp for this condition.

Warts.

Although there has been interest in using topical Canadian hemp for warts, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Canadian hemp for this condition.

More evidence is needed to rate Canadian hemp for these uses.

#### Adult

All routes:Research is limited; typical dosing is unavailable.

#### DIGOXIN (Lanoxin)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, Canadian hemp might increase the effects and adverse effects of digoxin.

Canadian hemp contains cardiac glycosides which might have additive effects with digoxin ( 19 ).

---

#### DIURETIC DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, use of Canadian hemp with potassium-depleting diuretics may increase the risk of toxicity.

Canadian hemp contains cardiac glycosides, whose toxicity can be increased by the potassium depletion caused by some diuretics ( 19 ).

#### General

The applicable part of Canadian hemp is the root. Canadian hemp contains cardiac or digitalis-type glycosides, including cymine ( 18 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

Canadian hemp contains cardiac or digitalis-type glycosides, including cymine. These may cause bradycardia with low blood pressure, increased power of heart contractions, and reflex hypertension. Canadian hemp does not produce the same therapeutic effect for atrial fibrillation as digitalis ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal effects

Canadian hemp contains cardiac or digitalis-type glycosides, including cymine. These may be more irritating to the intestinal mucosa than digitalis and strophanthus preparations ( 18 ).

#### Genitourinary effects

Canadian hemp contains cardiac or digitalis-type glycosides, including cymine. These may increase diuresis ( 18 ).
