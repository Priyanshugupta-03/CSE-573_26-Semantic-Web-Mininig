# Cananga Oil
Scientific Name Cananga odorata forma. macrophylla (synonym: Canangium odoratum forma. macrophylla) Family Annonaceae Other Common Names Aceite de Cananga, Aceite De Cananga, Huile de Cananga, Huile De Cananga, Huile de Cananga Odorant, Huile de Cananga Odorata
|

---

Caution

Cananga Oil, which is derived from the Cananga odorata forma macrophylla flower, should not be confused with Ylang Ylang Oil , which is derived from the Cananga odorata forma genuina flower.

## Overview

Cananga oil is the oil from the Cananga odorata forma macrophylla flower. It is used as a flavoring agent in foods, as well as for fragrance in cosmetics and soaps.

Likely Safe when used orally in amounts commonly found in foods. Cananga oil has Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) status in the US ( 4912 ).

Possibly Safe when used topically ( 11 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of cananga oil when used orally in amounts greater than those found in food.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, cananga oil is well tolerated in amounts commonly found in foods. Topically, cananga oil seems to be well tolerated.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Topically : Allergic skin reactions.

#### Immunologic

Topically, cananga oil may cause allergic skin reactions ( 5244 ).
