# Anodyne Therapy
Other Common Names Light Therapy, MIRE, Monochromatic Infrared Photo Energy, Phototherapy
|

---

## Overview

Anodyne therapy is a type of light therapy that is intended to help relieve the symptoms of pain, stiffness, muscle spasms, and circulation irregularities. This form of treatment is usually given in addition to physical therapy. The device to administer Anodyne therapy is marketed and sold by Spectropad Systems. The scientific term for Anodyne therapy is monochromatic infrared photo energy (MIRE). Anodyne therapy is available through several different machine models. Model 480, with a higher preset energy setting, is designed for use by physical therapists and physicians. And Model 120, with a lower preset energy setting, and lower risk of skin burns, is designed for use by patients and nurses. The Spectropad System Anodyne therapy product has received clearance from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to be marketed. As a relatively inexpensive treatment, Anodyne therapy is gaining popularity in the United States. Certain insurance plans may reimburse patients for Anodyne therapy.

## History

Anodyne therapy comes from the word anodyne, meaning a medical treatment that soothes or relieves pain. Anodyne therapy was first used in 1994.

## People Use This For

Anodyne therapy is used for chronic pain, diabetic neuropathy, and myalgia.

The device administering Anodyne therapy is approved by the FDA for marketing and is generally considered safe when used appropriately.

PREGNANCY: There is insufficient reliable evidence about the safety of Anodyne therapy in pregnancy; avoid use.

LACTATION: There is insufficient reliable evidence about the safety of Anodyne therapy in lactation; however, there is no reason to expect safety issues with this technique.

#### General

If Anodyne therapy is used improperly or the machine is malfunctioning, patients receiving this therapy at risk for minor to severe skin burns.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Diabetic neuropathy.

Some clinical research suggests that monochromatic near-infrared light helps improve pain, and restore sensation and foot sensitivity in patients with diabetic peripheral neuropathy.

Patients who choose Anodyne therapy also usually participate in regular physical or occupational therapy. Most individuals receive Anodyne therapy at a healthcare provider's office. However, some individuals may also receive this treatment at home. Generally, the practitioner recommends that a patient receives three 30-45 minute sessions of Anodyne therapy per week for at least four weeks. The treatment is delivered by eight rectangular light-emitting pads that are placed directly on the skin. During treatment in an office, the patient generally sits still while the practitioner places the pads to the affected body parts. Some individuals experience a feeling of warmth during the treatment. Other patients may feel a tingling sensation or a small amount of discomfort. These symptoms generally disappear after a few minutes of the treatment. Patients may receive Anodyne therapy a maximum of three times daily. In these instances, the intensity of infrared light delivered to the patient's skin and the length of each session may decrease.

#### BROKEN, DAMAGED SKIN

Avoid administering to those with infectious skin diseases, rashes, or open wounds. Anodyne may worsen broken or damaged skin.

#### CANCER

Avoid using Anodyne therapy over active cancerous tumors, as it might worsen this condition.

Anodyne therapy is a type of light therapy using infrared light. Light therapy, also known as phototherapy, is the use of a specialized machine to emit a specific wavelength of the light spectrum via lasers, LEDs, fluorescent lamps, dichroic lamps, or full-spectrum light, for a prescribed amount of time. Each wavelength in the light spectrum is said to possess specific qualities. Generally, the affected body part is exposed in light therapy. Light therapy machines are able to offer more of a particularly useful wavelength than would be available by exposure to direct sunlight.
