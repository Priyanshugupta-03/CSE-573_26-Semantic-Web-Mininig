Caution

7-keto-DHEA should not be confused with its parent compound DHEA , other DHEA metabolites ( 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA , 7-beta-hydroxy-DHEA ), or Raspberry Ketone .

## Overview

7-keto-DHEA is a metabolite of the adrenal steroid, dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA), which is formed in the body. Unlike DHEA, it is not converted to androgens or estrogens in the body and does not activate androgen or estrogen receptors ( 92185 ).

Possibly Safe when used orally and appropriately, short-term. In clinical trials, 7-keto-DHEA has been used safely in doses up to 100 mg twice daily for up to 8 weeks ( 10404 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

|

#### General

Orally, 7-keto-DHEA seems to be well tolerated when used short-term.

#### Cardiovascular

Orally, 7-keto-DHEA may modestly decrease blood pressure in some patients ( 10404 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, 7-keto-DHEA has been associated with two reports of nausea in one clinical study ( 92185 ).

#### Hematologic

A case report describes decreased hemoglobin and hematocrit levels in a man taking 7-keto-DHEA orally, 25 mg twice daily for 7 days ( 10404 ). The clinical significance of this is not clear.

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, 7-keto-DHEA has been associated with one report of vertigo in one clinical study ( 92185 ).

#### Absorption

After oral administration, 7-keto-DHEA and 7-keto-DHEA acetate are not detected in the blood, being rapidly converted to sulfate salts. In healthy men less than 50 years old, the mean time to peak plasma levels of 7-keto-DHEA sulfate is 2.2 hours, and the apparent mean volume of distribution is 540 liters ( 10404 ). 7-keto-DHEA does not appear to accumulate when taken orally at a dose of 100 mg of the acetate twice daily for 28 days. Trough plasma levels on days 7 and 28 were 15.8 mcg/L and 16.3 mcg/L respectively ( 10404 ).

#### Distribution

In healthy men less than 50 years of age the apparent mean volume of distribution is 540 liters ( 10404 ).

#### Metabolism

7-Keto-DHEA is metabolized to 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA and 7-beta-hydroxy-DHEA ( 93203 ). After topical application of 7-keto-DHEA 25 mg daily in a gel formulation for 8 days, there is a 160% increase in plasma levels of 7-beta-hydroxy-DHEA, and a 28% increase in plasma levels of 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA levels ( 93204 ).

#### Excretion

The mean half-life of 7-keto-DHEA sulfate in healthy men under 50 years of age is 2.17 hours ( 10404 ).

#### General

7-keto-DHEA is formed in the body by metabolism of the adrenal steroid, dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA) ( 5837 ). DHEA is metabolized to 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA which is then oxidized to 7-keto-DHEA ( 10404 ). Unlike DHEA, 7-keto-DHEA is not converted to androgens and estrogens, and it does not activate androgen receptors ( 5837 , 5840 , 5842 , 10404 ). It is thought that 7-keto-DHEA, 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA, and 7-beta-hydroxy-DHEA can be interconverted in various tissues in the body ( 93204 ). Oral or topical administration of 7-keto-DHEA does not seem to have a clinically significant effect on plasma levels of steroid hormones ( 10404 , 10405 ). In one study in healthy men aged 21-47 years, there were small decreases in total blood testosterone levels and small increases in free testosterone levels, but both remained within the normal range ( 10404 ).

#### Circulatory effects

Thermogenic effects of 7-keto-DHEA, producing increases in mitochondrial heat production, have been suggested as a potential treatment for Raynaud's phenomenon. It is theorized that, in order to maintain the core body temperature, the extra heat produced will be dispersed to the periphery via the circulation ( 93201 ).

#### Endocrine effects

7-keto-DHEA has effects that mimic thyroid hormones. Both 7-keto-DHEA and thyroxine stimulate production of liver mitochondrial glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase and malic enzyme, increasing thermogenesis ( 92187 ). 7-keto-DHEA can normalize an induced hypothyroid state in animals, and significantly increases triiodothyronine (T3) levels in overweight adults taking 100 mg twice daily for 8 weeks, although levels remained within normal limits ( 5842 ). In this study there was no significant change in thyroxine (T4) or thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH) levels. In healthy men who applied 7-keto-DHEA gel topically, 25 mg daily for 8 days, there was a small decrease in plasma TSH, T3 and T4 during treatment, followed by a small rise immediately after stopping treatment, and then a return to baseline ( 93203 ).

#### Immunologic effects

7-keto-DHEA is reported to increase interleukin-2 (IL-2) production by human mononuclear leukocytes in vitro ( 5841 , 10404 ).

#### Psychiatric effects

7-Keto-DHEA reduces oral alcohol intake in animals previously habituated to accessing it on-demand ( 92186 ).

#### Weight loss effects

7-keto-DHEA is thought to produce weight loss by increasing basal metabolic rate and thermogenesis. Evidence in animals suggests it stimulates the thermogenic enzymes glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase and malic enzyme in the liver, and increases the rate of mitochondrial substrate oxidation, catalase activity, and fatty acyl-CoA oxidase activity ( 5837 , 92185 , 92187 , 93202 ). These effects on the rate of mitochondrial respiration and thermogenesis are similar to the effects of thyroid hormones ( 93202 ). 7-keto-DHEA is also thought to increase synthesis of uncoupling proteins, allowing entry of protons into the mitochondria where the free energy from substrate oxidation is converted to heat, instead of being conserved through adenosine triphosphate (ATP) synthesis ( 93201 ). 7-keto-DHEA is between 2.5 and 4 times as active as DHEA as an inducer of thermogenesis ( 10404 , 93201 ). In obese adults on a calorie-restricted diet, taking 7-keto-DHEA 100 mg twice daily for 7 days increased the basal metabolic rate by 1.4%, compared to a decrease of 3.9% seen with the diet alone ( 92185 ).
