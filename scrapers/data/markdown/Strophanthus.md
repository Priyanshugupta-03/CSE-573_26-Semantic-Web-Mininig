# Strophanthus
Scientific Name Strophanthus caudatus, Strophanthus divaricatus, Strophanthus gratus (synonym: Roupellia grata), Strophanthus hispidus, Strophanthus kombe, Strophanthus sarmentosus, Strophanthus wallichii Family Apocynaceae Other Common Names Estrofanto, Graines de Strophantus, Kombe, Kombe-Strophanthus Seeds, Strophanthi Grati Semen, Strophanthi Kombe Semen, Strophanthus Seeds, Strophantus, Yang Guo Nau
|

---

## Overview

Strophanthus seeds can be confused with other African Strophanthus species ( 18 ). Strophanthus is used as an arrow poison in Africa ( 4077 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, strophanthus is used for arteriosclerosis, cardiac insufficiency, diabetes, gastrocardial symptoms, hypertension, and neurodystonia.

Unsafe when used orally for self-medication ( 12 , 4077 ).

PREGNANCY: Unsafe when used orally due to possible uterine stimulating effects ( 19 ); avoid using.

LACTATION: Unsafe when used orally; avoid using ( 4077 ).

|

#### General

Orally, side effects include nausea, vomiting, headache, stupor, disturbance of color vision, and cardiac arrhythmias, particularly during overdose ( 18 ).

#### Cardiovascular

Orally, side effects include cardiac arrhythmias ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, side effects include nausea and vomiting ( 18 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, side effects include headache, stupor, and disturbance of color vision ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of strophanthus.

#### Adult

No typical dosage.

#### Standardization & Formulation

There is insufficient reliable information available about the standardization of strophanthus.

#### CORTICOSTEROIDS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Use of corticosteroids may cause electrolyte depletion and increase the risk of strophanthus toxicity ( 19 ).

---

#### DIGOXIN (Lanoxin)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Contraindicated; therapeutic duplication increases risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity ( 2 ).

---

#### DIURETIC DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, concomitant use of potassium-depleting diuretics and strophanthus  can increase the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity due to potassium depletion ( 19 ). Some diuretics that can deplete potassium include chlorothiazide (Diuril), chlorthalidone (Thalitone), furosemide (Lasix), hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ, Hydrodiuril, Microzide), and others.

---

#### QUINIDINE
Interaction Rating Major Do not take this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Contraindicated; cardiac medications, including quinidine, may increase the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity;  ( 18 ).

---

#### QUININE
Interaction Rating Major Do not take this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Taking quinine with strophanthus may increase the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity ( 18 ).

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVES
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Overuse or misuse of laxatives may cause electrolyte depletion and increase risk of strophanthus toxicity ( 19 ).

|

#### CALCIUM SUPPLEMENTS

Calcium supplements may enhance the effects of strophanthus and increase cardiac glycoside toxicity ( 18 ).

---

#### CARDIAC GLYCOSIDE-CONTAINING HERBS

Contraindicated, and concomitant use can increase the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity. Cardiac glycoside-containing herbs include black hellebore, Canadian hemp roots, digitalis leaf, hedge mustard, figwort, lily of the valley roots, motherwort, oleander leaf, pheasant's eye plant, pleurisy root, squill bulb leaf scales, and uzara ( 19 ).

---

#### CINCHONA

Use with strophanthus seed may increase risk of toxicity ( 19 ).

---

#### EPHEDRA (Ma huang)

Use with strophanthus seed may increase risk of toxicity ( 19 ).

---

#### HORSETAIL

Theoretically, concomitant use of strophanthus with horsetail increases the risk of potassium depletion.

---

#### LICORICE

Theoretically, concomitant use of strophanthus with licorice increases the risk of potassium depletion.

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVE HERBS

Due to electrolyte loss, stimulant laxative herbs can worsen strophanthus seed toxicity, particularly with long-term use. Some of these herbs include aloe, alder buckthorn, black root, blue flag, butternut bark, colocynth, European buckthorn, fo ti, gamboge, gossypol, greater bindweed, jalap, manna, Mexican scammony root, rhubarb, senna, and yellow dock ( 19 ).

#### CARDIAC CONDITIONS

CAUTION, strophanthus seed may cause arrhythmias ( 18 ).

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of strophanthus.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of strophanthus.

#### General

The applicable part of strophanthus is the seed ( 18 , 4077 ).

#### Anti-diabetes effects

Animal research in diabetic rat models shows that taking aqueous strophanthus root extract orally for 28 days reduces blood glucose and lipid levels when compared to control. Theoretically, strophanthus lowers blood glucose by inhibiting alpha-amylase and alpha-glucosidase ( 103091 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

Strophanthus gratus contains a cardiac glycoside called strophanthin-G (ouabain), which has mild digitalis-like effects. The cardiac glycoside in Strophanthus kombe, strophanthin-K (strophoside), has milder effects than strophanthin-G ( 18 , 4077 ).
