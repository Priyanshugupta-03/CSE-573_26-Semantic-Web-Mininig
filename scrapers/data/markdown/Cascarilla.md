## Overview

Cascarilla is a plant. It is commonly used as a bitter flavor and fragrance, especially in liqueurs. It was traditionally used in folk medicine ( 97870 ).

## History

In the past, cascarilla was added to tobacco before smoking due to its pleasant odor when burned ( 215 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, cascarilla is used for digestive disorders, diarrhea, and vomiting.

#### General

The applicable part of cascarilla is the bark ( 18 ). Constituents include clerodane diterpenoids, known as cascarilla clerodanes, and a halimane diterpenoid. Other constituents include prenylisabolane and eudesmane sesquiterpene ( 97867 , 97868 , 97869 , 97870 , 97871 ).

#### Gastrointestinal effects

Cascarilla is traditionally used for digestive disorders. Laboratory research suggests that cascarilla extract increases gastric acid secretion from the stomach ( 97870 ).
