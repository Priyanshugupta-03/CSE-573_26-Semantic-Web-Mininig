## Overview

Detoxification, also known as "detox" dieting, is a dieting strategy purported to remove toxins from the body. Detox diets are promoted as methods to improve health and lose weight and involve fasting or food modification. Many detox diets also employ laxatives, diuretics, vitamins, minerals, and certain "cleansing" foods to facilitate toxin elimination. Although the detox industry continues to grow, there is a lack of clinical evidence to support the use of these diets ( 93688 ).

## People Use This For

In addition to general health and wellness, detoxification is used for weight loss, gastrointestinal disorders, fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue syndrome, inflammatory diseases, poisoning, and autoimmune conditions. Orally, a variety of natural medicines are taken including herbs, vitamins, minerals, and specific foods in an effort to detoxify the body. Rectally, various fluids are administered, such as coffee or coffee grounds, to remove toxins from the colon.

Possibly Safe when a specific detoxification plan, the Wellnessup detoxification diet, is used for up to 4 weeks  This diet, consisting of organic fruits, vegetables, nuts, and whole grains, has been used with apparent safety ( 104877 ).

Possibly Unsafe when coffee enemas are used rectally for detoxification. Coffee enemas have been linked to cases of severe electrolyte abnormalities and septicemia leading to severe side effects including death ( 3026 , 3347 , 3349 , 6652 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of other methods of detoxification.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information; avoid using.

#### General

The safety and adverse effects of detoxification depend on the method and substances used. Most information available on potential adverse effects is based only on anecdotal reports. Orally, detoxification regimens that involve high fiber intake can cause constipation. Regimens that involve the use of laxatives can cause diarrhea. Detoxification that reduces protein intake can cause mood changes, fatigue, tiredness, and other symptoms. Regimens that involve long-term fasting can cause vitamin and mineral deficiencies, protein deficiency, lactic acidosis, and death. Also, regimens that involve implementing extreme stress on the body can lead to electrolyte imbalances ( 93688 , 99478 ). Conversely, no adverse effects have been reported with the Wellnessup detoxification diet, which consists of organic fruits, vegetables, nuts, and whole grains. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted ( 104877 ). Rectally, coffee enemas used for detoxification have been linked to at least three deaths. Two of these deaths are related to severe electrolyte imbalance, and a third is associated with polymicrobial septicemia following use of coffee enema ( 3026 , 3347 , 3349 , 6652 ). Coffee enemas have also been reported to cause proctocolitis ( 96868 , 103273 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Detoxification that involves significantly altering the types of foods consumed can result in several potential side effects. Diets that eliminate caffeine may result in diarrhea, which can lead to fluid and electrolyte loss and dehydration. High fiber diets sometimes result in constipation. Detoxification programs often include laxatives. These can cause significant diarrhea leading to fluid loss and electrolyte imbalances. At least 5 cases of proctocolitis related to the use of coffee enemas have been reported ( 96868 , 103273 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Detoxification that eliminates caffeine can result in caffeine withdrawal headaches. Detoxification that eliminates or reduces protein intake by eliminating meat can result in mood changes, fatigue, tiredness, and a variety of other symptoms. In one case, serotonin syndrome was reported in a 19-year-old man attempting detoxification with tryptophan and St. John's wort after using MDMA (ecstasy) ( 93688 ).

#### Renal

Certain types of detoxification practices can cause electrolyte imbalances. For example, there was a case of severe hyponatremia after a sauna sweating "purification" practice from the Hubbard Purification Rundown ( 99478 ).

#### Other

Some methods of fasting can be safe short-term; however, long-term or extreme fasting can result in vitamin and mineral deficiencies, protein deficiency, lactic acidosis, and death ( 93688 ). Rectally administered coffee enemas used for detoxification have been linked to at least three deaths. Two of these deaths were related to severe electrolyte imbalance, and a third was associated with polymicrobial septicemia following use of coffee enema ( 3026 , 3347 , 3349 , 6652 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Obesity.

Preliminary clinical research in healthy normal weight and overweight females suggests that adhering to a Wellnessup detoxification diet (1225 kcal/day) consisting of organic fruits, vegetables, nuts, and whole grains for 4 weeks reduces weight by about 1.5 kg when compared with a regular diet. However, the weight loss with the Wellnessup detoxification diet was less than the weight loss with a calorie-restricted diet (1215 kcal/day) ( 104877 ). The effects of other detoxification diets on weight loss are unclear.

More evidence is needed to rate the effectiveness of detoxification for this use.

Detox diets and protocols are popular strategies that claim to eliminate toxins; some also claim to increase weight loss. Specific detox diets include the master cleanser/lemon detox diet, the liver cleansing diet, Martha's vineyard Detox Diet, the Clean Cleanse, Dr. Oz's 48-h weekend cleanse, BluePrintCleanse, Fat Flush, the Hubbard Purification Rundown, and others. These diets can range from a couple of days to weeks in duration and usually involve elimination of certain foods, use of laxatives, and increased consumption of specific liquids and supplements ( 93688 , 99481 ). For example, a specific type of commercial detoxification protocol and diet is the Hubbard Purification Rundown. This regimen involves daily exercise, followed by 2.5 hours of sweating in a 140-180℉ sauna with short breaks to hydrate. This regimen includes supplementation with increasing doses of crystalline niacin (nicotinic acid) and also specific vitamins, minerals, electrolytes, and polyunsaturated oils such as soy, walnut, peanut, and safflower oil ( 99480 ). Certain detoxification diets involve a specific commercial product. For example, UltraClear is a specific type of commercial detox product that is sometimes called a medical food. There are many iterations of this supplement. One example contains 440 calories, 8 grams of fiber, 30 grams of protein, 8 grams of fat, and many vitamins and minerals per serving ( 99479 ). Some regimens involve slowly increasing the dose of the supplement from half a scoop twice daily to 2 scoops twice daily over a period of 4 weeks ( 99479 , 99483 ). Some detoxification diets focus on the use of organic food products. The Wellnessup detoxification diet, which has been referred to as a nutritionally balanced detoxification diet, permits the consumption of vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and nuts for a total daily intake of 1225 calories, 27 grams of fiber, 39 grams of protein, and 48 grams of fat ( 104877 ).

#### ANEMIA

People with iron or vitamin deficiency anemia should avoid detoxification programs that restrict consumption of food sources that provide these nutrients. Dietary restriction of these nutrients could exacerbate anemia.

#### CRITICAL ILLNESS

Patients with serious illness such as cancer should avoid detoxification programs that restrict consumption of adequate amounts of food or restrict certain groups of food. Restricting nutrients from important food groups could exacerbate illness.

Proponents of detoxification theorize that the body accumulates harmful toxins over time. These toxins include metals, pesticides, and other harmful chemicals. They suggest that special diets or special treatments including herbals or supplements administered orally or rectally are needed to remove these toxins from the body ( 93688 ). Some "detox diets" focus on ingesting foods with a minimal amount of chemical contamination, such as organic foods. These diets often eliminate caffeine and alcohol consumption. They also often emphasize foods with a high nutrient content as well as foods with a high fiber content, which is theorized to facilitate removal of toxic substances ( 93688 ). Some also believe that the body experiences substantial stress through the normal digestion process. Therefore, water or juice fasting is used to rest the digestive system by avoiding solid foods, resulting in reduced bodily stress and fatigue, improved healing, and better general health. There is no scientifically reliable information to support these theories. There is no reliable evidence that the body is fatigued by normal digestion or that the body retains numerous toxins that are dangerous to health. The kidney and liver are efficient filters, processors, and eliminators of toxins ( 93688 ). Some people report feeling better after implementing these approaches; however, this is based only on anecdotal reports ( 93688 ). The Hubbard Purification Rundown is a specific type of detoxification protocol developed by Mr. Hubbard from the Church of Scientology and has been described in several preliminary clinical publications. It is theorized to eliminate fat-stored toxins by inducing sweating via sauna. Crystalline niacin (nicotinic acid) is also used to theoretically reduce toxins such as polychlorobiphenyls (PCBs), polybrominated biphenyls (PBBs), dioxins, drugs, and pesticides.  Preliminary research suggests that it might help improve cognitive function and physical symptoms of toxicity. However, studies have not evaluated whether the symptomatic improvements might instead be attributed to the passage of time and the natural clearance of toxins via the liver and kidneys. Also, the amount of toxins in the blood and fat is not consistently reduced before and after this Hubbard Purification Rundown detoxification regimen. Overall, this research suffers from poor methodology, small sample sizes, and inadequate control groups ( 99480 , 99482 , 99484 , 99485 , 99486 ). While detoxification programs or diets have not been evaluated in high-quality clinical research, some animal and preliminary clinical evidence suggests that certain dietary supplements or ingredients might aid in the elimination of heavy metals, organic pollutants, or other toxins. Malic acid, citric acid, succinic acid, pectin, coriander, selenium, and chlorella may have a role in heavy metal elimination, while chlorella, nori, and olestra may remove persistent organic pollutants and other chemicals ( 93688 , 99481 ). A small clinical study suggests that adhering to the Wellnessup diet, which consists of organic fruits, vegetables, nuts, and whole grains, for 4 weeks may reduce the presence of heavy metals, such as gallium, in the hair when compared with a standard calorie-restricted diet ( 104877 ).
