## Overview

Alpha hydroxy acids are natural acids found in foods, including, but not limited to, citrus fruits, sugarcane, sour milk, apples, and grapes. Some alpha hydroxy acids are commonly used in cosmetic products. Others are used orally, intravaginally, or by inhalation ( 3262 , 6064 , 10104 , 95601 , 108165 , 108166 , 108169 , 108172 ). Refer to this list of Alpha Hydroxy Acids to find more information on specific compounds.

This topic discusses alpha hydroxy acids as a class. Always keep in mind that the evidence varies for different alpha hydroxy acids. Some alpha hydroxy acids may be beneficial for some indications, while others have not been studied or may not be beneficial. For detailed information, refer to specific topics .

#### General

Alpha hydroxy acids are a group of natural acids found in foods including citric acid (apple cider vinegar, citrus fruits), glycolic acid (sugarcane), lactic acid (sour milk and tomato juice), malic acid (apples), tartaric acid (grapes, tamarind, wine), and others ( 6064 , 10104 , 13183 , 15170 , 15214 , 92217 , 108163 , 108164 , 108167 , 108168 ).

#### Dermatological effects

Hyperkeratinization is thought to contribute to acne, dry skin, and photo-aging. Some alpha hydroxy acids work by exfoliating the top layers of dead skin cells ( 948 , 950 , 6064 , 106612 ). Alpha hydroxy acids seem to improve the appearance of aging skin by increasing smoothness and reducing dark spots ( 6064 ). The degree of exfoliation is determined by the type, concentration, and pH of the alpha hydroxy acid, as well as the other ingredients in the product. The lower the pH of the alpha hydroxy acid, the more rapidly it is absorbed into the skin ( 106612 ). Alpha hydroxy acids that are small in molecular size per volume are more active and penetrate the skin more deeply than larger alpha hydroxy acids. Glycolic acid is smallest in molecular size followed by lactic, pyruvic, malic, tartaric, and citric acid ( 10102 ). The exfoliating action of alpha hydroxy acids reduces the thickness of the stratum corneum by diminishing corneocyte cohesion at the lower, newly forming levels, but not the mature upper levels, of the stratum corneum. This improves the flexibility of the stratum corneum and the skin surface. The exact mechanism of this effect is unknown. However, some researchers think alpha hydroxy acids interfere with the function of the enzymes involved in the formation of ionic sulfate and phosphate bonds in the stratum corneum ( 10104 ). Alpha hydroxy acids have been studied in combination with other therapies. A meta-analysis of 18 clinical studies in patients with acne shows that using alpha hydroxy acids along with intense pulsed light therapy is more effective than either therapy alone ( 111751 ).

#### Other effects

Not all alpha hydroxy acids are of interest for skin conditions. For example, tartaric acid is of interest especially for gastrointestinal and respiratory health ( 108165 , 108166 , 108167 ), whereas malic acid is of interest for the production of saliva and blood pressure control ( 26970 , 95565 ). More information is needed related to their mechanism of action. See specific monographs for detailed information.
