## Overview

The cabbage soup diet is also referred to as the soup diet. This calorie-restrictive diet follows a 7-day meal plan with the goal of losing 10 pounds in a single week. The diet is low in fat, high in fiber, and is marketed as a way to jumpstart a more sustainable eating program ( 102102 ).

## People Use This For

Obesity, weight loss.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the cabbage soup diet. However, the design of the cabbage soup diet might result in insufficient daily intake of essential vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients ( 102101 ).

#### General

The design of the cabbage soup diet might result in inadequate intake of essential vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients ( 102101 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the cabbage soup diet.

The cabbage soup diet is a calorie-restrictive diet that permits an unlimited intake of cabbage soup. Although there are a number of variations on this diet, most recipes include the addition of onions, tomatoes, celery, and carrots to the cabbage soup. The diet typically provides much less than 1000 calories per day on days 1-3, and just over 1000 calories per day on days 4-7 ( 102102 ). This diet follows a 7-day meal plan that allows the consumption of specific foods to maximize weight loss. Day 1 of the diet usually consists of cabbage soup plus unlimited raw fruit, excluding bananas. Day 2 includes cabbage soup and unlimited raw or cooked vegetables, excluding potatoes. Day 3 allows cabbage soup plus unlimited raw fruit and vegetables, excluding potatoes and bananas. Day 4 includes cabbage soup plus up to eight bananas and as much skim milk as desired. Day 5 allows for cabbage soup plus up to 20 ounces of beef and up to 6 tomatoes. Day 6 consists of cabbage soup plus unlimited beef and raw vegetables, excluding potatoes. Finally, day 7 allows for cabbage soup, brown rice, and unsweetened fruit juice ( 102102 ).

Any weight loss seen with the cabbage soup diet is likely due to severe calorie restriction. However, this calorie restriction is only maintained for one week, and the diet is not intended to result in long-term weight loss or weight maintenance ( 102102 ). White cabbage, which contains lutein, zeaxanthin, kaempferol, and quercetin, can reduce inflammation and improve insulin resistance in vitro. The cabbage soup diet employs the theory that these effects might enhance weight loss ( 102102 ). However, the evidence for the use of cabbage for weight reduction is limited.
