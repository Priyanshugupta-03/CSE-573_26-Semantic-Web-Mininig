## Overview

Iridology is the study of the iris to diagnose disease or medical conditions ( 15790 ). Iridologists conduct an examination using only a magnifying glass and iridology diagnosis map ( 15785 ).

## History

Iridology was originally invented by Philippi Meyers in 1670. However, Ignatz von Peczely, a physician from Hungary, is considered the modern founder of iridology ( 15788 ). He published a text on iridology diagnosis in the 1880s ( 15787 ). Bernard Jensen, DC (1908-2001) was considered to be the leading American iridologist. He claimed that iridology diagnosis can reveal nutritional needs. He also developed an "iridology nutrition chart" to determine which vitamin, mineral, or herbal supplements are needed for specific iridology diagnoses ( 15786 ).

## People Use This For

Iridology is used to diagnose disease. It is not used to treat disease.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of iridology; however, since iridology does not involve an invasive procedure or ingestion of any substance, it is not expected to cause adverse effects. Since iridology is not proven to be an accurate method of diagnosis, iridology might not reliably detect disease. Therefore, a potential source of harm from iridology is undetected disease or an inaccurate diagnosis.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, since iridology is not proven to provide an accurate diagnosis, a potential risk to using iridology is incorrect diagnosis or missed diagnosis.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Cancer diagnosis.

Preliminary research shows that iridology practitioners do not consistently or accurately diagnose various cancers including breast, ovary, uterus, prostate, or colorectal cancer ( 15788 , 88950 ).

Disease diagnosis.

Preliminary observational research shows that radii solaris and intestinal crypt lesions were common in adults with hemiplegia. However, this study only included subjects without hemiplegia and could not determine the ability of these lesions to predict diagnosis ( 112198 ).

Gallbladder disease diagnosis.

Preliminary research shows that iridology practitioners do not consistently or accurately diagnose gallbladder disease ( 15787 ). According to iridologists, gallbladder disease is a condition easily identified.

Hypertension diagnosis.

Preliminary research suggests that patients with hypertension are more likely to have a "neurogenic" constitution based on iridology diagnosis ( 15789 , 88949 ). This suggests that iridology diagnosis of neurogenic constitution is associated with an increased risk of hypertension.

Kidney disease diagnosis.

Preliminary research suggests that practitioners do not consistently or accurately diagnose kidney disease ( 88951 ).

More evidence is needed to rate iridology for these uses.

None known.

Iridology is the study of the iris to diagnose disease or medical conditions ( 15790 ). The iris is the colored part of the eye. It regulates how much light enters by opening and closing the pupil. Some non-ocular conditions can be diagnosed by examining the eye; however, iridology suggests that each bodily organ is linked to a particular section of the eye. Iridologists divide the eyes into 60 sections similar to the face of a clock ( 15790 ). For example, the thyroid gland would be linked to the right eye at about 2:30 and the left eye at 9:30. Abnormalities such as streaks or discoloration in this area of the eye would indicate disease in that particular organ or gland. Iridologists conduct an examination using only a magnifying glass and iridology diagnosis map. According to iridology, iris color corresponds to three primary constitutional types: 1.) Blue eyes are known as "lymphatic type" and correspond to tendencies of swollen lymph nodes, splenitis, acne, eczema, dry skin, coughs, diarrhea, arthritis, vaginal discharge, and other characteristics; 2.) Pure brown eyes are known as "hematogenic type" and correspond to tendencies of anemia, blood disease, muscle spasms, arthritis, flatulence, constipation, dyspepsia, diabetes, and other characteristics; 3.) The combination of the two are known as "mixed type" and corresponds to tendencies of colitis, flatulence, liver disorders, pancreatic disorders, and other conditions ( 15785 ).
