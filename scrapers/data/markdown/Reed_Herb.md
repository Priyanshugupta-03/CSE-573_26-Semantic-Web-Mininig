## Overview

Reed herb is a perennial grass growing in wetlands, marshes, and around lakes. The stem and rhizome have been traditionally used as medicine for digestive disorders. Reed herb has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for over 2000 years ( 115349 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of reed herb.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION : Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Breast cancer.

Although there has been interest in using oral reed herb for breast cancer, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of reed herb for this purpose.

Diabetes.

Although there has been interest in using oral reed herb for diabetes, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of reed herb for this purpose.

Insect bite.

Although there has been interest in using topical reed herb for insect bites, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of reed herb for this purpose.

Leukemia.

Although there has been interest in using oral reed herb for leukemia, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of reed herb for this purpose.

Respiratory tract infections.

Oral reed herb has only been evaluated in combination with other ingredients; its effect when used alone is unclear.

Meta-analyses of small clinical trials in patients with respiratory tract infections show that taking mixtures of Chinese herbs including reed herb root modestly increases the proportion of patients with total or almost total elimination of symptoms and modestly reduces fever clearance times when compared with placebo or usual care ( 115349 ). The validity of this meta-analysis is limited by low methodological quality, high risk of bias, and heterogeneity of herbal mixtures and types of respiratory tract infections studied.

More evidence is needed to rate reed herb for these uses.

#### General

The applicable parts of reed herb are the stem and rhizome. Reed herb contains vitamin A, vitamin C, and several vitamins of the B-group, along with polysaccharides and amino acids. It also contains p-coumaric acid, sterols, polyphenols, triterpenes (beta-amyrin and taraxerol), and several flavonoids including chrysoeriol, isoquercitrin, luteolin, rutin, and tricin ( 18 , 115349 ).

#### Diuretic effects

Reed herb is thought to have diuretic effects and to stimulate sweating, but these effects have not been well studied ( 18 ).
