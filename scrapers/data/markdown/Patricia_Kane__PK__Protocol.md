## Overview

The Patricia Kane protocol, often called the PK protocol, is a three-part program marketed for the treatment of neurologic disorders, such as amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), Parkinson disease, Alzheimer disease, multiple sclerosis, autism, chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS), and fibromyalgia. The protocol involves intravenous and oral supplementation with phosphatidylcholine, leucovorin, and glutathione, as well as a special nutrient-dense diet that involves the use of certain oils and supplements ( 102935 ). Patricia Kane specializes in researching metabolic disorders and lipid disturbances. There is a lack of information about the area in which she earned her PhD.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the PK protocol. This protocol uses high doses of phosphatidylcholine and glutathione. See Phosphatidylcholine and Glutathione for more information.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted. Orally and intravenously, phosphatidylcholine and glutathione may cause adverse effects. See Phosphatidylcholine and Glutathione for more information.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the PK protocol.

Patricia Kane, PhD, has not made the details of her protocol available to the general public. Her staff states that it is only provided to approved medical doctors. According to the information that has been made available, the protocol appears to be a three-part program. Part one is an organic diet that includes fresh vegetables, berries, and natural meats. Part two is an oral supplement program that consists of certain branded (BodyBio) oils and supplements. Part three includes intravenous or oral phosphatidylcholine, leucovorin, and glutathione. Individualized treatments are based on red blood cell fatty acid levels and various other undisclosed tests ( 102935 ).

None known.

None known.

Phosphatidylcholine and glutathione may interact with certain conditions. See Phosphatidylcholine and Glutathione for more information.

Phosphatidylcholine and glutathione may interact with certain lab tests. See Phosphatidylcholine and Glutathione for more information.

The PK Protocol is intended to restore the function of nerve cells. The primary focus of the protocol is on nutritional intervention and supplementation with phosphatidylcholine. Patricia Kane believes that phosphatidylcholine is the most crucial component of cells and cell membranes and is vital to recovery from severe neurologic illness ( 102935 ). However, the evidence for the use of phosphatidylcholine for neurological disorders is limited. Refer to Phosphatidylcholine for more information.
