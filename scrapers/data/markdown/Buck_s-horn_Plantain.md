## Overview

Buck's-horn plantain is an herb found in Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. The plant is consumed as food. In addition, various parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine ( 26150 , 103471 , 103472 , 103473 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, buck's-horn plantain is traditionally used to treat kidney disease and urinary disorders. It is also used for pain, inflammation, fever, cancer, and respiratory problems. Decoctions or boiled leaves of buck's-horn plantain are also used as a laxative. In foods, the leaves and herb are consumed cooked or raw.

#### General

The applicable parts of buck's-horn plantain are the leaves and above-ground parts of the plant. Constituents include phenolic acids, flavonoids, coumarins, tannins, lignans, glycosides, and triterpenes ( 103471 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

The constituent phenolic acids and polyphenols in buck's-horn plantain are thought to be responsible for antioxidant effects shown in vitro. It is possible that these properties play a role in its clinical effects ( 103471 ).
