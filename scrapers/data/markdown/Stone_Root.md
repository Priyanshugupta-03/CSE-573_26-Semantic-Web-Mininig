Caution

Do not confuse stone root, which has also been called citronella, with Citronella Oil .

## Overview

Stone root is an aromatic perennial herb with a strong, unpleasant smell. The root and rhizome have traditionally been used as numbing agents, for kidney stones and gastrointestinal disorders, and for purported diuretic and diaphoretic effects ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of stone root.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

|

#### General

There is limited reliable information available about the adverse effects of stone root.

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, large amounts of stone root can cause intestinal tract irritation and colic-like pain dizziness, and nausea ( 18 ).

#### Genitourinary

Orally, large amounts of stone root can cause painful urination ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of stone root.

#### Adult
Oral:

Traditionally, dried stone root has been taken as 1-4 grams three times daily. It has also been taken as a tea three times daily, prepared by simmering 1-4 grams of root or rhizome in 150 mL of boiling water for 5-10 minutes and then straining.

#### General

The applicable parts of stone root are the rhizome and root. The rhizome and root contain a volatile oil, caffeic acid derivatives, saponins, tannin, mucilage, and resin ( 4 , 18 ). Constituents of the volatile oil include caryophyllene, germacrene D, limonene, and alpha- and beta-pinenes ( 18 ).
