## Overview

Logwood is a tree with twisted, thorny branches and rough, dark bark. It grows up to 10-12 meters in height and is native to tropical regions of the United States ( 18 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, logwood is used for diarrhea. Topically, it is used as an astringent to control minor bleeding.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of logwood.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

In animals, ingestion of hematoxylin, a constituent of logwood, can cause elevated body temperature, vomiting, anuria, coma, and death ( 18 ).

#### General

The applicable part of the plant is the heartwood, which is red in color. The color is due to the quinoide hematein, formed by oxidation from homoisoflavanes. The heartwood also contains hematoxylin and tannins ( 18 ).
