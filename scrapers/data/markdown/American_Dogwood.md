## Overview

American dogwood is a tree. The bark is sometimes prepared in medicine; however, its use is rare ( 18 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, American dogwood is used for headaches and fatigue. It is also used orally to increase strength, for fever, chronic diarrhea, and to stimulate appetite. It is also used orally as a tonic. Topically, American dogwood is used as an astringent for boils and wounds. Historically, American dogwood was used orally as a substitute for quinine.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of American dogwood.

#### General

The applicable part of American dogwood is the bark ( 18 ).

#### Antiparasitic effects

Animal data suggests the water-insoluble fraction of American dogwood has antimalarial effects comparable to quinine and sulfadiazine ( 18 ).  Also, American dogwood destroys the snails that carry the tropical human parasite species Schistosoma ( 18 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

A methanol extract of American dogwood has a dose-dependent inhibiting effect on heart activity, and stops the heartbeat at high doses ( 18 ).
