## Overview

Procaine is a chemical. It was first synthesized to be used as a local anesthetic. However, it became popular as an unproven anti-aging agent. Although banned from importation by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), procaine is available online or over-the-counter outside of the U.S. ( 99208 ).

## History

Procaine was first synthesized in 1905 by Alfred Einhorn, a German chemist. In the 1950's, procaine became popular as an anti-aging product. Products available online contain procaine and other ingredients including potassium metabisulphite, benzoic acid, and sodium phosphate, vitamins, minerals, and enzymes, hematoporpyrine, plant extracts and bee products such as propolis and royal jelly ( 99208 ).

## People Use This For

Orally or parenterally, procaine is used for age-related cognitive decline, quality of life, anxiety, arthritis, cerebral atherosclerosis, dementia, depression, hair loss, hypertension, sexual dysfunction, and an overall rejuvenating effect. Parenterally, procaine is used for migraine and asthma. Prescription-only procaine is used for local anesthesia.

Likely Safe when the prescription-only product is used parenterally as a local anesthetic ( 15 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the oral use of procaine.

PREGNANCY: Likely Unsafe when used for self-medication; avoid using ( 6 ).

LACTATION: There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of procaine for self-medication; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, procaine can cause heartburn, migraines, and systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) ( 6 , 99208 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, procaine can cause heartburn ( 6 ).

#### Immunologic

Orally, procaine can cause systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) ( 6 , 99208 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, procaine can cause migraines ( 6 , 99208 ).

Effective

Local anesthesia . Procaine injection is an FDA-approved prescription product used as a local anesthetic ( 15 ).

---

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Age-related cognitive decline.

Preliminary clinical research shows that oral or intramuscular procaine may improve some, but not most, measures of cognitive function in individuals described as mentally healthy elderly. However, withdrawals due to adverse effects were higher in those using procaine, and most studies failed to adequately demonstrate that the included patients were actually healthy ( 99208 ).

Dementia.

Preliminary clinical research involving 12 patients with dementia shows that procaine does not improve cognitive function compared to placebo ( 99208 ). It is possible that the study was underpowered.

More evidence is needed to rate procaine for these uses.

#### AMINOSALICYLIC ACID
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

A metabolite of procaine, aminobenzoic acid, can antagonize the effect of aminosalicylic acid. Theoretically, procaine might also reduce aminosalicylic activity ( 15 ).

#### SULFONAMIDE ANTIBIOTICS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

A metabolite of procaine, aminobenzoic acid, can antagonize the effect of sulfonamides. Theoretically, procaine might also reduce sulfonamide activity ( 15 ). Some sulfonamides include sulfamethoxazole (Gantanol), sulfasalazine (Azulfidine), sulfisoxazole (Gantrisin), and trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole (Bactrim, Septra).

#### CARDIAC GLYCOSIDE-CONTAINING HERBS

Intravenous use of  procaine hydrochloride is contraindicated with concurrent use of digitalis ( 15 ).

|

#### MYASTHENIA GRAVIS

The intravenous use of procaine is contraindicated in individuals with myasthenia gravis, which is progressive muscular weakness ( 15 ).

#### PSEUDOCHOLINESTERASE DEFICIENCY

Contraindicated ( 6 ).

#### SYSTEMIC LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSUS (SLE)

Contraindicated. Theoretically, procaine might exacerbate this condition ( 6 ).

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of procaine.

#### Absorption

Procaine has poor oral absorption, and there is no evidence that pharmacologic levels are achieved with oral administration ( 6 ). Hematoporphyrin is added to some oral preparations to increase procaine absorption ( 6 ), although no evidence supports this effect. .

#### General

Procaine is a synthetic chemical ( 6 ). The mechanism of action of oral procaine is unknown as it is poorly absorbed ( 6 ).
