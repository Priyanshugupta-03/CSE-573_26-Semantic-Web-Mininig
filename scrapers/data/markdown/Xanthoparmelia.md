## Overview

Products containing xanthoparmelia are often marketed for sexual enhancement ( 14285 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used orally. Scabrosin esters contained in xanthoparmelia are considered toxic ( 14285 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Possibly Unsafe when used orally. Scabrosin esters contained in xanthoparmelia are considered toxic ( 14285 ).

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, scabrosin esters contained in xanthoparmelia are considered toxic.

#### General

Xanthoparmelia is a lichen. The active constituents are scabrosin esters, which belong to the class of epipolythiodioxopiperazines ( 14284 ).

#### Antineoplastic effects

Several scabrosins, constituents of xanthoparmelia, seem to have in vitro cytotoxic activity against animal mastocytonia and human breast cancer cell liens ( 14284 ). However, these compounds also seem to be toxic to numerous other cells by inducing apoptosis ( 14285 ).
