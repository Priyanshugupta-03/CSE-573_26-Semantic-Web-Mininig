## Overview

Curanderismo is a system of medicine that originated in Latin America. It is based on the theory that illnesses have both natural and supernatural sources. Alleged supernatural sources may include evil spirits and brujos (witches). The word curanderismo is derived from the Spanish verb curar, which means "to cure" or "to heal" ( 102309 ). Folk healers in this tradition are called curanderos. These individuals learn how to practice curanderismo from close relatives or from apprenticeships with experienced healers. Curanderos can have many purported specialties, including botanical remedies, massage, midwifery, and psychic healing ( 102309 ). This traditional healing system blends Native American, Aztec, Hispanic, and spiritual traditions with modern medicine. It involves herbs, massage, dietary modifications, prayer, amulets, spiritual medicine, and psychic healing. Curanderismo is commonly used in Hispanic-American communities in the southwestern United States, as well as many Latin American countries ( 102309 ).

Curanderismo is a system of medicine that originated in Latin America. It is based on the theory that illness and disease has both natural and supernatural causes. Although training is considered to be an important form of preparation for practitioners of curanderismo (curanderos), many curanderos claim that their ability to heal involves divine energy being channeled through their bodies. Thus, spiritual and psychic healing through prayer and religious faith act as a strong foundation of curanderismo ( 102308 , 102309 ). Curanderos act on the foundational belief that the mind and body are inseparable, as are the body and soul ( 102308 ). Practitioners of curanderismo believe good health is achieved by maintaining certain balances, such as a balance of hot and cold, or of the spiritual and physical ( 102308 ). This balance can be disrupted in many ways, including through physical or emotional fatigue, an imbalanced relationship, or an abrupt change in temperature due to an action taken, such as going outside with wet hair. Cooling activities include menstruation, childbirth, surgical operations, and exposure to cold air and substances. Heating activities include digestion, pregnancy, and exposure to heat ( 102309 ). Treatment involves physical, spiritual, and mental approaches. While some aspects of curanderismo may be practiced at home, such as folk remedies for minor illness, many people pursue the help of curanderos. Treatment selection is based on the perceived balance between hot and cold, or spiritual and physical. Herbs, emetics, and diuretics may be used to balance the body. Certain foods and medicines are considered "hot" and would be used to correct an excess of "cold", and vice versa. Supporters also claim folk illnesses such as mal de ojo (the evil eye), susto (fright), and empacho (blockage of the digestive tract) can be treated by curanderismo. To treat these illnesses, the curandero may perform barridas (ritual cleansing) to rebalance the body and soul of the patient ( 102309 ). A traditional healing session may include any combination of spiritual cleansing, ritual, prayer, massage, and herbal therapy. Curanderos may use herbs and spices, eggs, lemons, flowers, fruits, holy water, pictures of saints, crucifixes, candles, incense, and oils in their healing sessions. Each object has a specific purpose. To relieve the physical and spiritual manifestations of illness, curanderos may use a number of special tools and treatments. For physical illnesses, herbal mixtures, poultices, or teas are often recommended. The treatments administered by curanderos vary widely depending upon the nature of the illness or complaint ( 102308 , 102309 ).
