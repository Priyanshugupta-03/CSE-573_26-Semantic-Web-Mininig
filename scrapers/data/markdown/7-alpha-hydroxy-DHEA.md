Caution

7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA should not be confused with 7-beta-hydroxy-DHEA , 7-keto-DHEA , or DHEA .

## Overview

7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA is a metabolite of dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA) that occurs naturally in the body ( 13639 ).

7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA is considered a prohibited substance by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) ( 102469 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### General

7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA is a metabolite of dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA), which occurs naturally in the body ( 13639 ).

#### Anti-aging effects

Observational research has found that plasma levels of 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA decline with age ( 13638 ). However, it is unclear if supplementation with 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA can improve signs and symptoms of aging.

#### Antioxidant effects

Animal research shows that 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA has antioxidant effects ( 13641 ).

#### Breast cancer effects

Observational research has found that 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA levels increase in patients with advanced breast cancer ( 13642 ). The mechanism by which 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA might be involved in the pathogenesis of breast cancer is unclear.

#### Cognitive effects

Observational research has found that 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA levels increase in patients with Alzheimer disease ( 13643 , 13644 ). The mechanism by which 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA might be involved in the pathogenesis of Alzheimer disease is unclear. Some researchers suggest 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA levels might be useful to differentiate early Alzheimer disease from vascular dementia ( 13644 ).

#### Hormonal effects

Preliminary research suggests steroids such as dexamethasone cause an increase in 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA, which might antagonize glucocorticoid effects ( 13639 ).

#### Immunostimulant effects

Animal and in vitro research shows that 7-alpha-hydroxy-DHEA appears to stimulate immune function ( 13640 ).
