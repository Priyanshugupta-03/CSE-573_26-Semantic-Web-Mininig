## Overview

1,4-DMAA is a chemical ( 94370 , 100692 ). It has been found in very small amounts in geranium plants ( 94370 ); however, the amount of 1,4-DMAA found in products marketed for athletic performance or weight loss is much greater than the quantity that is found in nature. This has raised concerns that manufacturers are spiking supplement products with synthetic 1,4-DMAA rather than obtaining it from natural sources ( 100692 ). In 2019, the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) advised consumers to avoid supplements containing 1,4-DMAA. It also added the chemical to its Dietary Supplement Ingredient Advisory List, noting that it appears to be unlawful to include 1,4-DMAA in dietary supplement products ( 100690 ). 1,4-DMAA is also included on the World Anti-Doping Agency's list of prohibited substances ( 100691 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, 1,4-DMAA is used for athletic performance and obesity.

Likely Unsafe when used orally. 1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Clinical evidence on the safety of 1,4-DMAA is lacking; however, a related stimulant called 1,3-DMAA has been linked to serious adverse events, including lactic acidosis, hemorrhagic stroke, heat stroke, and death ( 17599 , 17660 , 17662 , 17663 , 17904 , 94361 , 94369 , 94374 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, clinical evidence on the safety of 1,4-DMAA is lacking, although it has demonstrated stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects in animal studies ( 100692 ). A related stimulant called 1,3-DMAA has been linked to serious adverse events in humans, including lactic acidosis, hemorrhagic stroke, heat stroke, and death ( 17599 , 17660 , 17662 , 17663 , 17904 , 94361 , 94369 , 94374 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of 1,4-DMAA.

#### Adult

No typical dosage.

#### Standardization & Formulation

Several dietary supplements marketed for athletic performance or weight loss have been found to contain 1,4-DMAA, despite labels suggesting the presence of other ingredients. Two supplements said to contain 2-aminoisoheptane (from aconite) and one supplement said to contain 2-amino-6-methylheptane (from octodrine) were found to contain 1,4-DMAA instead. Based on the amount of 1,4-DMAA detected in these supplements, users taking these products at the maximum recommended amount would receive 43-94 mg of 1,4-DMAA per day ( 100692 ).

#### STIMULANT DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, taking 1,4-DMAA with stimulant drugs might increase the risk of adverse cardiovascular effects. Some stimulant drugs include amphetamine, caffeine, diethylpropion (Tenuate), methylphenidate, phentermine (Ionamin), pseudoephedrine (Sudafed, others), and many others.

#### HERBS AND SUPPLEMENTS WITH STIMULANT PROPERTIES

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, taking 1,4-DMAA with other herbs and supplements with stimulant properties might increase the risk of adverse cardiovascular effects. Some herbs and supplements with stimulant properties include ephedra, bitter orange, caffeine, and caffeine-containing supplements such as coffee, cola nut, guarana, and mate.

#### HYPERTENSION

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, 1,4-DMAA might exacerbate high blood pressure.

#### NARROW-ANGLE GLAUCOMA

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, 1,4-DMAA might exacerbate narrow-angle glaucoma.

#### SURGERY

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, 1,4-DMAA might interfere with surgical procedures by increasing heart rate and blood pressure. Tell patients to discontinue 1,4-DMAA at least 2 weeks before elective surgical procedures.

#### TACHYARRHYTHMIAS

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 100692 ). Theoretically, 1,4-DMAA might increase heart rate and worsen tachyarrhythmias.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of 1,4-DMAA.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of 1,4-DMAA.

#### General

1,4-DMAA is a chemical. It has been found in very small amounts in geranium leaves and stems, with 1,4-DMAA quantities ranging from 13-162 ng/gram of plant material ( 94370 ). Some analytical studies have also found 1,4-DMAA in jimson weed, Bacillus atrophaeus, and corn degradation products; however, the validity of these studies has been questioned ( 100692 ). Due to the high quantity of 1,4-DMAA found in supplement products, it is believed that these products contain synthetic 1,4-DMAA ( 100692 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

Animal research shows that 1,4-DMAA has vasoconstrictive effects, leading to increases in heart rate and blood pressure ( 100692 ).

#### Stimulant effects

1,4-DMAA is thought to have stimulant effects. This hasn't been demonstrated in humans, but some animal research shows that 1,4-DMAA has vasoconstrictive effects, leading to increases in heart rate and blood pressure. Also, 1,4-DMAA is structurally similar to other synthetic stimulants such as 1,3-DMAA, methylpentane, and octodrine ( 100692 ).
