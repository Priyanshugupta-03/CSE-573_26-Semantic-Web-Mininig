## Overview

Cha de Bugre is a small tree or shrub that grows to about 8-12 meters in height ( 93247 , 93248 ). It is indigenous to Brazil and can also be found in the tropical forests of Paraguay and Argentina ( 93248 , 93250 ). The tree produces a red fruit that looks similar to a coffee bean. This fruit is often roasted and brewed into a tea as a substitute for coffee ( 93248 , 93250 ).

## History

Cha de Bugre has been used in traditional Brazilian folk medicine as a diuretic and appetite suppressant ( 93250 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, Cha de Bugre is used for weight loss, obesity, and cellulite. It is also used to treat cough, edema, gout, cancer, viral infections, fever, hypertension, and cardiovascular disease. It is also used as a stimulant and diuretic. Topically, Cha de Bugre is used for wound healing.

#### General

The applicable parts of Cha de Bugre are the leaf and fruit. An extract of dried leaves contains long-chain hydrocarbons, long-chain alcohols, spathulenol, beta-sitosterol, and various fatty acids, including palmitic, myristic, stearic, oleic, and linoleic ( 93250 ). The fruit contains caffeine, potassium salts, allantoin, and allantoic acid ( 93250 , 94202 ).

#### Antiviral effects

An extract prepared from Cha de Bugre leaves and twigs has demonstrated viricidal activity against the herpes simplex virus in vitro ( 93249 ).

#### Diuretic effects

Cha de Bugre has been traditionally used as a diuretic. However, animal research does not support this use. In rats, administration of a dried leaf preparation 20 mg/kg for 13 days did not alter urine output ( 94201 ).

#### Lipidemic effects

Animal research shows that Cha de Bugre may have antilipemic effects. In healthy rats and in diabetic rats with hypertriglyceridemia, Cha de Bugre leaf extract lowered serum triglyceride levels ( 94201 ).

#### Weight loss effects

Cha de Bugre is thought to facilitate weight loss by reducing appetite ( 94201 ). However, animal research has yielded conflicting evidence. Cha de Bugre extract 300-1000 mg/kg daily for 7 days increased weight loss only in female mice receiving 600 mg/kg/day ( 93247 ). In other studies, Cha de Bugre leaf extract 20-2000 mg/kg daily for 13-15 days had no effect on food intake or body weight in any mice ( 93248 , 94201 ). In rats, Cha de Bugre whole plant extract 400 mg/kg daily for 90 days slowed the rate of weight gain, but lower doses had no effect ( 94202 ).
