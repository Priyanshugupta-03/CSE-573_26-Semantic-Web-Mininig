# Stavesacre
Scientific Name Delphinium staphisagria Family Ranunculaceae Other Common Names Albarraz, Herbe aux Goutteux, Herbe aux Poux, Raisin Sauvage, Staphisagra, Staphisaigre, Staphysagria, Staphysaigre

## Overview

Stavesacre is a flowering plant ( 18 ). The seeds have traditionally been used for hair loss, lice, scabies, and neuropathy.

Likely Unsafe when the seeds are used orally. Stavesacre seeds are considered poisonous ( 18 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of stavesacre seed when used topically.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally ( 18 ); avoid using.

|

#### General

Orally, stavesacre seed is likely unsafe. Topically, there is limited reliable information available about the adverse effects of stavesacre seed.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Gastrointestinal tract inflammation, nausea, pruritus, stool urgency, and urinary urgency. Topically : Inflammation, redness.

#### Dermatologic

Orally, stavesacre seed extract can cause pruritus ( 18 ). Topically, stavesacre seed has been reported to cause inflammation, eczema, and reddening of the skin ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, stavesacre seed extract has been reported to cause inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract ( 18 , 19 ), nausea, and stool urgency ( 18 ).

#### Genitourinary

Orally, stavesacre seed extract has been reported to cause urinary urgency ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of stavesacre.

#### Adult

Research is limited; typical dosing is unavailable.

#### Standardization & Formulation

There is insufficient reliable information available about the standardization of stavesacre.

None known.

None known.

#### GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) IRRITATION

Stavesacre can aggravate inflammation of the GI tract by irritating mucosal membranes ( 19 ).

None known.

#### Presentation

Ingesting 2 teaspoons of stavesacre seed can cause weakened pulse, stomach pain, labored breathing, and cardiovascular collapse ( 18 ).

#### Treatment

There is insufficient reliable information available about the treatment of overdose with stavesacre.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of stavesacre.

#### General

The applicable part of the plant is the seed. Stavesacre contains diterpene alkaloids including delphinine, staphisine, and staphisagroine, which are reported to have effects similar to aconitine ( 18 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the mechanisms of action of stavesacre.
