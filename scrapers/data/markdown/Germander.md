## Overview

Germander is a flowering subshrub that is native to the Mediterranean region. The above ground parts are used in medicine ( 18 , 101819 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Traditionally used for gastrointestinal issues, fever, weight loss, and oral hygiene, but there is no strong evidence to support any use.
- Likely unsafe when used orally. Germander is hepatotoxic and can be fatal.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, germander is used orally for gallbladder conditions, fever, abdominal pain, mild diarrhea, colic, hemorrhoids, as a digestive aid, an adjunct for weight loss, an antiseptic, and as "a rinse for gout." Topically, germander is used as a mouthwash for oral hygiene. In manufacturing, germander is used as a flavoring agent in alcoholic beverages.

Likely Unsafe when used orally. Dosages of 600 mg or more daily can be toxic ( 18 ). Germander is associated with multiple cases of hepatitis and death ( 271 , 3741 , 3742 , 3743 , 3744 ). France has banned its sale ( 17 ). Canada does not allow germander to be included in oral products as a non-medicinal ingredient ( 12 ). However, the US still allows germander to be used in small amounts as a flavoring agent in alcoholic beverages ( 12 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally ( 271 , 3741 , 3742 , 3743 , 3744 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of germander are the above ground parts. Germander contains teucrin A, a diterpene ( 3740 ). It also contains other teucrins, and phenylethanoid and caffeic acid glycosides ( 101819 ).

#### Hepatotoxic effects

The germander constituent teucrin A causes hepatic necrosis in mice ( 3740 ).
