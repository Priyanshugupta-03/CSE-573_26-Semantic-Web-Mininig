## Overview

Shiatsu is a massage style similar to acupressure that follows principles of traditional Chinese medicine. Pressure is applied to specific points along the meridians ( 95455 ).

## History

Tokujiro Namikoshi is largely credited with the development and systematization of shiatsu. He developed the Japan Shiatsu College in 1940. He is also famous for providing shiatsu treatments to celebrities such as Muhammad Ali and Marilyn Monroe. Modern day practitioners of shiatsu are known as "Shiatsupractors." However, official recognition and licensure standards, when they exist, are inconsistent and differ substantially depending on the state or country.

## People Use This For

Shiatsu is a Japanese form of massage that is used for conditions including muscle pain (myalgia), chronic pain, depression, Alzheimer disease, osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis (RA), musculoskeletal injuries, stimulating immune function, and other conditions.

Likely Safe when used appropriately. Shiatsu massage has been used in clinical studies without reports of serious adverse effects ( 89097 , 89098 , 89099 , 89100 , 95455 , 95464 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Possibly Safe when used appropriately. Shiatsu massage has been safely received by those who are pregnant without reports of serious adverse effects ( 89098 , 95461 ).

#### General

Shiatsu is well tolerated in most people, with no reports of serious adverse effects in clinical research ( 89097 , 89098 , 89099 , 89100 , 95455 , 95464 ). However, shiatsu massage given too vigorously or with the use of too much strength can cause damage to nerves or tissues to which it is applied, resulting in pain or loss of function. Usually the injured body part recovers with time ( 95457 , 95460 ).

#### Cardiovascular

In one report, a 56-year-old Japanese woman developed neck pain, dizziness, dysphagia, and gait disturbance during shiatsu treatment. Diagnostic tests showed right vertebral artery stenosis and cerebellar infarction likely related to shiatsu treatment ( 15784 ). In other case reports, shiatsu massage of the neck resulted in cerebral artery embolism and a jugular vein thrombosis ( 95463 , 100343 ).

Possibly Effective
Parturition.

Some clinical research shows that women using shiatsu techniques taught or administered by a midwife are more likely to go into labor spontaneously than controls not taught shiatsu techniques; however, shiatsu techniques do not seem to reduce labor pain severity ( 89098 , 95461 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Alzheimer disease.

A small clinical study in patients with Alzheimer disease and depression shows that receiving a 40-minute shiatsu massage once weekly for 10 months, in addition to participating in a physical activity program, reduces depression to a greater degree when compared with the physical activity program alone. Cognitive and physical function did not differ between groups ( 100734 ).

Back pain.

A small, uncontrolled study in patients with lower back pain shows that four, 1-hour shiatsu massage treatments over 8 weeks can reduce pain and anxiety ( 89099 ). The validity of these results is limited by the lack of a comparator group. Another small clinical study in patients with chronic lower back pain shows that receiving shiatsu massage for 1 hour once weekly along with standard care for 4 weeks, followed by standard care alone for 4 weeks, does not improve physical disability caused by lower back pain at 4 or 8 weeks when compared with standard care alone. However, improvements in some symptoms and quality of life are observed at 8 weeks ( 106561 ).

Delirium.

A small clinical study in agitated, mechanically ventilated adults shows that receiving shiatsu massage for three 5-minute periods separated by 2-minute breaks improves the Richmond Agitation-Sedation Scale score by about 1, compared with no change in patients receiving placebo touch alone ( 106562 ).

Pain.

Observational research in adults with chronic pain and sleep problems found that self-administration of hand shiatsu prior to bed does not improve sleep or pain when compared to baseline ( 95455 ).

Quality of life.

One small clinical study shows that shiatsu therapy once weekly for 5 weeks improves energy, stress, confidence, mobility, and other measures of quality of life in patients with advanced progressive disease receiving palliative care when compared to baseline ( 89100 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

Schizophrenia.

A small, low quality clinical study shows that shiatsu massage given as 8 sessions biweekly for 4 weeks can improve negative affect, psychosis, depression, and anxiety in patients with schizophrenia when compared to baseline ( 95459 ). The validity of these findings is limited by small study size and the lack of a comparator group.

More evidence is needed to rate shiatsu for these uses.

#### SURGERY

Shiatsu therapists should be extremely careful applying pressure around the area of surgery in patients with a history of cardiovascular any other surgery. In a case report, a 71-year-old male had a breakage and collapse of balloon expandable stents of the groin arteries due to a shiatsu massage applied in that area ( 95458 ).
