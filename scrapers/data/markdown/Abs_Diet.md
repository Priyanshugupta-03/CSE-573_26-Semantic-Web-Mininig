## Overview

The Abs Diet was created by Dave Zinczenko and popularized in his book of the same name. The Abs Diet involves a balanced diet that incorporates 12 different "power foods" and participation in daily exercise. The goal of the Abs Diet is to achieve lifelong weight loss. However, the immediate focus of the diet is to improve the physical appearance of the abdomen, as this is purported to promote better health ( 102006 ).

## People Use This For

The Abs Diet is used for obesity and overall health.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Abs Diet. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as all nutritional needs are being met.

#### General

None reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the Abs Diet.

The Abs Diet advocates consuming 12 types of food referred to as "power foods." These power foods comprise the acronym "Abs Diet Power 12" and include: almonds and other nuts, beans and legumes, spinach and other green vegetables, dairy, instant oatmeal, eggs, turkey and other lean meats, peanut butter, olive oil, whole-grain breads and cereals, extra protein powder, raspberries and other berries. The book recommends that two or three of these power foods be incorporated into every major meal, and at least one be incorporated into every snack ( 102006 ). This diet suggests that readers eat three meals and three snacks a day. The Abs Diet does not advocate counting calories, although the target daily caloric intake is around 1700 calories. Rather, adherents are encouraged to practice portion control, eat power foods, and avoid excessive alcohol ( 102006 ). Exercise also plays a major role in the Abs Diet regimen. The website and book series provide detailed explanations regarding workout routines that can be performed at the home or the gym and include strength training, cardiovascular exercise, and abdominal muscle exercises nearly every day of the week ( 102006 ).

The Abs Diet revolves around several power foods that are thought to promote weight loss, build muscle, regulate blood pressure, strengthen bone, prevent heart disease, improve immune functioning, and fight cancer. The diet also recommends eating smaller meals six times daily. The author postulates that this helps to boost metabolism and gives the body continuous energy ( 102006 ). As the name implies, the primary focus of the Abs Diet is to improve the physical appearance of the abdomen. The book emphasizes that a trimmer midsection is associated with better health. Although some research does suggest that abdominal obesity increases the risk for obesity-related comorbidities, including hypertension, dyslipidemia, and metabolic syndrome, the book suggests that following the Abs Diet can result in additional health benefits as well ( 102006 ).
