# Jalap
Scientific Name Convolvulus purga, Ipomoea purga (synonym: Exogonium purga) Family Convolvulaceae Other Common Names Indian Jalap, Ipoméep, Jalap des Indes, Jalap Officinal, Jalap Vrai, Jalapa, Jalape, Mechoacán

## Overview

Jalap is a plant. It has traditionally been used in medicine ( 12 , 271 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, jalap is used as a cathartic, purgative, and diuretic.

Unsafe when used orally. Jalap has potent purgative effects ( 12 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Unsafe when used orally. Jalap can be a menstrual stimulant ( 19 ); avoid using.

#### General

Orally, jalap has potent purgative effects ( 12 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, jalap has potent purgative effects ( 12 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of jalap.

#### Adult

No typical dosage.

#### DIGOXIN (Lanoxin)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Jalap has diuretic and stimulant laxative effects ( 19 ). Theoretically, overuse or abuse of jalap might increase potassium depletion and increase the risk for toxicity with cardiac glycoside drugs.

---

#### DIURETIC DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Jalap has diuretic effects ( 19 ). Overuse of jalap might compound diuretic-induced potassium loss ( 19 ). There is some concern that people taking jalap along with potassium depleting diuretics might have an increased risk for hypokalemia.

Some diuretics that can deplete potassium include chlorothiazide (Diuril), chlorthalidone (Thalitone), furosemide (Lasix), hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ, Hydrodiuril, Microzide), and others.

---

#### LITHIUM
Interaction Rating Minor Be watchful with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Jalap has diuretic effects ( 19 ). Theoretically, due to these potential diuretic effects, jalap might reduce excretion and increase levels of lithium. The dose of lithium might need to be decreased.

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVES
Interaction Rating Major Do not take this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence PROBABLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Jalap has stimulant laxative effects ( 19 ). Concomitant use of jalap with other laxatives may lead to electrolyte and fluid depletion.

---

#### WARFARIN (Coumadin)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Jalap has stimulant laxative effects ( 19 ). In some people jalap can cause diarrhea. Diarrhea can increase the effects of warfarin, increase international normalized ratio (INR), and increase the risk of bleeding. Advise patients who take warfarin not to take excessive amounts of jalap.

|

#### CARDIAC GLYCOSIDE-CONTAINING HERBS

Jalap has stimulant laxative activity ( 19 ). Overuse of jalap may increase the risk of cardiac glycoside toxicity. Cardiac glycoside-containing herbs include black hellebore, Canadian hemp roots, digitalis leaf, hedge mustard, figwort, lily of the valley roots, motherwort, oleander leaf, pheasant's eye plant, pleurisy root, squill bulb leaf scales, and strophanthus seeds ( 19 ). Jalap may increase risk of cardioglycoside toxicity due to increase risk of potassium loss.

---

#### HORSETAIL

Jalap has diuretic effects ( 19 ). Theoretically, concomitant use with horsetail increases the risk of potassium depletion.

---

#### LICORICE

Jalap has diuretic effects ( 19 ). Theoretically, concomitant use with licorice increases the risk of potassium depletion.

---

#### STIMULANT LAXATIVE HERBS

Jalap has stimulant laxative activity ( 19 ). Theoretically, concomitant use with other stimulant laxative herbs may increase the risk of potassium depletion. Stimulant laxative herbs include aloe, alder buckthorn, black root, blue flag, butternut bark, colocynth, European buckthorn, fo ti, gamboge, gossypol, greater bindweed, jalap, manna, Mexican scammony root, rhubarb, senna, and yellow dock.

#### GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) CONDITIONS

Jalap is a stimulant laxative and may have GI irritant effects ( 19 ). Contraindicated in individuals with intestinal obstruction, appendicitis, and infectious or inflammatory gastrointestinal conditions ( 19 ).

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of jalap.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of jalap.

#### General

The applicable part of jalap is the root. The roots of the jalap plant contain gluco-resins that act as a cathartic. They increase water elimination and cause peristalsis ( 271 ).
