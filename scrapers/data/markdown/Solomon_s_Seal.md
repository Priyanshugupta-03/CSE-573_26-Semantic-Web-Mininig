## Overview

Solomon's seal is a plant native to Europe, Nepal, Turkey, and Caucasia ( 102535 ). It has greenish-white flowers and blue-black frosted berries ( 18 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Sometimes used for inflammation and various skin conditions, but there is insufficient reliable evidence to its effectiveness for any use.
- Safety when used orally is unclear. High doses or long-term use might cause nausea, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal complaints.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, Solomon's seal is used for respiratory and lung disorders and inflammation. Topically, Solomon's seal is used for bruises, furuncles, hemorrhoids, skin irritation, inflammation, and wound healing.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Solomon's seal when used orally or topically.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

A thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted. Orally, long-term use of Solomon's seal has been reported to cause gastrointestinal irritation. Use of large doses or overdoses may cause nausea, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal complaints ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, Solomon's seal may cause gastrointestinal irritation when used long-term. Use of large doses or overdoses may cause nausea, diarrhea, and other gastrointestinal complaints ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of Solomon's seal.

#### ANTIDIABETES DRUGS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, concomitant use may enhance hypoglycemic drug effects and alter blood glucose control ( 19 ). Monitor blood glucose.

---

#### CHLORPROPAMIDE (Diabinese)
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Concomitant use may cause additive hypoglycemic effects ( 19 ).

---

#### INSULIN
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Insulin dosage adjustments may be necessary, due to the possible hypoglycemic effects of Solomon's seal ( 19 ).

#### HERBS AND SUPPLEMENTS WITH HYPOGLYCEMIC POTENTIAL

Theoretically, concomitant use with other hypoglycemic herbs may have additive effects ( 19 ).

|

#### DIABETES

Theoretically may interfere with blood glucose control ( 19 ).

#### SURGERY

Solomon's seal might affect blood glucose levels. Theoretically, Solomon's seal might interfere with blood glucose control during and after surgical procedures. Tell patients to discontinue Solomon's seal at least 2 weeks before elective surgical procedures.

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of Solomon's seal.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of Solomon's seal.

#### General

The applicable parts of the plant are the dried rhizome and root ( 18 ).

#### Anti-inflammatory effect

Solomon's seal contains steroid saponins, which are thought to be responsible for its purported anti-inflammatory activity ( 18 ).
