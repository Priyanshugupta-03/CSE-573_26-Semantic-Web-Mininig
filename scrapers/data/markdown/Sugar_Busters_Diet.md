## Overview

The Sugar Busters diet is a low-carbohydrate, high-protein diet. The primary goal of the diet is to eliminate dietary sugars and other carbohydrates with a high glycemic index, including white rice, white bread, and sugary foods, and increase foods high in fiber and lean protein ( 15899 , 15900 ).

## History

The Sugar Busters diet is based on a book by H. Leighton Steward, Morrison Bethea, MD, and Luis Balart, MD, which was originally published in 1995 ( 15900 ).

## People Use This For

The Sugar Busters diet is used for weight loss.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Sugar Busters diet.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the Sugar Busters diet.

Sugar Busters is a diet avoiding refined or simple sugars and making food choices based on the glycemic levels of various foods. Individuals are recommended to eat 3 meals a day and to only eat healthy snacks such as fruit or nuts between meals. Sugar Busters recommends intake of 40% of calories from high fiber foods such as vegetables, 30% from low-fat protein sources, and 30% from fats ( 15900 ). Baking, broiling and grilling are suggested as the methods to cook the foods. Some recommended foods are listed below. Meats including lean beef and pork, Canadian bacon, poultry, game meats (e.g. venison), fish and shellfish are recommended. Vegetables including beans, lentils, peas, spinach, lettuce, squash, zucchini, mushrooms, asparagus, artichokes, cabbage, celery, cucumbers, broccoli, brussels sprouts, eggplant and onions are recommended. Fruits including apples, lemons, limes, pears, cherries, raspberries, kiwis, grapefruits, apricots, melons other than watermelon, tomatoes, oranges and tangerines are recommended.

The Sugar Busters diet is a low-carbohydrate, high protein diet ( 15899 ). It primarily seeks to eliminate dietary sugars and carbohydrates that have a high glycemic index. This goal is similar to other low-carbohydrate diets. High glycemic index foods raise blood glucose levels quickly after consumption. It is theorized that high glycemic foods results in excessive insulin secretion resulting in fat accumulation, obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and other adverse outcomes ( 15903 ). However, there continues to be significant debate and research to determine if a low glycemic index diet can actually prevent these outcomes ( 15902 , 15903 , 15904 ). The Sugar Busters diet suggests avoiding foods such as white rice, white bread, pineapple, syrups, corn chips, potato chips, breaded foods, and sugary snacks. Recommended foods include high fiber vegetables, whole grains, beans, nuts and seeds, eggs, and lean meats such as skinless chicken or seafood ( 15900 ). The Sugar Busters diet has a significantly lower overall glycemic index compared to the high- or moderate-carbohydrate diets ( 15901 ); however, there is no reliable scientific evidence specifically evaluating the Sugar Busters diet for weight loss.
