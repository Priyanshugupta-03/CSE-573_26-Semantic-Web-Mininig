Caution

Homeopathy should not be confused with specific products used to make homeopathic preparations, such as Arnica , Arsenic , Belladonna , Luffa , Oscillococcinum , Poison Ivy , Sulfur , and Zinc . Also, homeopathy should not be confused with other diluted preparations used as medicine, such as Bach Flower Remedies .

## Overview

Homeopathy is an alternative medicine practice that is based on the theory that symptoms can be treated by using highly diluted substances that cause the same symptoms when used in larger doses ( 99743 , 99744 ). However, most homeopathic preparations are so dilute that they contain little or no active ingredient.

This topic discusses homeopathy as a class, including the indications for which homeopathy is most commonly used and/or well-studied. Always keep in mind that safety and effectiveness varies for different homeopathic products. For detailed information on specific ingredients that might be used in homeopathic preparations, refer to the topics listed under CAUTION. The legal status of homeopathic remedies in the US is unique. In 1938, the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act contained legislation that recognized all products listed in the Homeopathic Pharmacopeia of the United States as drugs, to be regulated by the FDA. As a result, homeopathic products are legally sold in the US as a unique category of drug ( 14490 ). While the use of homeopathic drugs in the US is widespread, the FDA regulates homeopathic preparations much less stringently than conventional drugs. For example, homeopathic products are not required to prove effectiveness or safety. However, some regulations do apply. Homeopathic products can only be sold without a prescription if they are specifically for self-limiting conditions such as the common cold. Products marketed for severe conditions such as cancer must be prescription-only ( 14489 ). Furthermore, in 2016, the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) tightened its regulation over marketing materials used in homeopathic products. Homeopathic products are now held to the same expectations and standards as other products marketed with similar claims. For example, if a homeopathic product makes a drug claim, it is held to the standards of a drug. While the FTC still allows homeopathic products to make unsubstantiated efficacy claims in marketing materials, the materials must also clearly state that there is no scientific evidence to support these claims and that the claims are based on theories which are not accepted by most medical experts ( 99744 ). Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) : Despite claims to the contrary, there is no good evidence to support using homeopathy for COVID-19. Recommend healthy lifestyle choices and proven prevention methods instead.

Likely Safe when used orally or topically and appropriately. Most homeopathic preparations contain little or no active ingredient. Therefore, it is unlikely that most homeopathic preparations have any harmful effects. Furthermore, homeopathic treatments have been studied in clinical trials without reports of serious adverse effects ( 19132 , 32413 , 32426 , 34171 , 35632 , 40324 , 40358 , 60080 , 89178 , 89191 )( 89197 , 89206 , 89213 , 89222 , 89240 , 89254 , 89255 , 89258 , 89261 , 89264 )( 89269 , 99740 , 109064 , 109066 , 109067 ). However, it is important to recognize the possibility of homeopathic products being mislabeled and containing active and potentially dangerous substances that are not homeopathic dilutions ( 89211 , 99744 ). Also, the inactive ingredients in some homeopathic preparations may be unsafe for some patient populations. For example, certain homeopathic dilutions have been found to contain allergens or large quantities of ethanol ( 103007 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of homeopathy when used intranasally. One intranasal zinc product (Zicam) is labelled as homeopathic but contains measurable quantities of zinc. Several hundred reports of anosmia have been submitted to the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and the manufacturer of this product ( 16800 , 16801 ).

CHILDREN: Possibly Safe when used orally or topically and appropriately. Homeopathic treatments have been studied in clinical trials without reports of serious adverse effects ( 34170 , 89181 , 89207 ). However, it is important to recognize the possibility of homeopathic products being mislabeled and containing active and potentially dangerous substances that are not homeopathic dilutions ( 89211 , 99744 ). There have been over 400 reports of serious toxicity and death in infants and children using products labeled as homeopathic which were found to contain measurable amounts of belladonna and its active constituents, atropine and scopolamine ( 17493 , 34142 , 34146 , 93537 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using. Since most homeopathic preparations contain little or no active ingredient, it is unlikely that most homeopathic preparations would have an adverse effect during pregnancy or breast-feeding. However, it is important to recognize the possibility of homeopathic products being mislabeled and containing active and potentially dangerous substances that are not homeopathic dilutions ( 89211 , 99744 ). Also, the inactive ingredients in some homeopathic preparations may be unsafe for some patient populations. For example, certain homeopathic dilutions have been found to contain allergens or large quantities of ethanol ( 103007 ).

#### General

Most homeopathic preparations contain little or no active ingredients. Therefore, appropriately prepared homeopathic preparations are unlikely to have adverse effects. However, the inactive ingredients in some homeopathic preparations may be unsafe for some patient populations. Additionally, some homeopathic products have been found to contain measurable quantities of toxic ingredients, which can cause serious adverse effects.

#### Dermatologic

A meta-analysis of clinical research shows that dermatitis is one of the most commonly reported adverse effects with homeopathic treatments. However, the rate of dermatitis was similar to that seen with placebo ( 99749 ). Some research also reports homeopathic aggravation after homeopathy treatments, such as worsening of eczema. This symptom could have occurred because the underlying condition did not receive proper treatment ( 99749 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

A meta-analysis of clinical research shows that gastrointestinal disorders are among the most commonly reported adverse effects with homeopathic treatments. However, there was no difference in adverse effects between homeopathic treatments and placebo ( 99749 ).

#### Hepatic

Orally, homeopathic preparations with high levels of ethanol can precipitate liver injury. In one case report, a 38-year-old obese male with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NALFD)-related cirrhosis developed alcoholic hepatitis after using homeopathic preparations containing 18.3% ethanol for one month ( 103007 ).

#### Immunologic

Some research reports homeopathic aggravation after homeopathy treatments, such as worsening of allergy. This could have occurred because the underlying condition did not receive proper treatment ( 99749 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

A meta-analysis of clinical research shows that headache and dizziness are among the most commonly reported adverse effects with homeopathic treatments. However, there was no difference in adverse effects between homeopathic treatments and placebo ( 99749 ). Some research also reports homeopathic aggravation after homeopathy treatments, such as worsening of headache and hot flushes. These symptoms could have occurred because the underlying condition did not receive proper treatment ( 99749 ).

#### Ocular/Otic

A case report describes anisocoria (unequal pupil sizes) in a 70-year-old female who used homeopathic pink eye relief drops (Similasan) containing belladonna, eyebright, and hepar sulphuris (calcium sulfide) in one eye for 3 days. The pupil dilation lasted more than 2 weeks and did not respond to bright light or pilocarpine ( 106907 ).

#### Pulmonary/Respiratory

Several hundred reports of anosmia have been submitted to the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) related to use of an intranasal zinc product (Zicam). This product is labelled as homeopathic but contains measurable quantities of zinc ( 16800 , 16801 ). Some research reports homeopathic aggravation after homeopathy treatments, such as worsening of asthma. This could have occurred because the underlying condition did not receive proper treatment ( 99749 ).

Possibly Ineffective
Measles.

Homeopathic nosodes do not seem to be effective for the prevention of measles.

Clinical research in students shows that a homeopathic nosode is no different than placebo for increasing antibody titers against measles. Conversely, a conventional measles, mumps, rubella (MMR) vaccine led to a strong antibody response in these students ( 99746 ).

Parotitis (mumps).

Homeopathic nosodes do not seem to be effective for the prevention of mumps.

Clinical research in students shows that a homeopathic diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus, mumps, and measles nosode vaccine is no different than placebo for increasing antibody titers against mumps. Conversely, a conventional measles, mumps, rubella (MMR) vaccine led to a strong antibody response in these students ( 99746 ).

Tetanus.

Homeopathic nosodes do not seem to be effective for the prevention of tetanus.

Clinical research in students shows that a homeopathic tetanus nosode is no different than placebo for increasing antibody titers against tetanus. Conversely, a conventional diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus (Tdap) vaccine led to a strong antibody response in these students ( 99746 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Acne.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for allergic rhinitis.

Preliminary clinical research shows that taking individualized homeopathy does not reduce acne severity or improve quality of life when compared with placebo. Individualized treatment was non-standardized and included 26 different homeopathic products with the most common being sulfur, pulsatilla, Natrum muriaticum (sodium chloride), Calcarea phosphorica (calcium phosphate), and Antimonium crudum ( 109065 ). The effect of any specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Allergic rhinitis (hay fever).

It is unclear if oral or nasal homeopathy is beneficial for allergic rhinitis; the available research is conflicting.

Some clinical research reports no improvement, or evening a worsening of allergic rhinitis symptoms, in patients taking Betula homeopathic dilutions when compared with placebo ( 35632 , 35633 ). However, preliminary clinical research in adults with allergic rhinitis shows that using a homeopathic nasal spray containing homeopathic doses of luffa, Galphimia glauca, histamine, and sulfur (Luffa comp.-Heel, Biologische Heilmittel Heel GmbH) in both nostrils four times daily for 6 weeks might provide similar symptom relief to cromolyn sodium nasal spray ( 60080 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Anxiety.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for anxiety.

Preliminary clinical research in college students with test anxiety shows that taking the homeopathic product Argentum nitricum (silver nitrate) 12X twice a day for 4 days does not reduce anxiety when compared with placebo ( 89204 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Asthma.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for asthma.

Although some measures of lung function have improved following use of homeopathic products in individual clinical studies of patients with asthma, most studies show no difference in lung function when compared with placebo ( 14495 ). Studies are small and heterogenous with respect to dosing and endpoints. The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Attention deficit-hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for ADHD.

A meta-analysis of preliminary clinical research in children aged 5-18 years shows that individualized homeopathic treatment has a modest beneficial effect on ADHD symptoms when compared with control, such as standard care and/or placebo ( 109062 ). In one preliminary clinical trial included in the analysis, 17 different individualized homeopathic treatments were used ( 89210 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Back pain.

It is unclear if topical homeopathy is beneficial for back pain.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with acute low back pain shows that a specific homeopathic gel (Spiroflor SRL) containing comfrey, poison ivy, and marsh Labrador tea in a 2:1:1 ratio might work as well as capsaicin cream for reducing pain ( 40358 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Breast cancer-related hot flashes.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for breast cancer-related hot flashes.

A clinical study in females with non-metastatic breast cancer treated with tamoxifen or an aromatase inhibitor shows that taking a specific homeopathic medicine (Actheane, Laboratories Boiron) daily for 3 months does not reduce hot flashes when compared with placebo. This product contains arnica, black cohosh, bloodroot, and nitroglycerin 4CH each, and the snake Lachesis muta 5CH ( 103004 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Bruises.

It is unclear if oral or topical homeopathy is beneficial for bruises.

Most small clinical trials show that topical products containing arnica 10%, as well as homeopathic oral products, are not effective for reducing postoperative bruising. Oral products were available as tablets, globules, and capsules ( 19107 , 19119 , 96772 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Canker sores.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for canker sores.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with canker sores shows that taking an individualized homeopathic dilution may modestly improve pain and ulcer size when compared with placebo ( 89206 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for CFS.

Clinical research in patients with CFS shows that taking an individualized homeopathic treatment by mouth does not improve symptoms or function when compared with placebo ( 89216 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for COPD.

One small clinical trial in patients with COPD shows that taking oscillococcinum (Boiron Laboratories) one tube weekly for up to 6 months in conjunction with receiving influenza vaccination reduces the risk of having at least one upper respiratory tract infection by around 63% when compared with influenza vaccination alone. However, taking oscillococcinum does not appear to prevent COPD exacerbations or reduce the severity of exacerbations in these patients ( 107274 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for the prevention or treatment of COVID-19.

Clinical research in adults and children aged 5 years and older who are quarantining after exposure to COVID-19 shows that taking the homeopathic preparation coronavirus nosode CVN01 30c twice daily for 3 days modestly reduces the prevalence of developing COVID-19 by day 30, and possibly the period of illness, when compared with placebo. Some research also shows that taking bryonia 30c modestly reduces the prevalence of developing COVID-19 by day 30; however, this finding is dependent on the statistical measure used. Conversely, the homeopathic preparations Arsenicum album 30c, camphora 1M, or a combination of Arsenicum album 30c, bryonia 30c, gelsemium 30c, and influenzinum 30c, were not beneficial ( 109060 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the large number of groups and lack of randomization between groups. Homeopathic preparations have also been evaluated in those hospitalized with COVID-19 receiving standard care. Use of individualized homeopathic preparations appear to reduce the time to clinical recovery and fever resolution by about 2 days and 20 hours, respectively, and modestly reduce symptom severity over 10 days, when compared with placebo. The various homeopathic treatments used in the study included Arsenicum album, bryonia and phosphorus, poison ivy, sodium chloride, and others ( 109067 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Diarrhea.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for diarrhea in children; the available research is conflicting.

A meta-analysis of small, low-quality clinical trials investigating the use of individualized homeopathic treatment shows that the duration of diarrhea is reduced by about 0.8 days when compared with placebo ( 14492 ). However, clinical research shows that use of a combination homeopathic product providing arsenicum album, calcarea carbonica, chamomilla, podophyllum, and sulphur, does not decrease the time to resolution of symptoms when compared with placebo ( 89178 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Encephalitis.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for acute encephalitis syndrome (AES).

Preliminary clinical research in infants and children 6 months and older with AES who are receiving standard treatments shows that taking one of 29 individualized homeopathic treatments modestly improves outcome measures at discharge when compared with placebo. Of those receiving homeopathy, death or neuro-vegetative state occurred in 15% of patients, compared with 30% of those given placebo. For every 6.6 patients treated with homeopathy, one additional death or neuro-vegetative outcome was prevented ( 109068 ). The valitidy of these findings is limited by the use of non-standardized treatments. The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Exercise-induced muscle soreness.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for preventing muscle soreness after exercise.

The evidence related to the use of oral homeopathic arnica formulations for delayed-onset muscle soreness is mixed and limited to small, low-quality studies ( 19130 , 19132 , 89240 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Febrile seizure.

It is unclear if homeopathy is beneficial for the prevention of febrile seizures after vaccination.

In children receiving a second or third Diphtheria-Pertussis-Tetanus (DPT)-Hepatitis B-Polio vaccination who experienced febrile seizure after the first dose, taking six doses of arsenicum album 30C three times daily for 2 days does not reduce the rate of fever when compared with placebo ( 109112 ). However, this trial may have been inadequately powered to detect a difference between groups.

Fibromyalgia.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for fibromyalgia.

A meta-analysis of preliminary clinical studies in patients with fibromyalgia shows that homeopathic treatments seem to modestly reduce fatigue and tender point count, but not depression, when compared to control ( 99742 ). This analysis was limited by poor trial methodology and heterogeneity of homeopathic treatments. For example, while some trials used standardized homeopathic treatment, others used individualized treatment which varied from patient to patient ( 89225 , 99742 ). Additionally, the validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a placebo control. The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for GAD.

In patients with GAD, taking an individualized homeopathic treatment for 3 months does not improve anxiety when compared with placebo. Individualized treatment was non-standardized and included 25 different homeopathic products with the most common being sulfur, Calcarea carbonica (calcium carbonate), phosphorus, and Natrum muriaticum (sodium chloride) ( 106024 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Hypertension.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for preventing hypertension.

Clinical research in patients with pre-hypertension shows that use of individualized homeopathic treatments, in combination with dietary and lifestyle modification for 3 months, does not reduce the number of patients progressing to hypertension when compared with placebo. The most frequently used homeopathic compounds were clubmoss, thuja, and sodium chloride ( 109066 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Influenza.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for the treatment or prevention of influenza.

Although some individual research disagrees, meta-analyses of published and unpublished studies show that there is no reliable evidence that taking oscillococcinum (Boiron Laboratories) can prevent influenza ( 10804 , 12245 , 19066 , 19069 , 92287 ). It is unclear if taking oscillococcinum is beneficial for influenza treatment. Also, the effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Insect bite.

It is unclear if topical homeopathy is beneficial for insect bite treatment.

Preliminary clinical research shows that using a homeopathic after-bite gel, containing Echinacea, marsh Labrador tea, and stinging nettle, on affected areas does not reduce erythema or itching after a mosquito bite when compared with placebo ( 89235 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Insomnia.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for insomnia.

A clinical study in patients with insomnia shows that taking individualized homeopathy might modestly improve sleep time and efficiency as recorded in a sleep diary, but does not seem to affect time to fall asleep or frequency of overnight awakenings, when compared with placebo ( 103005 ). This study is limited by the use of non-standardized treatments and the subjective nature of the measured outcomes. The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for IBS.

Meta-analyses of small preliminary clinical trials in patients with constipation-predominant IBS shows that homeopathic treatments containing asafoetida seem to improve overall symptoms at two weeks when compared with placebo ( 99748 , 103006 ). These analyses are limited by short follow-up duration and significant heterogeneity between the individual trials. The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Lung cancer.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for lung cancer.

Preliminary clinical research in adults with stage IIIB/IIIC or stage IV non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) receiving platinum-based chemotherapy shows that add-on individualized homeopathy treatment is associated with greater quality of life scores at 9 and 18 months when compared with placebo. However, it is unclear if the improvement from baseline differed between groups ( 106020 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Migraine headache.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for migraine headache.

Preliminary clinical research shows that taking an undefined homeopathic solution decreases the occurrence of migraine attacks when compared with placebo ( 34171 , 89228 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Osteoarthritis.

It is unclear if topical homeopathy is beneficial for osteoarthritis.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with osteoarthritis shows that use of a homeopathic gel containing comfrey, marsh Labrador tea, and poison ivy, 1 gram three times daily for 4 weeks, is as effective as a 5% piroxicam gel for reducing pain ( 89245 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Otitis media.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for otitis media.

Clinical research in children aged 18 months to 6 years with otitis media shows that individualized homeopathic remedies three times daily for 5 days reduces symptoms over 1-3 days when compared with placebo ( 89180 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear .

Plantar heel pain.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for plantar heel pain.

A small clinical study in patients with plantar fasciitis shows that use of individualized homeopathy treatments for 3 months has a large beneficial effect on foot function when compared with placebo. Symptoms such as pain, disability, and activity limitation were improved. The most common homeopathic treatments were sodium chloride, poison ivy, and rue ( 109064 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Postoperative pain.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for postoperative pain. Most of the available research utilizes arnica-based products and findings are mixed.

One clinical trial shows that taking homeopathic arnica 30C preparation orally modestly reduces postoperative pain following tonsillectomy release when compared with placebo ( 19114 ). However, homeopathic arnica D4 is inferior to diclofenac following bunion surgery ( 109102 ). Also, some clinical research in patients recovering from a knee ligament reconstruction shows that a homeopathic solution containing Arnica montana 5 CH, Bryonia alba 5 CH, Hypericum perforatum 5 CH, and Ruta graveolens 3 DH is no more effective than placebo for reducing analgesic use ( 32413 ). Other clinical research in patients recovering from carpal tunnel surgery shows that taking a homeopathic Arnica montana solution by mouth is no more effective than taking placebo for reducing postoperative pain and bruising ( 32413 ). Preliminary clinical research in patients recovering from abdominal hysterectomy also shows that taking Arnica C30 homeopathic solution by mouth for 5 days does not improve pain or instances of infection when compared with placebo and usual care ( 19117 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Postoperative recovery.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for postoperative recovery.

One meta-analysis of small to moderate clinical trials shows that taking homeopathic arnica does not improve postoperative recovery when compared with controls. Recovery endpoints were combined and included pain, swelling, bruising, wound healing, and others ( 109061 ). One small clinical trial included in the analysis, in patients recovering from saphenous stripping, used a homeopathic dilution of Arnica montana CH5 ( 19119 ). Another meta-analysis of clinical research in patients recovering from abdominal or gynecological surgery suggests that homeopathic treatment may decrease time to first flatus; however, homeopathic treatments greater than or equal to 12C strength appeared to be no more effective than placebo ( 32426 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Quality of life.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for improving quality of life in adults with chronic conditions.

A clinical study in females with various chronic symptoms, including anxiety, joint problems, headache, hypertension, and dizziness, shows that taking a homeopathic treatment is associated with higher self-reported quality of life at 6 months when compared with not using homeopathic treatment. However, it is unclear if the improvement from baseline differed between groups. The validity of this finding was also limited by the lack of placebo control ( 106023 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Radiation dermatitis.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for radiation dermatitis.

Preliminary clinical research in patients receiving radiotherapy shows that taking a homeopathic treatment by mouth may improve symptoms of radiodermatitis such as skin color, warmth, and swelling during the recovery period, but not throughout the radiotherapy treatment period, when compared with placebo. Treatment consisted of Belladonna 7cH, 3 granules sublingually twice a day and X-ray 15cH, 3 granules sublingually once a day ( 34142 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for RA.

Some preliminary clinical research shows that homeopathy does not improve pain or stiffness when compared with placebo in patients with RA ( 89256 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Rhinosinusitis.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for acute or chronic rhinosinusitis.

Some preliminary clinical research in adults with acute rhinosinusitis suggests that taking a specific homeopathic product (Sinfrontal), which contains Cinnabaris Trituration D4, Ferrum phosphoricum Trituration D3, and Mercurius solubilis Hahnemanii Trituration D6, for 22 days may improve symptoms of acute maxillary rhinosinusitis when compared with placebo ( 89191 ). For chronic rhinosinusitis, one small clinical study in adults shows that individualized homeopathy does not improve patient-reported symptoms when compared with placebo ( 106021 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Sepsis.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for sepsis.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with severe sepsis shows that taking a 200C homeopathic treatment at 12-hour intervals during a hospital stay does not improve survival after 30 days when compared with placebo ( 89258 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Traumatic brain injury (TBI).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for TBI.

Preliminary clinical research in patients that have persistent symptoms after a mild TBI shows that taking a homeopathic treatment by mouth may modestly improve some functional measures when compared with placebo ( 89264 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Upper respiratory tract infection (URTI).

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for URTI prevention or treatment.

A meta-analysis of 6 clinical trials shows that using homeopathy for the treatment or prevention of acute URTI in children does not improve the cure rate, disease severity, or recurrence rate ( 106022 ). However, a high risk for bias and high heterogeneity limits the validity of these findings. Another meta-analysis of two clinical trials in otherwise healthy children less than 10 years of age with URTIs shows that individualized homeopathic treatments do not reduce antibiotic usage ( 99745 ). Homeopathic treatments in these studies were individualized based on symptoms ( 89250 , 89251 ). Homeopathic treatment also did not seem to prevent recurrence of URTIs or treat existing URTIs in children ( 99745 ). Although one small study in children suggests that homeopathic treatments can decrease the duration of URTI, this study is of low quality ( 89252 ).

Vaginal candidiasis.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for vaginal candidiasis.

Some preliminary clinical research shows that homeopathic treatment is less effective than itraconazole for vaginal candidiasis ( 89213 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Vertigo.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for vertigo.

A meta-analysis of clinical and observational research shows that taking a specific homeopathic solution (Vertigoheel) does not improve symptoms of vertigo when compared with placebo ( 89195 ). The effect of other homeopathic products is unclear.

Vitiligo.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for vitiligo.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with vitiligo shows that taking individualized homeopathic treatment for 6 months might reduce symptom severity and improve quality of life when compared with baseline; however, it is unclear if any improvements are statistically significant when compared with placebo ( 109063 ). Seventeen different homeopathic treatments were used; the effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

Warts.

It is unclear if oral homeopathy is beneficial for warts.

Some clinical research in children and adults shows that taking a homeopathic treatment by mouth does not decrease warts or wart size when compared with placebo ( 89267 , 106025 ). The effect of specific homeopathic products is unclear.

More evidence is needed to rate homeopathy for these and other uses.

Homeopathy is an alternative medicine practice that is based on the law of similars. This theory states that symptoms can be treated by using highly diluted substances that cause the same symptoms when used in larger doses ( 99743 , 99744 ). Most homeopathic preparations are so dilute that they have little or no active ingredient. Dosing depends on the specific homeopathic product being used. Homeopathic preparations follow a specific nomenclature to indicate the level of dilution, and therefore, the level of potency. Dilutions of 1/10 are signified by "X." So, 1X = 1/10, 2X = 1/100, 3X = 1/1000, and 6X = 1/1,000,000, and so on ( 14487 ). Dilutions of 1/100 are signified by "C." So, 1C = 1/100, 2C = 1/10,000, 3C = 1/1,000,000, and so on ( 14487 ). Dilutions of 1/50,000 are signified by "Q". So, Q1 = 5/1,000,000,000, Q2 = 2.5/100,000,000,000,000, Q3 = 1.25/10,000,000,000,000,000,000, and so on ( 99740 ). Some homeopathic remedies use individual ingredients ( 89206 , 107274 ), whereas others contain multiple ingredients ( 60080 , 89178 , 89191 , 89245 , 103004 ). Common homeopathic ingredients include aconite, antimonium, arnica, belladonna, chamomile, iron, mercury, magnesium, nux vomica, poison ivy, and many others. Many homeopathic remedies are individualized to the patient, symptoms, and condition. Many clinical studies examine the use of such individualized treatments ( 89206 , 89210 , 106020 , 106024 , 109060 , 109063 , 109064 , 109065 , 109066 , 109067 ). There are also commercially available homeopathic remedies that are used for specific indications, some of which have been investigated in clinical research ( 40358 , 89191 , 103004 , 107274 ).

Homeopathy was started over 200 years ago by the German physician Samuel Hahnemann. The word homeopathy comes from Greek and literally means "similar disease" ( 14486 , 14487 ). At the time that Hahnemann developed homeopathy, medical treatment focused on balancing the 'humors' of the body. The medical treatments of the day were blood letting, purging, leeching, and other 'treatments' that often did more harm than good. Homeopathy is guided by several basic theories and principles:

#### Homeopathic aggravation

A homeopathic aggravation means a temporary worsening of an existing condition after a homeopathic treatment. In the theory of homeopathy, this is a desired response which should then be followed by improvement of the condition ( 99749 ). Overall, these theories and principles are generally inconsistent with the current understanding of pharmacology, chemistry, and physics.

#### Law of infinitesimals

In the beginning of homeopathy, small doses of the "proved" substance were used. This eventually evolved into using extreme dilutions of the original substance. Hahnemann suggested that the more dilute the substance, the more potent effect it had against a given disease.

#### Law of similars

Hahnemann believed that like cured like. If a substance in large amounts CAUSES a certain disease, then that same substance in small amounts could cure the disease. Hahnemann proposed that disease was a dysfunction of the body's ability to cure itself. Therefore, administering a diluted form of a toxic substance could provide the stimulus the body needed to begin to heal itself ( 14488 ).

#### Potentiation through dilution

Related to the law of infinitesimals is the concept of potentiation through dilution. The preparation of a homeopathic product involves a step-wise dilution process along with succussion. Each dilution is succussed, or shaken vigorously. It is believed that this process leaves behind the image, essence, or spirit of the original compound in the water. The more times this process is repeated, or the more dilute the end-product, the more potent the homeopathic preparation ( 14486 , 14488 ).

#### Provings

In order to find homeopathic treatments, Hahnemann conducted "provings." This involved testing substances such as herbs or minerals in healthy people to see what kind of reaction occurred. Reactions were documented in detail in order to determine which substances might serve as treatments for certain conditions. If a substance caused fever, then that substance was noted as a treatment for conditions involving fever.
