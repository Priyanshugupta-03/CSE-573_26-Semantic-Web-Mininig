# Feldenkrais Method
Other Common Names Awareness Through Movement, Feldenkrais Bodywork, Functional Integration, Gestalt Synergy, Méthode Feldenkrais, Método Feldenkrais
|

---

## Overview

The Feldenkrais method involves gentle movements such as stretching, reaching, and changing posture. It is often used as an adjunct to conventional interventions ( 21071 ).

## History

The Feldenkrais method was developed by Moshe Feldenkrais (1904-1984), a Russian-born Israeli physicist who was disabled from a knee injury. Moshe Feldenkrais called on his formal training in science and martial arts to develop an approach that aimed to help the body move in more natural and comfortable ways ( 21063 ).

## People Use This For

The Feldenkrais method has been used for back pain, balance problems, cerebral palsy, chronic pain, dystonia, eating disorders, athletic performance, fibromyalgia, improving coordination, improving mobility, learning, mood, multiple sclerosis (MS), musculoskeletal disorders, neck pain, shoulder pain, neurologic disorders (rehabilitation), physical functioning and well-being in the elderly, post-stroke rehabilitation, quality of life, rheumatoid arthritis (RA), temporomandibular joint disorder (TMD), and voice disturbance.

Likely Safe when administered for up to 15 weeks. The Feldenkrais method has been used in clinical research without reports of adverse events  ( 21064 , 21065 , 21066 , 100216 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient available evidence; however, there is no reason to expect safety issues.

#### General

The Feldenkrais method is well tolerated. No adverse events have been reported in clinical literature ( 21064 , 21065 , 21066 , 100216 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Back pain.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with chronic low back pain shows that participating in one 30-minute Feldenkrais Awareness Through Movement (ATM) session decreases the unpleasantness, but not the severity, of pain when compared with baseline ( 21065 ). ATM has also been studied for a longer duration. A small clinical study in a similar population shows that practicing the ATM technique for 10 lessons reduces pain and disability to a similar degree as practicing a multidisciplinary method called Back School ( 100211 ). Another small clinical study in females with mild to moderate chronic low back pain shows that practicing the Feldenkrais method in outpatient exercise therapy sessions lasting 30-45 minutes, twice weekly for 5 weeks, improves quality of life and interoceptive awareness and decreases disability index when compared with home-based core stability exercises. However, changes in pain scores were similar between groups ( 105826 ).

Fall prevention.

There is interest in using the Feldenkrais method to prevent falls by improving balance and mobility in older patients. One small clinical study in healthy older adults shows that practicing Feldenkrais Awareness Through Movement (ATM) for one hour 3 times weekly for 5 weeks improves mobility and speed and reduces the fear of falling when compared to a waitlist control ( 100214 ). In another small clinical study in elderly patients, participating in a specific group program (Getting Grounded Gracefully) based on the Feldenkrais ATM for 40-60 minutes twice weekly for 8 weeks modestly reduces the fear of falling and improves gait speed when compared with no additional intervention. However, there was no difference in balance, activities of daily living, or other mobility measures ( 100215 ).

Fibromyalgia.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with fibromyalgia shows that one individual and two group Feldenkrais sessions weekly for 15 weeks improves balance and lower extremity muscle function in the short-term when compared with receiving group-based pain education followed by a pool program ( 21064 ).

Learning disabilities.

A small clinical study in adults with learning disabilities shows that receiving 30 Feldenkrais Awareness Through Movement (ATM) classes improves physical function, such as balance and chair stands, when compared with no intervention ( 100213 ).

Multiple sclerosis (MS).

A small clinical crossover study in patients with MS shows that 45 minutes of Feldenkrais bodywork weekly for 8 weeks improves stress and anxiety, but does not improve MS symptoms, when compared with no treatment ( 21067 ).

Neck pain.

A small clinical study in visually-impaired patients with chronic neck or scapular pain shows that receiving a 2-hour long Feldenkrais session weekly for 12 weeks reduces neck pain for up to 1 year when compared with no treatment ( 100210 ).

Parkinson disease.

A small clinical study in patients with Parkinson disease shows that participating in Feldenkrais exercise sessions for 1 hour twice weekly for 25 weeks improves quality of life and depression when compared with receiving educational lectures ( 100212 ).

Quality of life.

Preliminary clinical research in healthy adult females shows that one 90-minute Awareness Through Movement (ATM) session reduces anxiety levels and improves mood and well-being when compared with non-mindful aerobic exercise or a non-exercise control ( 21066 ).

More evidence is needed to rate the Feldenkrais method for these uses.

The Feldenkrais Method is a specific, trademarked technique that is administered by licensed personnel; however, there might be differences in teaching methodologies between practitioners. The Feldenkrais method involves two main forms: Awareness Through Movement (ATM) and Functional Integration (FI). ATM involves group exercises taught verbally by a physiotherapist. FI involves one-on-one gentle manual guidance by the physiotherapist. Both forms involve stretching, reaching, movement, and changing posture ( 100211 , 100216 ). The patient is guided through movements and sensations to learn which ones feel easier, require less effort, and do not generate pain. The sessions are usually 30 minutes to 1 hour and include practicing sitting, reaching, walking, turning, and other common movements ( 100216 ).

None known.

None known.

The Feldenkrais method involves two forms: Awareness Through Movement (ATM) and Functional Integration (FI). These forms intend to increase patient's self-awareness, repertoire of movements, and function ( 100211 ). The creator of the Feldenkrais method, Moshe Feldenkrais, stated that the method depends mainly on the human ability to learn how to learn. The patient is guided through movements and sensations and judges which ones feel easier and take less effort. This practice and learning is thought to reduce pain and improve mobility of various chronic conditions by increasing awareness, relaxation, and efficiency of movements ( 100216 ).
