# Mercury Herb
Scientific Name Mercurialis annua Family Euphorbiaceae Other Common Names Chou de Chien, Foirolle, Hierba Mercurial, Merculiare, Mercurial, Mercuriale, Mercuriale Annuelle, Mercuriale des Jardins, Ortie Bâtarde, Ortiga Muerta, Vignette

## Overview

Mercury herb is an annual plant found in North America, Europe, and northern parts of Africa. It typically grows to around 16-24 inches tall ( 4144 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Sometimes used for constipation, infections, swelling, and other conditions, but there is insufficient reliable evidence to rate its effectiveness for these uses.
- Likely unsafe when used orally. High doses might cause nerve paralysis, liver failure, kidney failure, and even death.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, mercury herb is used for inflammation, infections, water retention, gastrointestinal disorders, and urinary tract disorders.

Likely Unsafe when the fresh plant, particularly the root and rhizome, are used orally ( 18 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally ( 18 ); avoid using.

|

#### General

Orally, mercury herb might cause diarrhea and overactive bladder ( 18 ). Symptoms of poisoning might include nerve paralysis, liver and kidney failure, and death ( 18 ). The pollen has shown to be allergenic ( 4143 , 4144 ), and may be responsible for rhinitis and asthmatic symptoms ( 4143 ). Mercury herb might also cause allergic reactions in individuals allergic to ash, castor bean plant, mugwort, olive, pellitory-of-the-wall, and Salsola kali ( 4143 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of mercury herb include the flowering plant, root, and rhizome. Mercury herb contains saponins, a small amount of cyanogenic glycosides, pyridone derivatives including hermidin, amines including methylamine, and flavonoids including rutin, narcissin, and isorhamnetin. The insignificant amount of cyanogenic glycosides are not believed to account for the plant's toxicity ( 18 ).

#### Laxative effects

The root and stock of mercury herb are thought to have laxative effects ( 18 ).
