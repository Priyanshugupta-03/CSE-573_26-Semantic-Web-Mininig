## Overview

Pellitory-of-the-wall is a plant. The name comes from its habit of growing in old walls and dry, stony areas ( 7161 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, pellitory-of-the-wall is used for hypertension, congestive heart failure (CHF), fluid retention, constipation, cough, urinary tract infections (UTIs), and kidney stones (nephrolithiasis). Topically, pellitory-of-the-wall is used for burns and wound healing.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of pellitory-of-the-wall.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted. Pellitory-of-the-wall pollen has been associated with seasonal allergy ( 7161 ).

#### Immunologic

Pellitory-of-the-wall pollen has been associated with seasonal allergy ( 7161 ).
