# American Elder
Scientific Name Sambucus canadensis Family Adoxaceae/Sambucaceae or Caprifoliaceae Other Common Names American Elderberry, Common Elderberry, Elder Flower, Elderberry, Sabugueiro, Saúco, Saúco Americano, Saúco de Canada, Sureau, Sureau Blanc, Sureau blanc, Sureau du Canada, Sweet Elder
|

---

Caution

Do not confuse American elder with similarly named products, such as Dwarf Elder , Elderberry , and Elderflower .

## Overview

American elder is a plant. The flower and ripe berry are used in traditional medicine. The fruit is also used as a flavoring component in foods and beverages. The stem contains cyanogenic glycosides and has been reported to cause toxicity in children ( 6 ).

Likely Safe when the flower or cooked fruit is used in amounts found in foods. The flowers have Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) status in the US ( 4912 ). Cooked, ripe fruit has few if any adverse effects when consumed in typical food amounts ( 6 ).

Possibly Unsafe when the leaves, stems, or unripe fruit are used orally. All these plant parts contain cyanogenic glycosides ( 12 ). American elder juice has been reported to cause dizziness, nausea, numbness, stupor, and vomiting when consumed in large quantities; limit intake to avoid toxicity ( 6 ).

There is insufficient reliable information about the safety of American elder when used orally in medicinal amounts.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when the leaves, stems, or unripe fruit are used orally ( 6 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the flower or cooked fruit during pregnancy or lactation; avoid using.

There is currently a limited amount of information on the adverse effects of American elder when taken in medicinal amounts. A thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Dizziness, nausea, numbness, stupor, vomiting.

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, ingesting several glasses of American elderberry juice has been reported to cause nausea and vomiting ( 6 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, ingesting several glasses of American elderberry juice has been reported to cause weakness, dizziness, numbness, and stupor ( 6 ).

#### Presentation

Elder leaves ( 6 ) and unripe berries ( 12 ) contain cyanogenic glycosides. If ingested, the leaves or unripe berries can cause cyanide poisoning ( 6 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of American elder are the leaf, flower, and ripe fruit. They are a rich source of vitamin C ( 6 , 19 ). Elder leaves ( 6 ) and unripe berries ( 12 ) contain cyanogenic glycosides. The fruit contains anthocyanin ( 116130 ). Elder flowers are believed to have diuretic and laxative effects ( 6 ). Sambucus species contain plant lectins with hemagglutinin characteristics that might be useful in blood typing and other blood testing ( 6 , 11 ).

#### Anti-aging effects

American elder fruit is a rich source of anthocyanin, an antioxidant. Antioxidants are of interest for slowing the aging process. In an animal model, anthocyanin extracted from American elder fruit promoted the clearance of aging cells, possibly by inhibiting signaling pathways involved in the aging process and/or the removal of aged cells ( 116130 ).

#### Antibacterial effects

In vitro research suggests American elder leaf might have antibacterial activity ( 12666 ).

#### Gastrointestinal effects

Elder flowers are believed to have laxative effects, but this has not been well studied ( 6 ).
