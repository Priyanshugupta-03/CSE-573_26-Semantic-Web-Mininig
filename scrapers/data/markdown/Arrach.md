## Overview

Arrach is a plant that is native to Europe, although it can also be found growing in areas of North America. Also known as stinking goosefoot, it is easily identified by its rotten-fish smell, which is related to its trimethylamine content ( 86403 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, arrach is used for dysmenorrhea and to induce menstruation. Topically, arrach is used for dysmenorrhea.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of arrach.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, taking arrach may have photosensitizing effects ( 19 ). However, a high quality assessment of safety has not been conducted.

#### Dermatologic

Orally, taking arrach may have photosensitizing effects. Avoidance of excessive sun exposure is recommended in patients taking arrach ( 19 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of arrach.

#### General

The applicable part of arrach is the whole flowering plant. Arrach contains trimethylamine ( 86403 ), saponins, and oxalic acid.

#### Phototoxic effects

Chenopodium species, including arrach, are stated to be potentially photosensitizing ( 19 ).
