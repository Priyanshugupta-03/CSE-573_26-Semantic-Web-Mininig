# Ajuga nipponensis
Scientific Name Ajuga nipponensis Family Lamiaceae Other Common Names Ajuga, Ajuga Nipponensis, Bugle nippone, Junihitoe
|

---

Caution

Do not confuse Ajuga nipponensis with Bugle , which is sometimes also called Ajuga.

## Overview

Ajuga nipponensis is an herb. The whole plant has been traditionally used as anti-inflammatory, diuretic, and anti-infective.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Ajuga nipponensis when used orally.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

|

#### General

There is currently a limited amount of information on the adverse effects of Ajuga nipponensis.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Oral : Diarrhea, nausea, vomiting.

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, Ajuga nipponensis has been reported to cause diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting ( 14070 ).

#### Renal

Orally, Ajuga nipponensis has been reported to cause acute renal failure. An adult male with pre-existing liver cirrhosis developed acute renal failure after ingesting approximately 45 grams of whole Ajuga nipponensis ( 14070 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Cough.

Although there has been interest in using oral Ajuga nipponensis for cough, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Ajuga nipponensis for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate Ajuga nipponensis for this use.

The applicable part of Ajuga nipponensis is the whole plant. Constituents of Ajuga nipponensis include cyasterone compounds and ajugasterone ( 14070 ).
