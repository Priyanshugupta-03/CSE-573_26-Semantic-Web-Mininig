## Overview

Sessile oak is a tree commonly found throughout Europe. The wood of this oak contains high amounts of chemicals known as ellagitannins ( 93293 , 93413 , 93414 ).

## History

Historically, the acorns of sessile oak have been used for food. The wood has been used for construction and for oak barrels to store wine ( 93414 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, sessile oak bark extract is used for diarrhea and mild inflammation of the mouth, throat, genital region, and anal region. Topically, sessile oak bark is used for inflammatory skin conditions.

#### General

The applicable parts of sessile oak are the bark and wood. Sessile oak wood and bark contain various ellagitannins, including the grandinin and roburin E. Sessile oak wood also contains triterpene saponins and their glycosides ( 93297 , 93414 ). Oligomeric proanthocyanidins include mainly catechin, epicatechin, and gallocatechin ( 93418 ). In the human diet, constituents of sessile oak can be obtained by consuming wines and spirits stored in sessile oak barrels ( 93412 , 93415 , 93417 ).

#### Anti-cancer effects

There is some preliminary laboratory evidence suggesting that sessile bark extract might inhibit the growth of some types of cancer cells, such as colon, prostate, and glioblastoma ( 93416 ). However, the mechanism of action is not clear, and clinical effects are unknown.

#### Antioxidant effects

Antioxidant activity of sessile oak leaves has been shown in laboratory models ( 93302 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

Sessile oak bark extract decreases blood pressure as well as cardiac fibrosis in an animal model of metabolic and cardiovascular disease risk. This may be related to a decrease in abdominal fat as well as blood glucose, cholesterol, and triglycerides ( 93415 ).

#### Hepatoprotective effects

In an animal model of metabolic and cardiovascular disease risk, sessile oak bark extract has hepatoprotective effects. The extract protects against liver fibrosis and decreases blood levels of the liver enzymes aspartate transaminase, alanine transaminase, and alkaline phosphatase ( 93415 ).
