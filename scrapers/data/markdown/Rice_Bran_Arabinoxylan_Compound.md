## Overview

Rice bran arabinoxylan compound is a blend of hemicelluloses derived by reacting hydrolyzed rice bran with enzymes from shiitake mushroom. The primary hemicellulose in rice bran arabinoxylan compound is arabinoxylan ( 98688 , 98692 , 98693 , 99101 ).

## History

A specific rice bran arabinoxylan compound called MGN-3 (BioBran, Daiwa Pharmaceutical Co., Ltd.) received the Japan Health Food Authorization (JHFA) Mark from the Japan Health Food and Nutrition Food Association in 1999 ( 98693 ). This ingredient was included in products marketed in the US by LaneLabs. In December 1999, the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) sought a permanent injunction against the marketing of MGN-3 by LaneLabs. The complaint charged that MGN-3 is an unapproved drug product promoted as treatment for cancer and HIV infection ( 387 ). In July 2004, a US District judge issued a permanent injection against LaneLabs, and the company was ordered to make restitution to anyone who purchased products containing MGN-3 since September 22, 1999. However, as of 2019, rice bran arabinoxylan compound is still marketed in the US and other countries under different brand names, including Noxylane4 (LaneLabs), BRM4 (Daiwa Health Development), PeakImmune4 (Daiwa Health Development), and others.

## People Use This For

Orally, rice bran arabinoxylan compound is most commonly used for boosting immune function. It is also used for cancer, chemotherapy-related side effects, HIV/AIDS, hepatitis, diabetes, chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS), and other immunodeficiency disorders.

Possibly Safe when used orally and appropriately, short-term. Rice bran arabinoxylan compound has been used with apparent safety in doses of 1 gram daily for 12 months, 3 grams daily for 6 months, or up to 6 grams daily for 2-3 months ( 98688 , 98692 , 98693 , 104768 , 104769 , 104770 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of rice bran arabinoxylan compound when used for longer than 12 months.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, rice bran arabinoxylan compound seems to be well tolerated. Side effects reported in clinical trials are typically mild and may include nausea and irritable bowel symptoms ( 98688 , 98693 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, rice bran arabinoxylan compound has been reported to cause one case of mild nausea and one case of irritable bowel symptoms in a clinical trial ( 98693 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS).

Evidence from an early, uncontrolled, observational study suggested that rice bran arabinoxylan compound can reduce fatigue and increase natural killer cell activity in patients with CFS. However, results from a more recent, higher quality clinical study shows that taking rice bran arabinoxylan compound 2 grams three times daily for 8 weeks does not improve fatigue, anxiety, depression, or other symptoms related to CFS compared to placebo ( 98693 ). However, due to the small size of this latter study, the possibility of type II error (false negative) cannot be ruled out.

Hepatitis C.

A small preliminary clinical trial shows that taking rice bran arabinoxylan compound 1 gram daily for 3 months is as effective for reducing viral load as PEGylated interferon 180 micrograms weekly subcutaneously plus ribavirin 1000 or 1200 mg daily based on weight in patients with hepatitis C virus genotype 4. There was no effect on liver enzymes ( 98688 ). A larger, well-designed trial is needed to confirm these results.

HIV/AIDS.

Preliminary clinical research in patients on stable antiretroviral therapy for HIV shows that taking a specific rice bran arabinoxylan product (Biobran/MGV-3, Daiwa Pharmaceuticals) 1 gram three times daily for 6 months does not increase CD4+ cell count, reduce blood pressure, or improve markers of liver and kidney function when compared with placebo. However, it does decrease CD8+ cell count when compared with placebo. This study may have been inadequately powered to detect a difference between groups ( 104769 , 104770 ).

Liver cancer.

Preliminary clinical research in patients with stage I or II hepatocellular carcinoma shows that taking rice bran arabinoxylan compound 1 gram daily for 12 months in conjunction with interventional therapy may increase survival after 2 years and reduce the risk of recurrence compared to interventional therapy alone. Survival after the second year was 35% for patients treated with rice bran arabinoxylan compound plus interventional therapy compared to only 6.7% for patients treated with interventional therapy alone. Recurrence of the disease occurred in 31.6% of patients treated with rice bran arabinoxylan compound plus interventional therapy compared to 46.7% of patients treated with interventional therapy alone. Interventional therapy consisted of transarterial oily chemoembolization (TOCE), percutaneous ethanol injection therapy (PEIT), and/or radiofrequency ablation (RFA) ( 98692 ).

Quality of life.

Preliminary clinical research in healthy adults at least 56 years of age shows that taking a specific rice bran arabinoxylan product (Biobran/MGV-3, Daiwa Pharmaceuticals) 250 mg divided three times daily for 3 months improves physical and mental health-related quality of life scores when compared with placebo ( 104768 ).

More evidence is needed to rate rice bran arabinoxylan compound for these uses.

#### Adult
Oral:

Hepatitis C : Rice bran arabinoxylan compound 1 gram daily for 3 months has been used ( 98688 ). Liver cancer : For stage I or II hepatocellular carcinoma, rice bran arabinoxylan compound 1 gram daily for 12 months has been used in conjunction with an interventional therapy consisting of transarterial oily chemoembolization with or without percutaneous ethanol injection treatment ( 98692 ). Quality of life : A specific rice bran arabinoxylan (Biobran/MGV-3, Daiwa Pharmaceuticals) 250 mg divided three times daily for 3 months has been used ( 104768 ).

#### IMMUNOSUPPRESSANTS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, rice bran arabinoxylan compound might interfere with immunosuppressants by stimulating the immune system. In laboratory and human research, rice bran arabinoxylan compound has been shown to stimulate the activity of immune cells ( 3116 , 3117 , 98688 , 98689 , 98690 , 98691 , 98694 , 98695 ).

Some immunosuppressants include azathioprine (Imuran), basiliximab (Simulect), cyclosporine (Neoral, Sandimmune), daclizumab (Zenapax), muromonab-CD3 (OKT3, Orthoclone OKT3), mycophenolate (CellCept), tacrolimus (FK506, Prograf), sirolimus (Rapamune), prednisone (Deltasone, Orasone), corticosteroids (glucocorticoids), and others.

None known.

#### AUTOIMMUNE DISEASES

Rice bran arabinoxylan compound seems to stimulate immune function ( 3116 , 3117 , 98688 , 98689 , 98690 , 98691 , 98694 , 98695 ). Theoretically, rice bran arabinoxylan compound might exacerbate autoimmune diseases by stimulating disease activity. Advise patients with autoimmune diseases such as multiple sclerosis (MS), systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), rheumatoid arthritis (RA), or others to avoid or use rice bran arabinoxylan compound with caution.

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the toxicology of rice bran arabinoxylan compound.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of rice bran arabinoxylan compound.

#### General

Rice bran arabinoxylan compound is a hemicellulose complex containing arabinoxylan as a major component. It is produced by hydrolyzing rice bran using enzymes from mycelia of Shiitake mushrooms ( 98688 , 98692 , 98693 ).

#### Anti-cancer effects

Rice bran arabinoxylan compound is used for cancer. It is described as a biological response modifier ( 99101 ). It is thought to sensitize cancer cells, increasing apoptosis ( 98692 , 99101 ). Results from small studies of healthy individuals and individuals with cancer suggest that rice bran arabinoxylan compound enhances natural killer cell and dendritic cell activity and increases the production of cytokines from activated T cells. However, it does not seem to increase natural killer cell count ( 3118 , 3119 , 3120 , 98690 , 99101 ). In vitro and in animal models, rice bran arabinoxylan compound has been shown to activate dendritic cells to prime tumor cell killing by T cells and to stimulate the activity of other immune cells such as macrophages. Rice bran arabinoxylan compound also has synergistic effects with chemotherapeutic agents in animal models ( 3116 , 3117 , 98688 , 98689 , 98691 , 98694 , 98695 , 99101 ).

#### Immune effect

Some laboratory studies suggest that rice bran arabinoxylan compound might improve immunity by enhancing natural killer cell activity, activating dendritic cells, increasing interferon-gamma production by peripheral blood mononuclear cells, increasing the production of other cytokines, and acting synergistically with interleukin-2 (aldesleukin) to increase natural killer cell activity and production of tumor necrosis factor alpha ( 3116 , 3117 , 98688 , 98689 , 98691 , 98694 , 98695 ). Other evidence suggests that rice bran arabinoxylan compound has activity against HIV  and increases the phagocytic capacity of macrophages ( 3113 , 98694 ). Results from small studies of healthy individuals and individuals with cancer suggest that rice bran arabinoxylan compound enhances natural killer cell and dendritic cell activity ( 3118 , 3119 , 3120 , 98690 , 99101 ). Increased levels of interferon-gamma and other cytokines have also been shown in human research ( 98688 , 98690 ).
