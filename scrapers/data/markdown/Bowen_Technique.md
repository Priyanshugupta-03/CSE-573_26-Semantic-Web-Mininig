## Overview

The Bowen technique originated from Tom Bowen (1916-1982), a self-proclaimed osteopath. This manipulative therapy is used in humans and in animals ( 100207 ).

## People Use This For

The Bowen technique is a manipulative therapy used for pain, fatigue, relaxation, frozen shoulder, stress, stroke recovery, and other conditions.

Possibly Safe when used appropriately. The Bowen technique has been used in clinical research without reports of adverse effects ( 100206 , 100207 , 100208 , 100209 , 104755 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; however, there is no reason to expect safety issues.

#### General

The Bowen technique seems to be well tolerated. No adverse events have been reported in clinical research ( 100206 , 100207 , 100208 , 100209 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Frozen shoulder.

Preliminary clinical research shows that the Bowen technique can reduce pain and improve mobility in patients with frozen shoulder when compared with baseline ( 14607 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

Pain (chronic).

Preliminary clinical research in people with various forms of chronic pain shows that receiving six sessions of Bowen technique does not reduce patient-reported pain when compared with sham treatment. Some patients that received Bowen technique sessions reported an immediate reduction in pain after treatment, but these effects were not sustained for 6 weeks after therapy ( 104755 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the small study size and the wide range of pain severity at baseline.

Stress.

Preliminary clinical research in healthy adults shows that receiving the Bowen technique over a 6-week period reduces stress and improves well-being when compared with baseline ( 100206 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

Stroke.

A small observational study in patients recovering from stroke found that receiving the Bowen technique for 30-60 minutes 13 times over 3 months is associated with improvement in gross motor function and physical health, but does not affect balance, walking, or grip strength, when compared to baseline ( 100207 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

More evidence is needed to rate Bowen technique for these uses.

The Bowen technique is a manipulative therapy that uses light pressure applied to specific locations throughout the body ( 15987 ). Practitioners move their fingers and thumbs in a rolling motion along specific muscles, joints, and connective tissues. After every couple of movements, the practitioner leaves the patient for about 2 minutes to allow the treatment to take effect, and then continues applying light pressure. Treatments usually last from 20 to 60 minutes ( 100206 , 100207 , 104755 ).

The Bowen technique is a manipulative therapy that uses light pressure applied to specific locations throughout the body. Practitioners of the Bowen technique believe that applying light pressure on specific areas on the body, such as the muscles, tendons, and connective tissues, can stimulate the body's own healing ability ( 15987 , 100207 ). However, there is no reliable scientific support for this belief. To determine the effects of the Bowen technique, some research has been conducted in healthy adults. However, results have not demonstrated a clear benefit. Some clinical research in healthy volunteers shows that receiving a 40-minute session of the Bowen technique might improve lower body postural control, but does not seem to improve pain thresholds or other postural control measures, when compared with placebo ( 100208 ). Additional clinical research in healthy volunteers shows that receiving a single 20-minute Bowen technique treatment seems to improve hamstring flexibility immediately after treatment when compared with control, but this improvement is not maintained at one week after treatment ( 100209 ). Initial clinical research in patients with chronic pain also suggests that any immediate improvements from the Bowen technique are not maintained ( 104755 ).
