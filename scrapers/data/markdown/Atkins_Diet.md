Caution

Do not confuse the Atkins diet with the modified Atkins diet, which is a type of Ketogenic Diet , or with other Diets & Dietary Modifications .

## Overview

The Atkins diet is a low-carbohydrate, high-protein diet that includes four phases. Each phase differs in its level of restriction ( 15899 , 15925 ). The Atkins diet primarily seeks to eliminate carbohydrates with a high glycemic index, resulting in initial carbohydrate intakes of 20 grams daily, although each phase allows for slight increases depending on weight loss goals ( 15906 , 15925 ). The Atkins diet has been modified for use as a type of ketogenic diet for epilepsy. This Modified Atkins Diet, which provides lower amounts of carbohydrates, is covered in the Ketogenic Diet monograph.

Likely Safe when used appropriately, short-term. The Atkins diet has been safely used in clinical trials lasting up to one year ( 11253 , 15921 , 15922 , 15924 , 88480 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Atkins diet when used long-term. Limited observational research has found that a lower carbohydrate intake (39% or less of total daily energy) is associated with a higher risk of mortality when compared with higher intakes of carbohydrates. The lowest risk of mortality is associated with a carbohydrate intake of 50% or 55% of total daily energy, which is much higher than the Atkins diet carbohydrate intake ( 105922 , 105923 , 105924 ). Additionally, carbohydrate restriction is correlated with higher mortality when the diet consists of high amounts of saturated animal fat and nutrient-poor carbohydrates ( 105922 , 105928 ). As with any diet, the consumption of varied, nutrient-rich foods should be encouraged.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

The Atkins diet is generally well tolerated.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Elevated low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol levels.

#### Cardiovascular

When used for at least 3 weeks, increases in low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol and high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol levels have occurred ( 101992 ). However, not all research agrees ( 105896 , 109935 ). Additionally, clinical research shows that following the Atkins diet for 12-24 months results in a decreased LDL: HDL ratio ( 15922 ).

Possibly Effective
Obesity.

Multiple clinical trials show that the Atkins diet can lower body weight by 2-6 kg when followed for up to 12 months. However, it may not improve weight loss more than other types of diets.

Clinical trials in obese adults show that following the Atkins diet for 6 weeks to 12 months lowers body weight by 2-6 kg when compared with baseline or control ( 11253 , 15921 , 15922 , 15924 , 88480 , 101991 , 109935 ). Some research shows that weight loss from the Atkins diet is not significantly different than other diets such as the Weight Watchers, Ornish, or Zone diets ( 15922 , 88480 , 109935 ); however, other research shows that the Atkins diet produces significantly higher average weight loss when compared with the Zone diet, but not the Ornish or LEARN diets after 12 months ( 15924 ). Some research also shows that weight loss with the Atkins diet is greater in the first 1-6 months; after 6-12 months there is no difference when compared with other diets, such as low-calorie, high-carbohydrate, low-fat diets ( 11253 , 15921 ). Preliminary clinical research in obese adults shows that following the Atkins diet for 6 months is not as effective as intra-gastric balloon insertion ( 101991 ). The National Lipid Association (NLA) Nutrition and Lifestyle Task Force concludes that low-carbohydrate diets, such as the Atkins diet, are no better than other diets for weight loss and considers carbohydrate-restricted diets a reasonable option when followed for up to 2-6 months. Because long-term carbohydrate restriction may pose adherence difficulties, the NLA recommends a diet with moderate carbohydrate intake for durations longer than 6 months ( 105896 ). However, this recommendation is based on studies evaluating various types of low-carbohydrate diets, including, but not limited to, the Atkins diet.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Diabetes.

Although there has been interest in using the Atkins diet for diabetes, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of the Atkins diet for this purpose.

Epilepsy.

Although there has been interest in using the Atkins diet for epilepsy, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of the Atkins diet for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate the Atkins diets for these uses.

#### ANTIDIABETES DRUGS
Interaction Rating Minor Be watchful with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence UNLIKELY Level of Evidence C (Expert opinion)

Theoretically, following the Atkins diet might have additive hypoglycemic effects.

Following the Atkins diet while taking antidiabetes drugs might increase the risk of hypoglycemia. In patients with diabetes, low-carbohydrate diets such as the Atkins diet can reduce the need for antidiabetes drugs; monitor blood glucose closely ( 105896 ).

#### HERBS AND SUPPLEMENTS WITH HYPOGLYCEMIC POTENTIAL

Theoretically, following the Atkins diet might have additive hypoglycemic effects.

Following the Atkins diet while taking herbs and supplements with hypoglycemic potential might increase the risk of hypoglycemia. In patients with diabetes, low-carbohydrate diets such as the Atkins diet can reduce the need for antidiabetes drugs ( 105896 ). See products with hypoglycemic activity here .

#### CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONS

Theoretically, the Atkins diet might worsen some cardiovascular conditions. When used long-term, low-carbohydrate diets, such as the Atkins diet, have been associated with a higher risk for cardiovascular complications such as atrial fibrillation and heart failure ( 105896 ).

#### HYPERLIPIDEMIA

The Atkins diet may further increase low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol levels. Clinical trials show that following the Atkins diet for at least 3 weeks might increase LDL cholesterol levels ( 15922 , 101992 , 105896 ). However, not all clinical research agrees ( 105896 , 109935 ). Also, high-density lipoprotein levels may increase, and use for up to 24 months appears to decrease the LDL:HDL ratio ( 15922 , 105896 , 109935 ).

None known.

The Atkins diet is a low-carbohydrate high-protein diet ( 15899 ). It primarily seeks to eliminate carbohydrates with a high glycemic index ( 15906 ). This goal is similar to other low-carbohydrate diets; however, the Atkins diet is considered to be more carbohydrate restrictive than other low-carbohydrate diets such as the South Beach diet. The mechanism of action related to the weight loss effects of the Atkins diet is likely multi-factorial. High glycemic index carbohydrates raise blood glucose levels quickly after consumption. It is theorized that high glycemic foods results in excessive insulin secretion resulting in fat accumulation, obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and other adverse outcomes ( 15903 ). It is also hypothesized that initial weight loss is related to natriuresis and body water loss due to ketonuria and glycogen depletion ( 105896 ). It is possible that the higher protein and fat consumption in this diet causes an increase in resting energy expenditure; however, this has not been shown in all studies ( 105896 ). The high protein content may also promote satiety and preserve muscle mass during weight loss ( 105896 ). Other clinical research in obese females shows that using the Atkins diet for 6 weeks increases gastrointestinal Actinobacteria prevalence and decreases Proteobacteria prevalence when compared with a low-fat diet, but it is unclear how this affects weight loss ( 109935 ). There continues to be significant debate and research to determine if a low glycemic index diet, such as the Atkins diet, can actually prevent adverse outcomes ( 15902 , 15903 , 15904 ). Preliminary clinical research in obese adults shows that following the Atkins diet for up to 12 months does not affect blood glucose levels, but does decrease insulin levels, when compared with other diets such as those with calorie or fat restriction ( 15992 ). A meta-analysis of clinical research in patients with type 2 diabetes shows that low-carbohydrate diets, such as the Atkins diets, may reduce glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c) levels short-term, but not long-term, when compared with other diets ( 105896 ). It also does not seem to affect blood pressure ( 15992 , 105896 ). The effect of the Atkins diet, specifically, on blood sugar control is unclear.
