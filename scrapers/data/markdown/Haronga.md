## Overview

Haronga is a small evergreen tree that grows throughout tropical areas of Africa ( 18 , 108297 ). Traditionally, the leaves and bark have been used for dyspepsia, loss of appetite, and pancreatic, liver, and gallbladder complaints.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of haronga when used orally.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

#### Dermatologic

Orally, photosensitivity is theoretically possible due to  hypericin and pseudohypericin, although the quantities present are very small ( 2 , 18 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Aging skin.

It is unclear if topical haronga is beneficial for reducing skin aging.

Preliminary clinical research shows that applying a cream containing haronga leaf extract to the face for 56 days improves under-eye wrinkles, crow's feet, smoothness, firmness, and elasticity of the skin similarly to retinol cream ( 115758 ).

More evidence is needed to rate haronga for this use.

#### Adult

All routes:

Research is limited; typical dosing is unavailable.

#### Standardization & Formulation

Traditionally, haronga extracts and tinctures have been standardized to contain 0.1% or 0.01% chrysophanic acid derivatives, respectively ( 18 ).

#### BOWEL OBSTRUCTION

Haronga is thought to stimulate the secretion of gastric fluids ( 2 , 18 ). Until more is known, avoid haronga in patients with obstruction of the bowels.

#### GALLBLADDER DISEASE

Haronga is thought to have gallbladder stimulating effects ( 2 , 18 ). Until more is known, avoid haronga in patients with gallstones, gallbladder empyema, or biliary obstruction.

#### PANCREATITIS

Haronga is thought to have pancreas stimulating effects ( 2 , 18 ). Until more is known, avoid haronga in patients with acute or chronic pancreatitis.

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the presentation or treatment of overdose with haronga.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of haronga.

#### General

The applicable parts of haronga are the bark and leaf ( 2 , 18 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

Haronga has demonstrated antibacterial and anti-amoebic effects ( 18 , 1519 ). In vitro, the bark, leaf, and root extracts of haronga have antibacterial effects against various gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria. Type of extract or extraction method and time of harvest seem to alter the potency of these effects, with the greatest effects seen with the methanolic extract of bark that was harvested in the evening ( 108297 ).

#### Dermatologic effects

In vitro, an aqueous extract of haronga leaves activates genes for, and increases expression of, retinoid-like proteins in isolated fibroblasts and photoaged skin explants ( 115758 ).

#### Digestive system effects

Haronga can have gallbladder stimulating ( 2 ), pancreas stimulating, and gastric juice secretion stimulating effects ( 18 ).
