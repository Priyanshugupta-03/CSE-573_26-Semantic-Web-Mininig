## Overview

The Schwarzbein Principle diet is based on a book by Diana Schwarzbein, MD, which was originally published in 1999 ( 15907 ). The diet promotes low-carbohydrate, high-protein intake ( 15899 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Schwarzbein Principle diet.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

None reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Obesity.

Although there has been interest in using the Schwarzbein Principle diet for weight loss, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of the Schwarzbein Principle diet for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate the Schwarzbein Principle diet for this use.

The Schwarzbein Principle program has two phases, called "healing" and "maintenance." During the healing phase, carbohydrates are severely restricted; however, leafy green vegetables are allowed during this phase. The maintenance phase allows reintroduction of a moderate amount of carbohydrates to the diet. This phase is meant to continue indefinitely ( 15907 ).

The Schwarzbein Principle diet is a low-carbohydrate, high-protein diet. It primarily seeks to eliminate foods with high glycemic index, especially processed or refined foods and focuses on high protein and "good" or healthy fats. This goal is similar to other low-carbohydrate diets. High glycemic index foods raise blood glucose levels quickly after consumption. It is theorized that high glycemic foods results in excessive insulin secretion resulting in fat accumulation, obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and other adverse outcomes ( 15903 ). However, there continues to be significant debate and research to determine if a low glycemic index diet can actually prevent these outcomes ( 15902 , 15903 , 15904 ). The program has two phases, called "healing" and "maintenance." During the first phase, the healing phase, carbohydrates are severely restricted; however, leafy green vegetables are allowed during this phase. The healing phase is temporary and is intended to "heal" metabolism. The maintenance phase allows reintroduction of a moderate amount of carbohydrates to the diet. This phase is meant to continue indefinitely.
