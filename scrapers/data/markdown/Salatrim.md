# Salatrim

## Overview

Salatrim is an acronym for short- and long-chain acyl triglyceride molecules ( 90842 ). Salatrim is composed of triglycerides containing the short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) acetic acid, propionic acid, or butyric acid, as well as some long-chain fatty acids, most of which are stearic acid ( 90842 ). Salatrim is used in food as a reduced-calorie fat replacement ( 90842 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Used for weight loss, but there is no strong evidence to support this use.
- Possibly safe when used orally in doses of 30-60 grams daily for up to one week. At the higher dose, side effects may include mild gastrointestinal symptoms.
- No known major interactions.

## History

Nabisco, the original manufacturer of salatrim, eventually sold the rights to Danisco ( 90842 , 90843 ). Danisco currently markets salatrim under the brand name Benefat ( 90843 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, salatrim is used for weight loss. In foods, salatrim is added to reduce the energy content without reducing satiety.

Possibly Safe when used orally and appropriately, short-term. Doses of 30-60 grams daily have been used with apparent safety for up to 7 days. The higher doses of 60 grams daily has been associated with increased incidence of gastrointestinal symptoms. No clinically significant adverse effects have been observed at the lower dose of 30 grams daily ( 29418 , 27578 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### Absorption

There is some conflicting evidence regarding the amount of stearic acid that is absorbed from salatrim. This has led some researchers to question the reportedly reduced caloric value of salatrim. Some evidence suggests that short-chain fatty acids in salatrim increase stearoyl density, which reduces the digestibility of stearic acid and limits its available fat content ( 91003 ). However, other evidence shows that, in humans, the apparent absorption of stearic acid from salatrim is similar to the absorption of stearic acid from natural fats ( 91002 ).

#### Excretion

In humans, some stearic acid from salatrim is excreted in the feces due to its limited digestibility ( 91002 , 91003 ).

#### General

Salatrim is mainly composed of short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs), such as acetic acid, butyric acid, and propionic acid, as well as the long-chain fatty acid stearic acid ( 90842 ).

#### Weight loss effects

Salatrim is used for weight loss. In humans, a meal in which a portion of traditional fat is replaced with salatrim increases fullness and decreases hunger and free fatty acid levels more than a traditional fat meal ( 91001 ). While the exact mechanism for this effect is unclear, it does not appear to involve gastrointestinal hormones.
