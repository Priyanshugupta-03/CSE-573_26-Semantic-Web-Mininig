# Sonoma Diet

## Overview

The Sonoma diet is a weight loss diet based loosely on the Mediterranean diet. It promotes portion control and consumption of antioxidants, fiber, healthy fats, and whole grains while enjoying flavorful meals ( 102301 ). The Mediterranean diet is based on the healthy eating and lifestyle habits of the people living in southern Italy, the Greek island of Crete, and other areas of Greece in the early 1960s. The Mediterranean diet generally includes fruits, vegetables, fish, red wine, and unsaturated "good" fats, particularly olive oil ( 97094 ). The Sonoma diet uses the Mediterranean diet as a framework, but with adaptations to include mostly American food choices and reflect the "culinary creativity" of California's Sonoma County ( 102301 ).

## History

The Sonoma Diet book was published in 2005 by Connie Gutterson ( 102301 ).

## People Use This For

The Sonoma diet is used for obesity.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Sonoma diet. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available. Following the Sonoma diet while pregnant or lactating should not pose a risk to the child as long as nutritional needs are met. However, pregnant and lactating women should avoid alcohol consumption.

#### General

A thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted; however, safety concerns are not expected as long as proper nutrition is maintained.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the Sonoma diet.

The Sonoma diet involves cooking flavorful meals with fresh, whole, nutrient-rich ingredients while controlling portion size. The Sonoma diet provides a number of endorsed recipes. If readers choose to deviate from these recipes, it is suggested that they choose ingredients from a pre-approved list. The Sonoma diet also recommends physical activity ( 102301 ). Lean meats, fresh vegetables, nuts, olive oil, and wine are the staples of the Sonoma diet. The diet also specifically promotes the consumption of ten heart-healthy, nutrient-dense whole foods that are referred to as "power foods". These foods are almonds, bell peppers, blueberries, broccoli, grapes, olive oil, spinach, strawberries, tomatoes, and whole grains ( 102301 ). The Sonoma diet takes place in three phases, which are called "Waves." Wave 1 : This 10-day period is meant to introduce simple meals that contain only whole foods while eliminating highly refined foods and negative eating habits. Highly refined foods, such as those containing refined sugar, non-whole grains, and anything containing large amounts of hydrogenated or saturated fats, are permanently eliminated from the diet during this phase. Other foods, such as fruit, are not eaten during this period but integrated back into the diet in later waves ( 102301 ). Wave 2 : During this phase, habits of long term weight loss are adopted. Individuals are encouraged to consume fruit, vegetables, fat-free yogurt, and moderate amounts of wine. Occasionally, individuals may consume honey or dark chocolate. This phase is followed until a person achieves his or her target weight loss goal ( 102301 ). Wave 3 : This long-term phase is reached once the target weight is achieved. Occasional treats such as soda, butter, and dark chocolate are permitted in Wave 3 ( 102301 ).

Many of the recommended foods in the Sonoma Diet are theorized to offer unique, distinctive health benefits. Wine and olive oil, for instance, are considered by many to be important additions to a healthy lifestyle. It is theorized that tyrosol, a constituent of wine and olive oil, may decrease levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol. It is also theorized that another constituent, caffeic acid, may have antimitogenic, anticarcinogenic, anti-inflammatory, and immunomodulatory properties ( 102301 ). Wine, particularly red wine, contains resveratrol, a chemical that has been shown in some animal and laboratory studies to exhibit antioxidant, anticancer, antiproliferative, antifungal, antiviral, and antibacterial effects ( 15144 , 70941 , 70979 , 71112 ). Olive oil also contains monounsaturated fatty acids and antioxidant agents. Some advocates suggest that antioxidants protect the body's cells from the damaging effects of oxidation by scavenging for free radicals generated during the metabolic processes of the body. Some researchers suggest that the high portion of monounsaturated fatty acids in olive oil may cause a decrease in LDL cholesterol and an increase in high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol ( 10673 ). However, the evidence for the use of the Sonoma diet is limited. The Mediterranean Diet, which acts as the basis for many of the concepts in the Sonoma diet, has been extensively researched. Refer to the Mediterranean Diet monograph for more information.
