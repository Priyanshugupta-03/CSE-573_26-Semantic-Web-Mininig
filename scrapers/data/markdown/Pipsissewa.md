## Overview

Pipsissewa is a plant. The above ground plant parts are used as medicine ( 18 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Most commonly used for urinary tract infections (UTIs) and other bladder problems, skin ulcers, and other conditions, but there is no evidence to support its use.
- Likely safe when used orally in amounts found in foods. Possibly unsafe when used orally as medicine, long-term. Side effects may include hydroquinone toxicity, such as tinnitus, vomiting, delirium, convulsions, and collapse. The safety when used topically is unclear.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, pipsissewa is used as a urinary antiseptic, diuretic, astringent, mild disinfectant, and antispasmodic. It is also used for bladder stones, diabetes, epilepsy, nervous disorders, and cancer. Topically, pipsissewa is used for treating ulcerous sores and blisters. In food and beverages, pipsissewa extracts are used as flavor components.

Likely Safe when used in amounts commonly found in foods. Pipsissewa leaf extract has Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) status in the US ( 4912 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used long-term. Pipsissewa contains hydroquinone. Long-term use might lead to hydroquinone toxicity ( 18 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of topical use of pipsissewa.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, chronic use may lead to hydroquinone toxicity. Symptoms of hydroquinone toxicity include tinnitus, vomiting, delirium, convulsions, and collapse ( 11 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, chronic use may lead to hydroquinone toxicity. Symptoms of toxicity include vomiting ( 11 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, chronic use may lead to hydroquinone toxicity. Symptoms of toxicity include delirium, convulsions, and collapse ( 11 ).

#### Ocular/Otic

Orally, chronic use may lead to hydroquinone toxicity. Symptoms of toxicity include tinnitus ( 11 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of pipsissewa are the above ground plant parts. Constituents include chimaphilin and hydroquinone glycoside ( 18 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

The constituents chimaphilin and hydroquinone glycoside may have urinary antiseptic, bacteriostatic, and astringent activity ( 7 , 11 ).
