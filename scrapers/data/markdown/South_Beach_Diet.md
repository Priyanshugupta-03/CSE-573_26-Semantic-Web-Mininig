## Overview

The South Beach diet is a low-carbohydrate diet created by the cardiologist Arthur Agatston, MD. Low-carbohydrate diets aim to promote weight loss and wellness by avoiding insulin level spikes in the body ( 15905 ). Opponents of the South Beach diet claim that it contains far too many saturated and unsaturated fats, which may result in adverse health outcomes ( 99197 ). Low-carbohydrate, high-protein diets such as the South Beach diet are extremely popular in North America. As many as 12.5% of North Americans have utilized this type of diet for weight loss ( 15899 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the South Beach diet.Limited observational research has found that reduced carbohydrate intake (39% or less of total daily energy) is associated with a higher risk of mortality when compared with higher intakes of carbohydrates. The lowest risk of mortality is associated with a carbohydrate intake of 50% to 55% of total daily energy ( 105922 , 105923 , 105924 ). However, these findings are not specific to the South Beach diet, which usually involves restricting carbohydrate intake for about 2 weeks. Additionally, some research shows that carbohydrate restriction is only correlated with higher mortality when the diet consists of high amounts of saturated animal fat, animal protein, and/or nutrient-poor carbohydrates ( 105922 , 105928 ). As with any diet, the consumption of varied, nutrient-rich foods should be encouraged.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

There is currently a limited amount of information available about the adverse effects of the South Beach diet.

#### Endocrine

One case of hyperglycemic ketoacidosis, with symptoms of nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain, occurred in a non-diabetic 30-year-old patient that had implemented the South Beach diet for 3 weeks ( 90101 ).

The South Beach diet consists of three phases. In phase 1, intake of carbohydrates and high glycemic foods is severely limited for 2 weeks. The focus is on consuming vegetables and low fat proteins such as chicken, fish, and tofu. During phase 2, a select few carbohydrates such as fruits, whole grain breads, wine, etc., can be added back into the diet, but only in moderation. In the final phase, foods of all types are allowed back into the diet, but again with moderation ( 15905 ).

The South Beach diet is a low-carbohydrate, high-protein diet ( 15899 ). It primarily seeks to eliminate foods with high glycemic index. These high glycemic index carbohydrates are referred to as "bad" carbohydrates ( 15905 , 15906 , 105896 ). This goal is similar to other low-carbohydrate diets. High glycemic index foods raise blood glucose levels quickly after consumption. It is theorized that high glycemic foods result in excessive insulin secretion resulting in fat accumulation, obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and other adverse outcomes ( 15903 , 105896 ). However, there continues to be significant debate and research to determine if a low glycemic index diet can actually prevent these outcomes ( 15902 , 15903 , 15904 , 105896 ) or whether a low glycemic index diet is preferable for weight loss or maintenance of lean body mass when compared with reduced-calorie diets ( 105896 ). The South Beach diet consists of three phases. Phase 1 lasts for 2 weeks and involves the most severe restriction of carbohydrates. During this phase, sugar, alcohol, fruit, potatoes, pasta, bread, and other high carbohydrate and high glycemic index foods are severely restricted. The theory is that this brief phase of severe carbohydrate restriction will jumpstart the weight loss process. In phase 2, some of the previously restricted high carbohydrate foods can be added back into the diet; however, this is done only in moderation. In the final phase, additional food varieties are allowed back into to the diet, but again, with moderation ( 15905 ). Although some scientists feel that following the South Beach diet may result in adverse health outcomes due to the low intake of carbohydrates and higher intake of fats, total and low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol levels were reduced in non-obese healthy adults following this diet with the goal of weight maintenance ( 99197 ). Overall, low glycemic index diets have variable effects on total and LDL cholesterol levels ( 105896 ).
