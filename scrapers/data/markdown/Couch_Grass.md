## Overview

Couch grass is an invasive weed that grows throughout northern Europe, North America, temperate Asia, New Zealand, and Australia ( 90804 , 90805 ). Traditionally, the roots and aerial parts have been used as medicine ( 90806 , 90807 , 90808 ).

## History

The roots of couch grass have been used as a medicine since ancient times. Also, the roots of couch grass have been dried and ground for bread during times of famine ( 90808 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, couch grass root is used for constipation, cough, cystitis, fever, hypertension, and kidney stones (nephrolithiasis). It is also used as a diuretic. Topically, couch grass root or leaves are used for fever.

#### General

The applicable parts of couch grass are the root and leaves. Couch grass contains 5-hydroxyindole-3-acetic acid, 5-hydroxytryptophan, and tricin ( 26199 ). Couch grass also contains triticin, a mucilaginous compound ( 26200 ). Other constituents found in couch grass include agropyrene, ascorbic acid, beta-carotene, saponins, coumarins, iridoids, tannins, phenocarboxylic acids such as chlorogenic and p-hydroxycinnamic acid, and flavonoids such as rutin, hyperoside, and baicalein ( 26201 ).

#### Anti-inflammatory effects

Preliminary animal research suggests that an ethanolic extract of couch grass may have weak anti-inflammatory effects ( 13466 ).
