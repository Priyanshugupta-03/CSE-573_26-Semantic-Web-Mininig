## Overview

Moneywort is a perennial plant that is indigenous to Europe. It has been introduced into America and Japan. The whole flowering plant is used in medicine ( 18 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Traditionally used for diarrhea, atopic dermatitis (eczema), as an expectorant, and for other conditions, but there is no strong evidence to support any use.
- There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of oral or topical use.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, moneywort is used for diarrhea, to increase salivation, and as a cough expectorant. Topically, moneywort is used for acute and chronic atopic dermatitis (eczema) and wound healing. It is also used topically as an astringent and antibacterial agent.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of moneywort.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.
