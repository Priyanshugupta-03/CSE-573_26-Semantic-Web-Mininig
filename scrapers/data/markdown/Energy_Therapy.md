## Overview

Energy therapy is a broad category of modalities and practices that utilize energy to aid a person in maintaining health and recovering from illness ( 101006 , 101007 ). Energy therapy can be divided into two categories: veritable energy therapy and putative energy therapy. Veritable energy therapies involve the use of measurable energy. These typically include vibration (such as sound waves), lasers, light, and magnetism.  Putative energy therapies involve energies that do not have standardized, reproducible measurements. These involve the belief that a subtle form of vital energy infuses all living systems and can be channeled through the hands of a practitioner ( 101006 ). Energy therapy is a broad category of modalities and practices that utilize energy for the purposes of aiding a person in maintaining health and recovering from illness ( 101006 , 101007 ). These modalities fall into two general categories:

#### Putative energy therapy

These therapies are based on the idea that the body is surrounded by an energetic field, with illness arising in part due to psychosocial or emotional issues that manifest as an imbalance in energetic flow. Putative energy therapy typically involves the channeling of healing energy through the hands of a practitioner into an individual's body in order to balance or improve the flow of energy. It is sometimes referred to as energy healing ( 101007 ).

#### Veritable energy therapy

These therapies involve the use of measurable energy with the goal of correcting perceived imbalances in bodily function. They include sound waves from music therapy, light waves from phototherapy, and electromagnetic waves from magnet therapy. Theoretically, veritable energy may have a direct, measurable effect on the disease state of a person receiving the therapy ( 101006 ).
