## Overview

Ergogenic aids, also known as performance enhancers, are substances that are intended to improve an individual's strength, speed, endurance, or speed of recovery during physical activity. Ergogenic aids are commonly marketed as dietary supplements and may be available as individual ingredients or in combination products containing multiple ingredients ( 101009 ). Refer to our list of Ergogenic Aids to find information on specific ingredients.

Many ingredients in ergogenic aid supplements fall under the legal definition of a dietary supplement in the United States and are permissible for use before and during sporting events. However, several substances that are marketed as ergogenic aids are banned by major sporting organizations and/or are considered illegal in the United States. These include chemicals such as anabolic steroids , prohormones , and ephedrine. In addition to allowing the user an unfair advantage, these chemicals may be unsafe ( 101008 , 101009 ).

Ergogenic aids represent a wide group of herbs and supplements. See specific monographs for safety information.

Ergogenic aids represent a wide group of herbs and supplements that are intended to improve exercise performance capacity or enhance training adaptations ( 101008 ). The proposed mechanism of action for performance enhancement varies by chemical. Some ergogenic aids are believed to improve athletic performance by increasing testosterone levels. In theory, increased testosterone levels improve the ability to build muscle ( 91095 ). Other supplements are proposed to work by increasing the amount of energy available for the muscles to use. The supplement itself might be intended to provide that energy or might act as a precursor for that energy source ( 46786 ). Other ergogenic aids are proposed to enhance performance by reducing muscle breakdown or stimulating the central nervous system ( 37894 , 99254 ).
