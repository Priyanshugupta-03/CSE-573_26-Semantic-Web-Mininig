## Overview

The Art of Living is a transnational meditation movement. Based on the belief that "art is the appreciation of what is," the Art of Living encourages people to appreciate life itself, and not the accessories of life. Proponents believe that to live life fully and freely is an art, requiring skill, intuition, creativity, and knowledge. The Art of Living aims to reinforce human values and bring people from varied social, economic, geographic, cultural, and religious backgrounds together through various programs ( 102371 ). The Art of Living Foundation (AOLF) was founded in 1981 by Sri Ravi Shankar as a nonprofit, humanitarian organization. The AOLF offers programs in over 150 countries around the world and is the one of the largest volunteer based non-governmental organizations in the world. The foundation's service projects and programs on yoga, meditation, and stress elimination have reached over 20 million people of various religions, cultures, and traditions ( 102371 ). The AOLF strives toward a "stress-free, violence-free society." To further this objective, the foundation works with the Economic and Social Council of the United Nations (ECOSOC) and participates in various committees and activities that are related to health, education, and development ( 102371 ).

Likely Safe when individuals participate in Art of Living practices. These practices have been implemented by millions of people worldwide with no reported safety concerns ( 102371 ).

#### General

Art of Living techniques seem to be well tolerated; no adverse effects have been reported.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Cognitive function.

Preliminary clinical research in adolescents with visual impairment or complete blindness shows that learning Prajna yoga, also known as the Art of Living Intuition Program, improves mean scores for recognition and immediate recall on the Rey Auditory Verbal Learning Test (RAVLT) when compared to baseline. Continuing to practice the technique for 20 minutes per day for 40 days improves mean scores for delayed recall on the RAVLT ( 106751 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

Quality of life.

Although there has been interest in using Art of Living techniques to improve quality of life, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of Art of Living techniques for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate Art of Living for these uses.

To further its goals, the Art of Living Foundation (AOLF) offers a variety of programs that are available for people of all ages and backgrounds. An example is the 5H program. The 5 H's are home, health, hygiene, harmony in diversity, and human values. The priority of the 5H program is for participants to aid in the social and community development of rural areas in developing countries. The 5H program also includes a volunteer program where one year is spent helping and teaching victims of war to rebuild their lives. Volunteers also coordinate service projects to rebuild homes and provide clothing ( 102371 ). Other programs offered by AOLF include Juveniles at Risk, Prison SMART Foundation, Inc., and various courses focusing on relaxation, stress relief, and community service ( 102371 ).

None known.

The first goal of AOLF is to strengthen the individual through self-development. AOLF teaches stress management skills to participants of all ages, with a focus on how to live in the present moment. The second goal of the AOLF is for individuals to take more responsibility in society and attempt to meet society's needs through various humanitarian programs ( 102371 ). Art of Living proponents believe that participating in programs focused on these goals will help people of all backgrounds to reduce stress, increase feelings of well-being and belonging, and restore human values. This also contributes to their goal of a "stress-free, violence-free society" ( 102371 ). Art of Living proponents believe that peace, joy, and love are true states of being, and that stress, negative thoughts, and negative emotions are stored as toxins in the body. This theoretically leads to disease in the body and a disturbed mental state. Happiness is believed to be a quality of consciousness which depends on attitude and understanding rather than material wealth. These teachings derive from the Hindu texts, including the Vedas. However, the Art of Living focuses more on personal experiences and downplays any dogma associated with religion ( 102371 ).
