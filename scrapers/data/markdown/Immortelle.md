## Overview

Immortelle is a protected species. It can easily be confused with sandy everlasting (Helichrysum angustifolium, synonym Helichrysum italicum), or Helichrysum stoechas ( 18 ). Avoid confusion with immortal (Asclepias asperula) ( 11 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, immortelle is used for liver disease, gallbladder disease, and biliary disorders. It is also used for dyspepsia, loss of appetite, as a diuretic, and as an antimicrobial.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of immortelle.

#### General

There is currently a limited amount of information on the adverse effects of immortelle. Orally, immortelle can cause biliary colic when used by people with gallstones ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, immortelle can cause biliary colic and sudden pain in people with gallstones ( 18 ).

#### Immunologic

Theoretically, immortelle can cause an allergic reaction in individuals sensitive to the Asteraceae/Compositae family. Members of this family include ragweed, chrysanthemums, marigolds, daisies, and many other herbs.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of immortelle.

#### Adult
Oral:

General : One cup of tea daily. The tea is prepared by steeping 3-4 grams of dried flower in 150 mL of boiling water for 10 minutes and straining. The average daily dose is 3 grams of immortelle ( 18 ).

#### General

The applicable part of immortelle is the dried flower. Immortelle flower extract contains phenolic compounds and phytosterols, including quercitrin, isoquercitrin, chlorogenic acid, dicaffeoylquinic acids, kaempferol, luteolin, beta-sitosterol, stigmasterol, and campesterol, as well as apigenin and naringenin and their glycosides ( 99053 , 105511 ).

#### Antibacterial effects

An extract of immortelle flowers has demonstrated in vitro antibacterial activity against Staphylococcus aureus, Streptococcus pneumoniae, Escherichia coli, and Moraxella catarrhalis, including some antibiotic-resistant strains ( 99053 , 105511 ).

#### Antifungal effects

An extract of immortelle flowers has demonstrated in vitro antifungal activity against Candida albicans, Aspergillus niger, and Aspergillus flavus ( 105511 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

An extract of immortelle flowers has demonstrated in vitro free radical scavenging activity ( 105511 ).
