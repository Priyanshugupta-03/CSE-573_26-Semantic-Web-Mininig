## Overview

Cubebs is an evergreen shrub that is native to Indonesia and southern Borneo ( 11 ). Its dried, unripe berries are sometimes used as an alternative to black pepper, particularly in subtropical and tropical regions around Indonesia ( 97994 , 97995 , 97996 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, cubebs is used as a diuretic, urinary antiseptic, antiflatulent, expectorant, and anti-inflammatory agent. It is also used for gonorrhea, amoebic dysentery and other protozoal infections, mycobacterial infections, diarrhea, benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), and cancer. In foods, cubebs is used as a flavoring ingredient.

Likely Safe when used orally in amounts commonly found in foods. Cubebs is a permitted food additive for use in foods in the US ( 97997).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of cubebs when used as medicine.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of cubebs.

#### ANTACIDS
Interaction Rating Minor Be watchful with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Anecdotal evidence)

Theoretically, due to reports that cubebs increases stomach acid, cubebs might decrease the effectiveness of antacids ( 19 ).

---

#### CYTOCHROME P450 3A4 (CYP3A4) SUBSTRATES
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity MODERATE Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (In vitro or animal study)

Several compounds isolated from cubebs can inhibit cytochrome P450 3A4 (CYP3A4) in vitro ( 97996 ). So far, this effect has not been reported in humans. Theoretically, cubebs might increase the effects and side effects of drugs metabolized by CYP3A4. These include lovastatin (Mevacor), clarithromycin (Biaxin), cyclosporine (Neoral, Sandimmune), diltiazem (Cardizem), estrogens, indinavir (Crixivan), triazolam (Halcion), and numerous others.

---

#### H2-BLOCKERS
Interaction Rating Minor Be watchful with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Anecdotal evidence)

Theoretically, due to reports that cubebs increases stomach acid, cubebs might decrease the effectiveness of H2-blockers ( 19 ). The H2 blockers include cimetidine (Tagamet), ranitidine (Zantac), nizatidine (Axid), and famotidine (Pepcid).

#### PROTON PUMP INHIBITORS (PPIs)
Interaction Rating Minor Be watchful with this combination.
Severity MILD Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Anecdotal evidence)

Theoretically, due to reports that cubebs increases stomach acid, cubebs might decrease the effectiveness of PPIs ( 19 ). PPIs include omeprazole (Prilosec), lansoprazole (Prevacid), rabeprazole (Aciphex), pantoprazole (Protonix), and esomeprazole (Nexium).

#### GASTROINTESTINAL (GI) CONDITIONS

Cubebs might irritate the GI tract, which could worsen infectious or inflammatory GI conditions ( 19 ).

#### KIDNEY DISEASE

Cubebs might irritate the kidneys, which could worsen existing kidney disease ( 12 ).

None known.

#### General

The applicable part of cubebs is the dried, fully grown but unripe fruit (berry). An oil is produced by steam distillation of the crushed fruits ( 11 ) The fruits contain 10-20% volatile oil, as well as resins, lignans including cubebin and hinokinin, cubebic acid, asarone, caryophyllene, thujene, pinene, terpinene, cineole, and phellandrene ( 11 , 97994 , 97995 , 97996 ).

#### Anti-cancer effects

Cubebin, a constituent isolated from cubebs seeds, has demonstrated in vitro activity against several human cancer cell lines, including colon, cervical, and nasopharyngeal cancers, lung adenocarcinoma, and myeloid leukemia. It appears to cause cell death by increasing apoptosis ( 97994 ). In vitro, an ethanolic extract of the seeds reduces growth of dihydrotestosterone-dependent prostate cancer cells, and estradiol-dependent breast cancer cells ( 97995 ).

#### Anti-inflammatory effects

In cell cultures, an ethanolic extract of cubebs seeds inhibits the activity of cyclooxygenases 1 and 2 (COX-1 and COX-2) and 5-lipoxygenase, and reduces production of interleukin-6 (IL-6), suggesting anti-inflammatory activity ( 97995 ).
