Caution

Liverwort is sometimes called trefoil, and should not be confused with Red Clover which is also sometimes called trefoil.

## Overview

Liverwort is a genus of herbaceous perennial plants native to central and northern Europe, Asia, and North America ( 59972 ). Traditionally, liverwort is used for many uses including gastric discomfort and to stimulate appetite.

Likely Unsafe when the fresh, above ground parts are used orally or topically ( 2 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of dried liverwort.

PREGNANCY: Likely Unsafe when used orally or topically ( 2 ).

LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when the fresh above ground parts are used orally or topically ( 2 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of dried liverwort while breast-feeding; avoid using.

#### General

Information on the adverse effects of liverwort is limited, but there is concern about irritant effects when used orally or topically.

#### Most Common Adverse Effects

Orally : Diarrhea, gastrointestinal irritation, kidney irritation, and urinary tract irritation. Topically : Dermatitis, itching, and skin irritation.

#### Dermatologic

Topically, fresh liverwort has been reported to cause irritation, itching, and pustule formation known as ranunculus dermatitis ( 2 ). In a case report, fresh liverwort was associated with contact hypersensitivity ( 59971 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, fresh liverwort has been reported to cause diarrhea and gastrointestinal irritation ( 18 ).

#### Immunologic

Topically, fresh liverwort has been associated with contact hypersensitivity in a case report ( 59971 ).

#### Renal

Orally, fresh liverwort has been reported to cause kidney and urinary tract irritation ( 2 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of liverwort are the fresh and dried above ground parts. Liverwort contains ranunculin, riccardin C, and riccardin F ( 18 , 59972 ).

#### Cytotoxic effects

Liverwort contains ranunculin, which hydrolyzes to the toxic, unstable compound, protoanemonin. This compound readily dimerizes to anemonin, which is suspected to have cytotoxic activity ( 4 ).

#### Neurologic effects

In animal research, protoanemonin, a derivative of the liverwort constituent ranunculin, causes central nervous system stimulation, followed by paralysis ( 2 ).
