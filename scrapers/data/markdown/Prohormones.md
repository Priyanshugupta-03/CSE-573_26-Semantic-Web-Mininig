## Overview

A prohormone is a hormone precursor. Usually, a segment of the prohormone is cleaved or altered to produce a biologically active hormone, such as testosterone or estrogen. Many prohormone products marketed for bodybuilding and athletic performance are intended to exert anabolic activity ( 91150 ). However, due to the need for conversion to the active form, some prohormones may have reduced anabolic effects when compared with anabolic steroids ( 3861 , 31250 , 47198 ). Regardless, prohormones which are intended to provide anabolic activity in the body meet the definition of an anabolic androgenic steroid according to the US Designer Anabolic Steroid Control Act (DASCA) ( 8639 , 103909 ). Refer to this list of Prohormones to find more information about individual ingredients.

Despite the fact that it is unlawful for chemicals classified as anabolic androgenic steroids, including prohormones, to be added to dietary supplements, prohormones are sometimes marketed as dietary supplements ( 31250 ). There is also evidence that some supplements contain prohormones that are not declared on the label ( 100849 ). Therefore, it is important that athletes and coaches take proper precautions to identify a high-quality product holding third-party verification, such as USP verification or NSF Certified for Sport. Anabolic steroids, including prohormones, are prohibited by the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) ( 102469 ). Certain prohormones have also been prohibited by the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) ( 13163 ).

Possibly Unsafe when used orally, transdermally, or parenterally. Prohormones can be converted to active hormones in the body and cause adverse effects such as menstrual irregularities, infertility, hair loss, and acne. Prohormones have also been associated with hepatotoxicity and increased risk of cardiovascular disease. The U.S. Food and Drug Administration and other regulatory agencies warn that dietary supplements containing prohormones are dangerous ( 91094 ).

#### HORMONE SENSITIVE CANCERS/CONDITIONS

Some prohormones are precursors to testosterone and estrogen and can increase these hormone levels ( 674 , 1905 , 3861 , 6000 , 7236 , 19563 ). Patients with hormone sensitive conditions should avoid prohormones. Some of these conditions include breast, uterine, ovarian and prostate cancer; endometriosis; and uterine fibroids.

#### LIVER DISEASE

There is some concern that prohormones might adversely affect the liver. Certain prohormones have been associated with elevated liver enzymes ( 2111 , 91095 ). Consider checking liver function tests (LFTs) in patients with liver disease that are taking prohormones.

#### PROSTATE CANCER

There is some concern that prohormones might increase the risk of developing prostate cancer. There is preliminary animal model research showing that certain prohormones such as androstenedione can stimulate human prostate tumor cell growth ( 672 ); however, it's not known if this occurs in humans.

Prohormones can be metabolized to estradiol, estrone, testosterone and other hormones. Usually, a segment of the prohormone is cleaved or altered to produce a biologically active hormone such as testosterone or estrogen.  Prohormones are often promoted for their ability to be converted into testosterone and elicit desirable effects on muscle growth. The extent of prohormone conversion to testosterone is undetermined and may be negligible in some cases. For example, in one study, healthy young males took the prohormone dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA) 50-150 mg orally which resulted in increased serum levels of DHEA and androstenedione, but levels of testosterone were not affected ( 47198 ). This may be because the conversion of the prohormones to testosterone is modulated by the endogenous testosterone already present in the male body ( 3861 ). When the testes produce sufficient testosterone, there is no need for the body to convert serum prohormones to testosterone ( 31250 ). This is why prohormones such as DHEA, androstenedione, and androstenediol seem to result in insignificant athletic performance improvement in males, especially when compared to what is seen after administration of anabolic steroids or designer anabolic steroids ( 1365 , 1905 , 6000 , 7514 , 31266 , 91150 ). Females, on the other hand, have a naturally lower level of testosterone and may experience medium or large jumps in circulating testosterone following administration of prohormones such as androstenedione ( 19563 , 19564 ). Females taking large doses of the prohormones DHEA and androstenedione over long periods of time can experience masculinization ( 31266 ).
