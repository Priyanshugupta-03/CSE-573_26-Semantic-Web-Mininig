## Overview

Ground pine is a low growing, greyish-colored annual or biennial plant that grows in stony areas in Britain and parts of Europe, including the Mediterranean ( 18 , 223 , 103608 ). It is not a member of the pine family, but it resembles a pine seedling and gives off the characteristic smell of pine when crushed ( 103608 ).

## History

Ground pine was used by herbalists in Tudor times, and has been used for centuries in the Middle East for wound healing, and as a diuretic and tonic ( 103608 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, the leaf is used for gout, rheumatoid arthritis (RA), common cold, malaria, liver disease, and scleroderma. It is also used to stimulate menstrual flow and as a diuretic and tonic. Topically, ground pine is used for wound healing, hemorrhoids, snake bites, and scorpion bites.

#### General

The applicable part of ground pine is the leaf ( 223 ). Ground pine contains ajugoside, reptoside, 8-O-acetylharpagide, harpagide, 5-O-beta-D-glucopyranosyl-harpagide, asperulosidic acid, phytoecdysterols, ajugalactone, cyasterone, makisterone, and beta-ecdysone ( 223 , 103608 ). The volatile oil contains diterpenes, caffeic acid derivatives, ethyl linoleate, germacrene D, globulol, kaurene, linalool, beta-pinene, 1-octen-3-ol, and (E)-phytol ( 18 , 103608 ).

#### Anticancer effects

In vitro, extracts of ground pine and its essential oil have activity against human colon cancer, malignant melanoma, and breast adenocarcinoma cells ( 103608 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

In vitro, extracts of ground pine and its essential oil have antioxidant activity, including free radical scavenging effects ( 103608 ).
