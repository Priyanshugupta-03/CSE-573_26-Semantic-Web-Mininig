## Overview

Naturopathy is a distinct system of primary health care that uses its own methods to diagnose, treat, and prevent illness. The goal of naturopathic medicine is to restore and maintain optimum health by emphasizing nature's inherent self-healing process through a focus on holistic approaches and preventive care ( 102104 ). Naturopathic medicine involves many treatment methods, including nutrition, herbs and supplements, bodywork, physical activity, stress reduction, and acupuncture ( 102103 ). Naturopathy originated in Europe, but has gained popularity in North America, Australia, India, and other parts of the world ( 15919 , 102104 ). A naturopathic doctor (ND) is a primary healthcare practitioner who practices naturopathy. To become licensed in North America, a naturopathic physician attends a four-year graduate level naturopathic medical school. Naturopathic physicians are required to take professional board exams to be licensed by a state or jurisdiction as a primary care physician. Many American jurisdictions do not recognize NDs as practitioners and do not license these professionals to practice as primary care physicians ( 15919 , 102104 ). Do not confuse certified naturopathic physicians with non-certified holistic practitioners.

Naturopathy originated as a traditional system of healing based on philosophical principles similar to those found in Ayurvedic medicine. It places the focus on holistic care, in which a person must be treated physically, mentally, socially, and spiritually to achieve health. In naturopathy, the primary cause of disease, barring accidental injury, is thought to be due to the violation of natural laws. This can result in reduced vitality, abnormal blood composition, and accumulation of waste and poisons. Thus, naturopathy often emphasizes preventive medicine in addition to holistic care, and this often involves the use of nutritional interventions ( 15919 , 102103 ). Diagnostic approaches in naturopathy can include conventional laboratory tests and physical assessment as well as alternative approaches such as pulse and tongue diagnosis and other methods. In general, naturopathic practitioners believe in the importance of the healing power of nature and that therapies should enhance that healing power. These therapies can include herbs and supplements, homeopathic products, bodywork, physical activity, nutritional therapy, psychological therapies, and many others ( 15919 , 101204 ).
