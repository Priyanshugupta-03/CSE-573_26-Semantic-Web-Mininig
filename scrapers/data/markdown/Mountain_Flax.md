## Overview

Mountain flax is a plant native to Europe, Northern Africa, Western Asia, and the Caucasus ( 102536 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Sometimes used for constipation and as an emetic, but there is insufficient reliable evidence to rate its effectiveness for these uses.
- Possibly unsafe when used orally. Side effects may include vomiting, diarrhea, and inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, mountain flax is used for constipation and as an emetic.

Possibly Unsafe when used orally, particularly with long-term use ( 18 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Unsafe when used orally due to possible emetic effects ( 18 ).

#### General

Orally, mountain flax can cause vomiting, gastrointestinal tract inflammation, and diarrhea ( 18 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of mountain flax are the above ground flowering parts. Mountain flax contains the lignan achromatin, tannins, and a volatile oil ( 18 ).

#### Laxative effects

Mountain flax is thought to have laxative effects at 0.5 grams ( 18 ). The mechanism of this effect is unclear.
