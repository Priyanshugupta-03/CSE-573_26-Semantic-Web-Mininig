## Overview

Wood sage is an herb that smells faintly of leeks when being dried. It is native to western and central Europe, including the Mediterranean region. It has traditionally been used for gastrointestinal disorders, respiratory tract infections, wound healing, and for its purported expectorant and spasmolytic effects ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of wood sage.

#### General

The applicable parts of wood sage are the above ground parts. It contains flavonoids, a volatile oil, diterpenes, and iridoid monoterpenes ( 18 ).
