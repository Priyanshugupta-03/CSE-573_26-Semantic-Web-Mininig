# SlimFast Diet
Other Common Names Dieta SlimFast, Régime SlimFast, Slim-Fast Diet
|

---

## Overview

The SlimFast diet is an eating plan that revolves around the consumption of SlimFast products. SlimFast products include shakes and nutrition bars. These products are known as "meal replacements," because they provide a fair amount of nutritional value while remaining low in calories. SlimFast markets a number of product varieties to accommodate dietary constraints and preferences ( 103146 , 103147 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Some evidence for use in obesity and general weight loss. Also used for diabetes, but there is no strong evidence to support this use.
- Likely safe when consumed appropriately. No adverse effects have been reported.

## People Use This For

The SlimFast diet has been used for obesity, general weight loss, and diabetes.

Likely Safe when consumed as directed. SlimFast meal replacement products have been used without reports of safety concerns in multiple clinical trials ( 103142 , 103143 , 103144 , 103145 , 103146 , 103147 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the SlimFast diet in pregnancy and lactation. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

#### General

SlimFast meal replacement products are well-tolerated. No adverse effects have been reported in clinical studies ( 103142 , 103143 , 103144 , 103145 , 103146 , 103147 ).

Possibly Effective
Obesity.

SlimFast meal replacement products have been studied for obesity in adolescents and adults, often in comparison with other branded or popular diets. Most clinical research shows that following the SlimFast diet results in weight loss, but it is unclear if it is more effective than other dieting programs ( 103142 , 103143 , 103144 , 103145 , 103146 , 103147 ).

Clinical research in overweight or obese adults in Australia shows that consuming 2 SlimFast meal replacement products daily for 6 months results in similar weight loss as following conventional weight loss guidance ( 103146 ). Similarly, clinical research in overweight or obese adults in the United Kingdom shows that consuming 2 SlimFast meal replacement products daily for 6 months results in similar weight loss as the Weight Watchers, Atkins diet, and 'Eat Yourself Slim' diet programs ( 103145 ). However, the SlimFast group was the only group that did not experience improvements in either low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol or triglyceride levels ( 103144 ). Clinical research in obese adolescents shows that following a diet involving the consumption of 3 SlimFast meal replacement products and one pre-packaged meal daily results in a greater reduction in body mass index (BMI) at 4 months when compared with an isocaloric conventional diet. However, this reduction was not maintained at 12 months ( 103143 ). A small clinical study in obese adults with systolic heart failure and metabolic syndrome shows that consuming two SlimFast meal replacement products daily in conjunction with a walking exercise program for 3 months does not improve weight loss when compared with standard medical therapy alone ( 103142 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Diabetes.

Preliminary clinical research in overweight adults with type 2 diabetes shows that consuming SlimFast meal replacement beverages twice daily for 12 weeks results in greater weight loss than those consuming an exchange diet plan based on recommendations from the American Diabetic Association (ADA). Neither Original SlimFast or Sugar-Free SlimFast products affected postprandial glucose levels or increased the risk for hyperglycemia in these patients ( 103147 ).

More evidence is needed to rate the SlimFast diet for this use.

The foundation of the SlimFast diet is the use of its products as meal replacements. Bars, shakes made from powders, and pre-mixed drinks are some of the products available to consume as meal replacements ( 103145 , 103146 ). Traditionally, SlimFast literature recommends the use of two meal replacement products each day over an extended period of time, followed by a low-fat dinner. For these meals, the SlimFast diet encourages a regimen based on the "Sensible Meal Guide." In general, according to this guide, half of a plate should be filled with vegetables. This is usually about half a cup of cooked vegetables, or one cup of uncooked vegetables. A quarter of the plate should be filled with lean protein, such as lean beef, pork, poultry, fish, eggs, low-fat dairy products, or tofu. The final quarter of the plate should be comprised of starches, such as potatoes, corn, pasta, noodles, rice, or whole wheat bread. A side dish of one cup of a tossed salad or a dish of fruit along with a calorie-free beverage is also acceptable ( 103145 , 103146 ). However, SlimFast also promotes the use of its products in a more flexible manner, simply reinforcing that the products always be used to replace, not augment, a meal ( 103147 ). SlimFast products are available in a number of varieties. The Original SlimFast products are for those without special dietary needs. Many of the other varieties are marketed to people with specific dietary constraints or preferences, such as those seeking low-carbohydrate, lactose-free, or sugar-free options ( 103145 , 103146 , 103147 ).

None known.

The SlimFast diet is based around the use of its meal replacement products. These products are intended to provide adequate nutrition while remaining low in calories ( 103146 , 103147 ). Additionally, the SlimFast corporation claims that its products help retain a sensation of fullness for up to four hours. This sensation of fullness is purported to prevent the people who consume these products from eating more due to hunger. However, this claim has not been evaluated in clinical research.
