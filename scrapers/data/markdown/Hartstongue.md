Caution

Do not confuse hartstongue with other ferns, such as English Adder's Tongue , Maidenhair Fern , Male Fern , Ostrich Fern , or Polypodium leucotomos .

## Overview

Hartstongue is a fern that is native to most of Europe, North America, northern Africa, and eastern Asia. The frond has traditionally been used as medicine for its purported diuretic and mild laxative effects ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of hartstongue.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

None reported; however, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Dyspepsia.

Although there has been interest in using oral hartstongue for dyspepsia, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of hartstongue for this purpose.

Urinary tract infections (UTIs).

Although there has been interest in using oral hartstongue for UTIs, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of hartstongue for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate hartstongue for these uses.

#### General

The applicable parts of hartstongue are the above ground parts. Hartstongue contains tannins, mucilage, flavonoids (kaempferol-7-rhamnoside-3 coffeoyl-7-diglucoside), thiaminase, and sugars (sucrose and invert sugar) ( 18 ).
