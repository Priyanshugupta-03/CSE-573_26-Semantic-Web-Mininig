Caution

Do not confuse 1,3-DMBA with 1,3-DMAA , 1,4-DMAA , or Dimethylhexylamine (DMHA) .

## Overview

1,3-DMBA is a chemical ( 26231 , 100692 ). Some supplements claim that 1,3-DMBA is extracted from pouchong tea or Coreopsis tinctoria. However, the amount of 1,3-DMBA found in products marketed for athletic performance and weight loss is much greater than the quantity that is found in nature. This has raised concerns that manufacturers are using synthetic 1,3-DMBA rather than obtaining it from natural sources ( 26231 ).

In 2015, the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) issued an alert stating that supplement products containing 1,3-DMBA are considered adulterated and cannot be lawfully marketed or sold ( 101462 ). 1,3-DMBA is also included on the World Anti-Doping Agency's list of prohibited stimulants ( 100691 ).

Likely Unsafe when used orally. 1,3-DMBA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects, which have been demonstrated in animal studies ( 24150 , 26231 , 100692 ). Clinical evidence on the safety of 1,3-DMBA is lacking; however, it has a similar structure to 1,3-DMAA, a synthetic stimulant that has been linked to serious adverse events, including lactic acidosis, myocardial infarction, stroke, and death ( 17599 , 17660 , 17662 , 17663 , 17904 , 94361 , 94369 , 94374 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally. 1,3-DMBA is thought to have stimulant and vasoconstrictive effects ( 24150 , 26231 , 100692 ) which might increase the risk of serious adverse effects.

#### General

There is currently a limited amount of information available about the adverse effects of 1,3-DMBA. However, the closely related chemical 1,3-DMAA has been linked to serious adverse effects in humans, including lactic acidosis, myocardial infarction, stroke, and death.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Athletic performance.

Although there has been interest in using oral 1,3-DMBA to improve athletic performance, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of 1,3-DMBA for this purpose.

Cognitive function.

Although there has been interest in using oral 1,3-DMBA to improve cognitive function, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of 1,3-DMBA for this purpose.

Obesity.

Although there has been interest in using oral 1,3-DMBA for weight loss, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of 1,3-DMBA for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate 1,3-DMBA for these uses.

#### Adult
Oral:

Research is limited; typical dosing in unavailable. Theoretically, combining 1,3-DMBA with other stimulants, such as caffeine-containing foods, might increase the risk of cardiovascular toxicity. Animal research shows that 1,3-DMBA has stimulant effects ( 24150 ).

#### Standardization & Formulation

Several dietary supplements marketed for athletic performance and weight loss have been found to contain 1,3-DMBA despite labels suggesting the presence of other ingredients. Quantities of 1,3-DMBA ranged from 13-260 mg per serving, which would provide 26-320 mg per day when following the label directions ( 26231 ).

#### CARDIAC CONDITIONS

Theoretically, 1,3-DMBA might increase heart rate and worsen tachyarrhythmias.1,3-DMBA has vasopressor activity in animals, increasing blood pressure and heart rate ( 24150 ).

#### HYPERTENSION

Theoretically, 1,3-DMBA might exacerbate hypertension.1,3-DMBA has vasopressor activity in animals, increasing blood pressure and heart rate ( 24150 ).

#### NARROW-ANGLE GLAUCOMA

Theoretically, 1,3-DMBA might exacerbate narrow-angle glaucoma.1,3-DMBA has vasopressor activity in animals, increasing blood pressure and heart rate ( 24150 ).

#### PERIOPERATIVE

Theoretically, 1,3-DMBA might interfere with surgical procedures by increasing blood pressure and heart rate. 1,3-DMBA has vasopressor activity in animals ( 24150 ). Tell patients to discontinue 1,3-DMBA at least 2 weeks before elective surgical procedures.

None known.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the presentation or treatment of overdose with 1,3-DMBA.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of 1,3-DMBA.

#### General

1,3-DMBA is a chemical. It is an aliphatic amine stimulant ( 26231 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

1,3-DMBA has vasopressor activity in animals, increasing blood pressure and heart rate. It has about 84% of the potency of epinephrine ( 26231 ). Most animals experienced tachyphylaxis after receiving one intravenous dose of 1,3-DMBA ( 24150 ).
