# Volumetrics Diet
Other Common Names Dieta volumétrica, Régime volumétrique, Ultimate Volumetrics Diet
|

---

## Overview

The Volumetrics diet is a low-calorie, high-bulk, and low-fat diet. The diet is intended to allow for the consumption of large portions of foods that are low in calories. Regular exercise is also incorporated into the lifestyle modifications advocated by the Volumetrics diet ( 103152 , 103153 ). The diet was developed by Barbara Rolls, PhD. It is based on the idea that many people feel hungry when they diet, leading to the consumption of extra calories. In order to combat this, the Volumetrics diet promotes foods that increase satiety while keeping calorie intake low, such as foods with high water content. The diet permits the consumption of unlimited amounts of food provided that each serving averages out to fewer than 400 calories per pound of food ( 103152 , 103153 ).

## History

The Volumetrics Diet book, written by Barbara Rolls, PhD, was first published in 2000. Since then, the book has undergone several revisions, most recently involving the publication of the Ultimate Volumetrics Diet ( 103152 , 103152 ).

## People Use This For

The Volumetrics diet has been used for obesity and weight loss.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Volumetrics diet. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Volumetrics diet in pregnancy and lactation. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the Volumetrics diet.

The Volumetrics diet does not restrict portion size and instead focuses on increasing satiety. Food selection is geared towards foods that are high in bulk with a low calorie density. Foods that are low-bulk and high-calorie, such as crackers, chips, cookies, chocolate, candies, nuts, butters, and oils, are consumed sparingly. High-bulk, low-calorie foods, on the other hand, such as non-starchy vegetables, nonfat dairy products, and soup broths, are consumed often ( 103152 , 103153 ). The Volumetrics diet often requires people to cook or create low-fat alternatives to their favorite high-fat, high-protein, and high-carbohydrate foods. Many of these alternatives involve simple changes in food preparation. One example is the use of baking or sautéing instead of frying. Another is the use of skim milk, egg whites, yogurt, and applesauce as substitutes for vegetable oil, butter, margarine, eggs, and cream. Fish and lean meats are encouraged, while pork and red meat are avoided. The Volumetrics book contains charts that detail the fiber content and energy density of many foods and recipes ( 103152 , 103153 ). Energy-dense foods such as alcoholic beverages, sweets, and fats are not forbidden. However, the Volumetrics diet encourages moderation by restricting the frequency and portion size of these foods ( 103152 , 103153 ). Exercise is also encouraged with the Volumetrics diet. Adherents are asked to record what they eat and their level of physical activity. The records indicate the individual's level of physical activity and caloric intake at the start of the diet, which serves as a "baseline" reference point to track progress while on the diet ( 103152 , 103153 ).

The foundation of the Volumetrics diet is to increase satiety while reducing calorie consumption. The Volumetrics diet focuses on calorie reduction as a whole instead of focusing on altering the consumption of specific macronutrients ( 103151 ). The Volumetrics diet is based on the concept that people feel full after eating about the same amount of food every day, regardless of the fat, carbohydrate, or calorie content of that food. More specifically, the Volumetrics diet is based on the concept of energy density. The amount of calories in a specified amount of food, usually a serving, is its energy density. Foods with high energy densities do not cause a sensation of fullness quickly and also tend to contain large amounts of fat. To promote weight loss and limit additional calorie intake, the Volumetrics diet encourages dieters to eat low-calorie and high-fiber foods until feeling full ( 103150 , 103151 ).
