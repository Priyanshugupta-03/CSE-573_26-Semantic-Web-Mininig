## Overview

Kirlian photography is a diagnostic technique that uses a high-voltage, high-frequency electric field passed through a metal plate onto a piece of photographic paper. It is usually used to produce images of a person's body parts. Proponents believe that it provides a way of viewing auras, or unseen patterns of energy ( 28403 ). Every organism or substance is claimed to radiate and absorb energy differently, producing unique geometrical frequency and radiation characteristics. The colored image produced with Kirlian photography is believed to be the patient's aura, and to represent the body's energy fields or life forces. The photograph may be used to make a diagnosis ( 28401 ).

## History

Kirlian photography was developed by a Russian electrician and physicist, Semyon Kirlian, and his wife in the 1950s based on the theories and works of Rudolf Steiener, Nikola Tesla, and Christoph Lichtenberg ( 28403 ). Kirlian and his wife employed a high-frequency oscillator and took photographs with electric current and photographic film. He made controversial claims regarding the proof of supernatural auras resembling that of a colorful halo.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Disease diagnosis.

One small clinical study suggests that Kirlian photography has a low reliability in the diagnosis of kidney, bladder, spleen, pancreas, and stomach diseases ( 28402 ). Another small study suggests that Kirlian photography may have value as a supplementary diagnostic tool, although no reliability assessments were conducted ( 28404 ).

More evidence is needed to rate Kirlian photography for this use.

Kirlian photography is a means of taking pictures of the nonmaterial world without a photographic lens or camera. The equipment that produces the Kirlian photographic images uses no light. Instead, the radiation spark discharged from the photographed object is used. The object or body part being photographed is brought into direct contact with a specially constructed photographic plate and briefly exposed to a precisely controlled high frequency electrical charge. As this high frequency charge resonates with the object being photographed, a "corona discharge" emanation or "energy field" consisting of electrons moves from the photographed object to the photographic plate. Depending upon the type of film used, different colors or shadings appear in a corona surrounding the object ( 28403 ).

The theory and reliability of Kirlian photography are debatable ( 28409 ). In theory, the corona image on a Kirlian photograph is caused by the moisture of the subject's skin. During exposure, moisture is transferred from the subject to the photographic film surface, resulting in a picture which correlates to the electric charge of the subject's skin. There is also a theory suggesting that Kirlian photography may map circulation and energy in brain vessels and help detect problems with them early on ( 28406 ). One study claims that Kirlian photography shows bio-electrographic patterns of brain activation in patients in an altered state of consciousness induced by systematic mental training ( 28407 ). Another study claims that Kirlian photographs of fingers and toes from 8 patients with different disease states confirm the existence of individual electromagnetic fields ( 28408 ). Kirlian photography has also been utilized to assess the "memory" of water in homeopathic dilutions. Pictures of homeopathic solutions, distilled water, and solutions without the homeopathic potentiation process were compared by a computerized method and are stated to have shown different corona discharge activity depending on the level of dilution ( 28410 ). Despite the many claims attributed to Kirlian photography, high quality research supporting these claims is lacking.
