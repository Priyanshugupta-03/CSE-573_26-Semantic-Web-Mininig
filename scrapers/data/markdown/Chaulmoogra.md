# Chaulmoogra
Scientific Name Hydnocarpus anthelminthicus, Hydnocarpus kurzii (synonym: Taraktogenos kurzii), Hydnocarpus species. Family: Flacourtiaceae. Family Flacourtiaceae Other Common Names Chaulmugra, Da Feng Zi, Gynocardia Oil, Hydnocarp, Hydnocarpus, Oleum Chaulmoograe

## Overview

Chaulmoogra seeds provided the elemental materials for synthesizing the first antileprostatic agents ( 6002 ).

## People Use This For

Topically, chaulmoogra is used for skin disorders, psoriasis, and eczema. Parenterally, chaulmoogra is used for leprosy.

Likely Unsafe when used orally. The seeds are considered very toxic due to their cyanogenic glycoside content ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of chaulmoogra for its other uses.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, ingestion of the seed can cause cough, dyspnea, laryngospasms, nephrotoxicity, visual disorders, head and muscle pain, and central paralysis ( 18 ). Topically, use can cause skin irritation ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available to rate the effectiveness of chaulmoogra.

#### Adult

ORAL: No typical dosage. TOPICAL: People typically use chaulmoogra as a powder, oil, emulsion, or in ointments ( 18 ). PARENTERAL: No typical dosage.

The applicable part of chaulmoogra is the seed. Chaulmoogra is thought to have sedative, antipyretic, and skin effects ( 18 ). In mice, intraperitoneal and subcutaneous administration of chaulmoogra fatty acids demonstrated antimicrobial activity against Mycobacterium leprae ( 3826 ). The cyanogenic glycoside content of the seeds renders them extremely poisonous ( 18 ).
