## Overview

Bioenergetics was developed by Alexander Lowen, MD, a Swedish physician, in the 1950s.It is based on the theory that a person's repressed emotions result in muscle tension, rigidity, personality disorders, and psychosomatic disease. Practitioners refer to this rigidity as "armoring." Bioenergetics uses a variety of techniques, incorporated with psychoanalysis, including breathing exercises, therapeutic massage, body movements and posturing, and physical exercises ( 15882 , 15883 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of bioenergetics.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

Bioenergetics is based on the theory that a person's repressed emotions result in muscle tension, rigidity, personality disorders, and psychosomatic disease. Practitioners refer to this rigidity as "armoring." Bioenergetics uses a variety of techniques, incorporated with psychoanalysis, including breathing exercises, therapeutic massage, body movements and posturing, and physical exercises ( 15883 ). These techniques are used to unblock repressed emotions and negative energies. In some cases, physical exercises may include kicking and yelling, beating objects, and crying. It's thought that these exercises help patients release negative emotional energies. Bioenergetics practitioners use these methods in individual therapy sessions and in group therapy sessions. There is no reliable scientific evidence to support this therapeutic approach.
