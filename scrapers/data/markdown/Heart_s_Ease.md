## Overview

Heart's ease is a type of plant. It is native to or naturalized in large parts of Europe, the Middle East, and Central Asia. It is traditionally used as medicine ( 54705 , 100059 ).

## History

Heart's ease has a long history of use in Europe for inflammatory lung conditions and skin conditions. It is registered in the German commission E Monograph and the Pharmacopoeia of Europe ( 100059 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, heart's ease is used for constipation, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and as a demulcent for respiratory disorders such as pharyngitis and cough. Topically, heart's ease is used for atopic dermatitis (eczema), psoriasis, seborrheic dermatitis, seborrheic keratosis, warts, mild skin inflammation, acne, exanthema (skin eruption), impetigo, and itching of the female external genitalia.

Possibly Safe when used orally and appropriately ( 12 ). ...when used topically and appropriately ( 12 ). A lotion containing 5% heart's ease extract has been safely used in a study lasting up to 4 weeks ( 67372 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Orally, heart's ease seems to be generally well tolerated ( 12 ). It has caused hemolysis in a nine-month-old infant with glucose-6-phosphate-dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency ( 11324 ).

#### Hematologic

Orally, heart's ease boiled extract has caused hemolysis in a nine-month-old infant with glucose-6-phosphate-dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency. The methyl ester of salicylic acid, a constituent of heart's ease, is hypothesized to be responsible for this effect ( 11324 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Atopic dermatitis (eczema).

Preliminary clinical research shows that applying an ointment containing 5% each of the extracts of heart's ease, gotu kola, and Oregon grape twice daily for 4 weeks does not improve eczema when compared with a base cream. However, patients with eczema on skin exposed to cold weather might experience some improvement ( 67372 ).

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Preliminary clinical research in men aged 40-70 years with a history of smoking and stage 2 COPD shows that taking a combination of heart's ease 165 mg, calendula flowers 165 mg, and elderberry 165 mg (Inflaminat, INAT-Farma), 3 capsules daily for 6 months, modestly improves the score on the 5-point breathlessness, cough, and sputum scale (BCSS) and the mean forced expiratory volume in 1 second (FEV1), compared with no changes in the placebo group. However, the number of COPD exacerbations per month does not seem to be affected ( 103629 ). It is unclear if these effects are due to heart's ease, other ingredients, or the combination.

More evidence is needed to rate heart's ease for these uses.

#### GLUCOSE-PHOSPHATE DEHYDROGENASE (G6PD) DEFICIENCY

There is one case report of hemolysis in an infant with G6PD deficiency who consumed heart's ease boiled extract. It is hypothesized that the methyl ester of salicylic acid, a constituent of heart's ease, was responsible for this effect ( 11324 ). Until more known, patients with G6PD deficiency should avoid heart's ease.

#### General

The applicable parts of heart's ease are the above ground parts ( 18 ). Constituents in heart's ease include flavonoids, phenylcarbonic acids, coumarins, salicylic acid derivatives, and macrocyclic peptides known as cyclotides ( 54707 , 100059 ).

#### Anti-inflammatory effects

Heart's ease is traditionally used for inflammatory lung and skin conditions. In vitro, heart's ease extract inhibited the secretion of various inflammatory cytokines, resulting in inhibition of the proliferation of activated lymphocytes. The bioactive constituents appeared to be the cyclotides ( 100059 ).

#### Cardiovascular effects

There is interest in using heart's ease as a cardioprotective agent. In vitro and animal research shows that heart's ease extracts have vasorelaxant and hypotensive effects, although the precise mechanism of action is unclear ( 104155 ).

#### Immunologic effects

In vitro, heart's ease extract inhibits the secretion of various inflammatory cytokines. This results in inhibition of the proliferation of activated lymphocytes, although it does not affect their activation. The bioactive constituents appear to be the cyclotides ( 100059 ).
