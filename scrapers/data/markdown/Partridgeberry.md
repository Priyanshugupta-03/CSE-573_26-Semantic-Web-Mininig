## Overview

## People Use This For

Orally, partridgeberry is used for amenorrhea, anxiety, diarrhea, dysmenorrhea, edema, menorrhagia, fibrocystic breast disease, postpartum depression, varicose veins, parturition, improving lactation, insomnia, congestive heart failure (CHF), renal failure, liver failure, chronic dysentery, preventing miscarriage, as an emmenagogue, as an abortifacient, as an astringent, and to reduce mucous membrane and leukorrheal discharges. Topically, partridgeberry is used for treating sore nipples.

Possibly Safe when used orally and appropriately ( 90893 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of partridgeberry when used topically.

PREGNANCY: POSSIBLY UNSAFE when used orally. Partridgeberry may have uterine stimulant effects, which could adversely affect pregnancy.  Although it has been used traditionally to facilitate delivery or prevent miscarriage, abortifacient properties have been reported ( 90893 , 116932 , 116933 , 116934 ).

#### General

The applicable parts of partridgeberry are the above ground parts. Partridgeberry is reported to contain resin, wax, mucilages, dextrin, saponins, alkaloids, glycosides, and tannins ( 410 ).
