## Overview

Amino acids are organic compounds that form the building blocks for proteins. They are also a key energy source and are critical to various metabolic processes in the human body ( 100104 ). Amino acid therapy involves the use of amino acids for a variety of therapeutic purposes ( 100103 , 100104 ). Amino acids are organic compounds that act as the building blocks for proteins and fulfill other crucial functions in the human body. Amino acid therapy is intended to ensure that the necessary quantity of amino acids are present for appropriate cell functioning. Most of the amino acids used by the human body are also synthesized by the body. However, some amino acids can only be acquired through diet. These amino acids, referred to as essential amino acids, include histidine, isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, phenylalanine, threonine, tryptophan, and valine. A deficiency of essential amino acids typically involves overall protein deficiency accompanied by a deficiency of dietary energy and other nutrients due to malnutrition. In cases of malnutrition or insufficient oral intake, amino acids may be supplemented through parenteral nutrition therapy ( 94096 ). Amino acid disorders, or aminoacidopathies, are caused by inborn errors of metabolism. These are a group of rare and diverse disorders that are caused by the deficiency of an enzyme or transporter that is involved in amino acid metabolism. In these cases, amino acids, their precursors, or metabolites can build up, causing adverse effects and complications. The most widely recognized aminoacidopathy is phenylketonuria (PKU). In cases of diagnosed aminoacidopathy, dietary management, often involving protein restriction and specific amino acid supplementation, may be used ( 101003 , 101004 ). Amino acid therapy has also been considered for the treatment of perceived amino acid deficiencies that are believed to be the cause of various illnesses and developmental disturbances. It is assumed that supplementing with amino acids will correct these deficiencies and resolve various symptoms and illnesses ( 101005 ). However, there is no scientific evidence to support this theory.
