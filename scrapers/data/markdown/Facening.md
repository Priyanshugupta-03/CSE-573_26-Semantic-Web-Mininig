# Facening
Other Common Names Ejercicios faciales (Facening), Face Shaping, Face Training, Face Yoga, Facial Exercise, Facial Resistance Training, Facial Training, Gymnastique faciale
|

---

## Overview

Facening is a practice that uses specific facial exercises to relax and train the facial muscles. This is typically done for the purpose of cosmetic improvement ( 102921 , 102922 ). Some advocates promote this therapy as a natural alternative to plastic surgery or Botox injections, although it has also been studied as an adjunct to Botox injections ( 102922 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Used to improve the appearance of aging or wrinkled skin, but there is no strong evidence to support these uses.
- Likely safe when practiced appropriately.

## People Use This For

Facening is used for hemifacial spasm and to improve the appearance of aging skin and wrinkled skin.

Likely Safe when practiced appropriately, as directed. Facening has been performed with apparent safety for up to 20 weeks in clinical trials ( 102921 , 102922 , 106673 ). Additionally, there is no reason to expect safety issues when performed appropriately.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported in clinical trials ( 102921 , 102922 ). Additionally, there is no reason to expect safety issues when practiced appropriately.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Aging skin.

A small clinical study in healthy women shows that performing facening exercises for 4 hours after receiving botulinum toxin injections into the forehead and glabella rhytids improves the appearance of glabellar and forehead wrinkles by day 3 when compared with women who refrained from facial movement during this 4-hour period ( 102922 ).

Hemifacial spasm.

Preliminary clinical research in adults with hemifacial spasm (HFS) shows that practicing video-guided facening exercises for 7 minutes once after receiving an onabotulinumtoxin-A treatment improves HFS scores by 10% to 12% when compared with onabotulinumtoxin-A treatment alone. The facening exercise included 8 steps repeated for 5 cycles ( 106673 ).

Wrinkled skin.

A small clinical study in healthy women aged 40-65 years with photodamaged skin shows that performing facening exercises for 30 minutes daily for 8 weeks, then every other day for another 12 weeks, improves upper and lower cheek fullness when compared with baseline. It also reduced the estimated age of the participants by an average of 2 years, based on subjective rater review ( 102921 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a comparator group.

More evidence is needed to rate facening for these uses.

Facening involves various facial exercises that are intended to relax and train the muscles in the face. Facening exercises may include movements such as closing and opening each eye, raising eyebrows, puckering the lips, opening the eyes wide, and tilting the chin while lifting the tongue. Many instructors ask clients to practice in front of mirrors. This is believed to help the client see whether the exercises are being done properly ( 102921 , 102922 ). Facening classes may be offered online or at some gyms and spas. Certain facening approaches, such as Revita-Yoga, are yoga-based. Instructors recommend practicing for up to 15 minutes per session to avoid overtiring the muscles. Sessions can include anywhere from 5 to 20 different facial exercises ( 102921 , 102922 ).

None known.

None known.

Facening uses individualized sets of facial exercises in an effort to relax and train the facial muscles and improve circulation. This is purported to reduce wrinkles and the appearance of aging, although there is limited evidence supporting this practice ( 102921 , 102922 ).
