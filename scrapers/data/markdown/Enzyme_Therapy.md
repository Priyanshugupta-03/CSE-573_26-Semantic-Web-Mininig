## Overview

Enzymes are macromolecules that catalyze reactions. They are a crucial component of various bodily processes. Enzyme therapy involves the use of enzymes for therapeutic purposes, such as degrading potentially harmful or unwanted substances ( 95498 , 95549 ). Refer to this list of Enzymes to find more information on specific molecules.

## People Use This For

Enzyme therapy has been used for bezoars, burns, cancers, celiac disease, cystic fibrosis, disaccharidase deficiencies, pancreatic insufficiency, Fabry disease, Gaucher disease, herpes zoster (shingles), lactose or sucrose intolerance, lymphatic edema, phenylketonuria (PKU), rheumatological diseases, rhinosinusitis, and wound debridement.

#### General

Enzymes are macromolecules that catalyze reactions, often acting as crucial components in a variety of bodily processes ( 95498 , 95549 ).

#### Deficiency effects

Enzyme therapy is most commonly used in clinical practice to address specific enzyme deficiencies. For instance, sucrose intolerance would be treated with sacrosidase, which degrades sucrose, and lactose intolerance would be treated with lactase, which degrades lactose ( 95498 ). Some rare deficiency disorders can be treated with prescription enzyme therapies. Anderson-Fabry disease and Gaucher disease are examples of conditions which occur due to a deficiency of a specific enzyme, and can be effectively treated with prescription enzyme replacement therapy ( 95497 , 95500 ). Other rare enzyme deficiencies, such as Fabry disease, can be improved, but not effectively treated, by prescription enzyme replacement therapy ( 95499 ). Some patients lack adequate pancreatic enzymes due to various conditions, including chronic pancreatitis and cystic fibrosis. Pancreatic enzyme deficiency can be treated with pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy ( 95496 , 95548 ).

#### Degradation effects

Enzyme therapy has been used to enhance the breakdown of foods or tissues. For instance, topically applied enzymes can be used for wound debridement ( 18275 , 91113 ). Oral enzymes have also been proposed for augmenting digestion ( 97713 ) or eliminating bezoars ( 100201 , 100202 ).

#### Immunologic effects

Enzyme therapy has also been considered for immunostimulant purposes. There are several theories for this proposed effect. Enzymes can lyse bacteria in the gut, possibly creating byproducts which stimulate the immune system. Enzymes can also degrade components of microbial cell walls, making them more susceptible to attack by the immune system ( 95495 ).
