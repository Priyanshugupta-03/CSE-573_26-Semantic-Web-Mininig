## Overview

Justicia pectoralis is a flowering herb that is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including South and Central America, and some Caribbean islands including Trinidad and Tobago ( 29151 , 29155 , 103381 ).

## History

Some individuals native to tropical regions of the Americas have used Justicia pectoralis as an intoxicating snuff during rituals ( 38446 ). However, analysis of constituents from the plant extract suggests that this plant does not contain hallucinogenic alkaloids ( 29148 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, Justicia pectoralis is traditionally used for allergies, anxiety, asthma, bronchitis, colic, cough, common cold, congestion, diabetes, dysmenorrhea, epilepsy, fever, hypertension, insomnia, menopausal symptoms, pneumonia, premenstrual syndrome (PMS), nausea and other stomach complaints, rickets, tuberculosis, and to induce labor. Topically, Justicia pectoralis leaves have been crushed or dampened for skin rash, bruising, and wound healing. Justicia pectoralis has also been used as an insect repellent.

#### General

The applicable part of Justicia pectoralis is the leaf ( 103381 ). Justicia pectoralis contains coumarin, 7-hydroxycoumarin (umbelliferone), dihydrocoumarin, 3-(2-hydroxyphenyl)propionic acid, and flavonoids including apigenin and ellagic acid ( 29156 , 103381 ). Hallucinogenic alkaloids have not been identified in Justicia pectoralis ( 29148 ).

#### Anti-asthmatic effects

Justicia pectoralis is traditionally used for asthma, bronchitis, and other lung conditions. In isolated guinea-pig trachea, Justicia pectoralis extract had bronchodilator effects ( 29153 ).

#### Anti-inflammatory effects

Justicia pectoralis is traditionally used as an anti-inflammatory agent. In vitro, Justicia pectoralis extract inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX)-2 activity and inhibits the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines ( 29150 , 103381 ). However, in animal research, although the extract had antinociceptive effects, it did not have anti-inflammatory effects ( 29153 ).

#### Antibacterial effects

Justicia pectoralis is traditionally used as an antibacterial agent. In vitro, it has shown antibacterial effects against some, but not all, species tested ( 29151 , 103381 ).

#### Hormonal effects

Justicia pectoralis is traditionally used for menopausal symptoms and premenstrual syndrome (PMS). In vitro, Justicia pectoralis extract binds to the progesterone receptor and the alpha- and beta-estrogenic receptors, inducing the expression of estrogen responsive genes ( 29150 ).
