# Jaborandi
Scientific Name Pilocarpus microphyllus Family Rutaceae Other Common Names Arruda Bravam, Arruda Do Mato, Jamguarandi, Juarandi, Maranhao Jaborandi, Pilocarpus Jaborandi
|

---

## Overview

Jaborandi is a plant found in Brazil. It has a traditional history of use in medicine, but is no longer used due to safety concerns. Jaborandi has been used in the production of pilocarpine ( 18 ). Avoid confusing jaborandi with Pilocarpus jaborandi (Pernambuco jaborandi) ( 19 ) and Pilocarpus pennatifolius (Paraguay jaborandi).

## People Use This For

Orally, jaborandi is used as a diaphoretic and to treat diarrhea. Topically, jaborandi is used for glaucoma.

Unsafe when the leaf is used orally or topically due to its pilocarpine constituent. The lethal dose of jaborandi is estimated to be 5-10 grams of leaf ( 18 ).

PREGNANCY: Unsafe when used orally or topically because it has teratogenic and uterine stimulant effects and contains pilocarpine( 19 ).

LACTATION: Unsafe when used orally or topically because it contains pilocarpine ( 18 ).

|

#### General

Orally, jaborandi can cause toxicity. Symptoms of jaborandi poisoning include bradycardia, bronchospasm, colic, cardiac collapse and possible arrest, convulsions, hypotension, dyspnea, nausea, severe salivation, strong secretion of sweat, and vomiting. The lethal oral dose of jaborandi leaf is approximately 5-10 grams ( 18 ).

#### Cardiovascular

Orally, cardiovascular symptoms of jaborandi poisoning include bradycardia, cardiac collapse and possible arrest, and hypotension ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, gastrointestinal symptoms of jaborandi poisoning include colic, nausea, and vomiting ( 18 ).

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, neurologic symptoms of jaborandi poisoning include convulsions, severe salivation, and strong secretion of sweat ( 18 ).

#### Pulmonary/Respiratory

Orally, pulmonary symptoms of jaborandi poisoning include bronchospasm and dyspnea ( 18 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of jaborandi.

#### INTRAOCULAR PRESSURE

Jaborandi might reduce intraocular pressure due to its pilocarpine content ( 15 , 18 ).

The lethal oral dose of the pilocarpine constituent in jaborandi is approximately 60 mg, corresponding to approximately 5-10 grams of jaborandi leaf ( 18 ).

#### General

The applicable part of jaborandi is the leaf. Jaborandi contains the parasympathetic system stimulants pilocarpine and epiisopilosine. Other alkaloids include pilosine, isopilosine, epiisopiloturine, and macaubine ( 18 , 102775 ).

#### Cholinergic effects

Pilocarpine, a constituent of jaborandi, has cholinergic activity and stimulates saliva secretion, sweat, and smooth muscle contraction in the gastrointestinal tract ( 18 ). It also causes ocular miosis ( 15 ).
