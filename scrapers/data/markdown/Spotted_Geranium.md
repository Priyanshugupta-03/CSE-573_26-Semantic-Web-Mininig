## Overview

Spotted geranium is a plant. It is native to North America, especially Eastern Canada and North Central and Eastern United States ( 99271 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, spotted geranium is used for diarrhea, cholera, improving digestion, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), canker sores, and gum disease. Topically, spotted geranium is used for skin wounds, bleeding sores, hemorrhoids, vaginal discharge, and thrush.

#### General

The applicable parts of spotted geranium are the whole plant and root. The whole plant contains a high concentration of tannins. The root contains a higher concentration of tannins at about 10% to 20% ( 4078 , 16658 ).

#### Anti-diarrheal effects

The spotted geranium plant contains a high concentration of tannins. Tannins have an astringent effect on mucosal tissue. This effect dehydrates the tissue, reduces secretions, and forms a protective layer of constricted cells ( 4078 , 16658 ).

#### Antibacterial effect

Spotted geranium has been evaluated for antimicrobial activity. However, it does not demonstrate activity against Vibrio cholera in vitro ( 16657 ).

#### Wound healing effects
