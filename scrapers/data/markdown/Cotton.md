## Overview

Cotton is a plant that is commonly used for commercial purposes ( 46324 ). The root and seed of cotton are also used in medicine ( 18 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, cotton is used for amenorrhea, dysmenorrhea, menopause symptoms, poor lactation, nausea, fever, headache, diarrhea, dysentery, urethritis, nerve inflammation, hemorrhage, as an oxytocic, and to expel afterbirth. Cotton has been also used as an anti-fertility drug in males. Topically, cotton is used in vaginal contraceptive preparations.

Possibly Safe when preparations of the root bark, seed meal, and seed oil are used in amounts found in foods. The United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and Health Canada limit the content of free gossypol, a constituent of cotton, in products used in foods to less than 450 ppm ( 12 , 4912 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of cotton when used orally or topically in medicinal amounts.

PREGNANCY: Likely Unsafe when used orally because it is a possible abortifacient and uterine stimulant ( 12 ).

LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

There is a limited amount of information available about the adverse effects of cotton. Orally, cotton root bark, seed meal, and oil are considered safe for use in foods when the levels of gossypol, a constituent of cotton, do not exceed 450 ppm ( 12 , 4912 ). However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Malaria.

Preliminary clinical research shows that taking 125 mL of a specific preparation (AM-1) containing cotton, winter cherry, Jatropha curcas, and Delonix regia orally six times daily for two days, then three times daily for 5 days, eliminates malaria parasites in patients with malaria ( 46308 ). The validity of these findings is limited by the lack of a control group. Also, it is not clear if this effect is due to cotton, the other ingredients, or the combination.

More evidence is needed to rate cotton for this use.

#### Adult
Oral:

General : Traditionally, cotton root bark is prepared by boiling one teaspoon in a covered container with 3 cups of water for 30 minutes. The liquid is cooled slowly in the closed container and taken cold as 1 to 2 cups per day ( 5254 ).

#### UROGENITAL IRRITATION OR SENSITIVITY

Contraindicated ( 12 ).

None known.

In animals, long-term feeding with cotton seed has been linked to poisonings and death related to gossypol, a cotton constituent. Gossypol should be removed from cotton before using as animal feed ( 18 , 46327 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of cotton.

#### General

The applicable parts of cotton are the root bark and seed ( 18 ). Gossypol is a constituent of cotton seed and root bark ( 6 , 46318 ). Cotton seeds contain oligosaccharides and pectin ( 46311 , 46322 ).

#### Breast milk effects

Cotton extract induces an increase in complement components 3 (C3) and 4 (C4) in the colostrum of lactating mothers when administered orally ( 46322 ).

#### Inflammatory effects

Some evidence suggests that cotton root can cause histamine release ( 18 ).
