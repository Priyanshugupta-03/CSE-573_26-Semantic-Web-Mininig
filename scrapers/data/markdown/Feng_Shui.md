## Overview

Feng shui, meaning "wind and water," is an ancient Chinese philosophy focused on the relationship between people and their environment. It aims to maximize the beneficial movement of energy (qi) through a space ( 102933 , 102934 ). Feng shui originated during the Zhou Dynasty, which lasted from 11th Century B.C.E. to 256 B.C.E. ( 102933 ). Traditional feng shui is a Chinese science that addresses the design and layout of cities, villages, dwellings, buildings, home decorations, and furniture. The goal of feng shui is to create an attractive, safe, and nurturing space in which an individual can live and work more comfortably and productively ( 102933 , 102934 ). Today, feng shui is applied to existing structures and communities. It is also practiced as a complementary therapy and is sometimes used in health care setting design and hospital construction ( 102932 , 102933 ).

Likely Safe when used appropriately. Feng shui has been used for thousands of years with apparent safety ( 102932 , 102933 , 102934 ). Although feng shui has never been thoroughly evaluated in clinical research, there is no reason to expect safety issues.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of feng shui.

Feng shui uses shapes, colors, textures, sound, light, symbolic imagery, and the arrangement of furniture to adjust the energy of a home. Positive influences are encouraged and enhanced, while negative factors are corrected and minimized ( 102933 , 102934 ). Feng shui uses a specialized compass called a luopan and an array of calculations to determine the arrangement of a space. Also, spaces are instructed to be aligned with their ba gua, or the map of its energetic influences. Each area of the ba gua theoretically corresponds to a different aspect of an individual's life. The bottom edge of the ba gua is aligned with the wall of the front door, and then the ba gua is modified to cover the entire space. To apply the ba gua to an individual room, the same ideas of alignment and modification apply. If there is more than one way to enter a space, the ba gua should be oriented to the most prominent or frequently used entry ( 102934 ).

Feng shui, often called the art of placement and the art of flow, has foundation texts, core theories, and methods based on archeological discoveries and the work of archeoastronomers. Feng shui is founded on the basic principle that all things in the physical world are endowed with physical energy, or qi, and that this physical energy, which is constantly changing, connects every physical thing. Feng shui is proposed to work by directing the flow of qi through a physical space ( 102934 ). In theory, when qi is blocked or weak, an individual may feel tired, depressed or unable to focus. In contrast, someone with an overabundance of qi may feel out of control, overly emotional, or anxious. Fixing an overabundance or deficiency of qi with feng shui is intended to help with an individual's tiredness, depression, anxiety, and overall quality of life ( 102933 , 102934 ). Additionally, qi is constantly balancing between two extremes - yin and yang. Yin is the feminine side of nature and includes tranquility, darkness, cold, wetness, and depth. Yang is masculine and represents light, heat, activity, dryness, and height. Yin and yang are not the same as good and bad. Rather, they are considered complementary forces, which feng shui attempts to balance ( 102934 ). Although feng shui has a long history of traditional use, research evaluating its benefits is limited. Recently, it has been considered for use in hospitals and patient care spaces in an effort to benefit both the patients and the employees. However, it is unclear if this application is beneficial ( 102932 ).
