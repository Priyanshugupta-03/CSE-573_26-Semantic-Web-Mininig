## Overview

Expressive therapies combine some form of expression, such as art, music, dance, drama, poetry, creative writing, and/or play, with some form of therapy, including psychotherapy, counseling, rehabilitation, or health care. Each form of expressive therapy is thought to have a unique role in therapeutic work depending on its application, practitioner, patient, and setting ( 101203 ). Refer to our list of Expressive Therapies to find more information on specific modalities.

Expressive therapies represent a wide group of modalities that are used alone or in combination. See specific modalities for safety information.
