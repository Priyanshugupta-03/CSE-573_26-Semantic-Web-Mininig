## Overview

The Jenny Craig diet is a diet plan that combines elements of social support, pre-packaged meals, food and nutrition counseling, and exercise in an effort to assist clients in losing weight. It was designed by registered dieticians in consultation with a multidisciplinary medical advisory board. Though there are several different levels of participation available in the program, each focuses on assisting the participant as he or she works toward a weight loss goal and, eventually, weight maintenance. In addition to enrollment costs, the Jenny Craig diet requires that its followers purchase pre-packaged food products at the start and the middle of the diet ( 103149 ).

Likely Safe when used appropriately. The Jenny Craig diet has been widely used without reports of safety concerns ( 103148 , 103149 ). There is also no reason to expect safety issues.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Jenny Craig diet in pregnancy and lactation. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues, as long as usual nutritional needs are met.

#### General

The Jenny Craig diet appears to be well tolerated when followed appropriately. No adverse effects have been reported.

Obesity.

Most clinical research shows that the diet results in short-term weight loss when compared with baseline, although it is unclear if it is more effective than other dieting programs.

Preliminary clinical research in females with overweight or obesity shows that participating in the Jenny Craig at Home diet program for 12 weeks lowers caloric intake, body mass, waist circumference, and fat mass when compared with no diet intervention. Participating in the Jenny Craig diet resulted in approximately 5 kg body weight loss. However, these improvements were similar to Weight Watchers Points Plus, Curves Complete, and Nutrisystem Advance Select diet programs ( 103148 ). A prospective observational study of over 60,000 adults also suggests that participating in the Jenny Craig Platinum program is associated with 5 kg of body weight loss. However, most participants drop out of the program within 6 months and the percent weight loss was directly correlated to the amount of time a person remained in the program. The retention rate at 1 month was 73%, at 3 months, 42%, at 6 months, 22%, and at 1 year, 7% ( 103149 ). A meta-analysis of clinical trials in overweight or obese adults shows that participating in the Jenny Craig diet results in modestly more overall weight loss, but is less cost effective (measured as price per kg of weight lost) than the Weight Watchers diet ( 96824 ). There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of the Jenny Craig diet for its other uses.

The Jenny Craig diet is based around various elements which are intended to aid a person in achieving weight loss goals. These elements are the prepackaged Jenny Craig food products, a supportive counselor, accountability, and learning to cope with situations that provoke overeating or consumption of unhealthy, high calorie foods. This combination of techniques is intended to increase a person's likelihood for successful weight loss ( 103149 ).
