## Overview

Angostura is a shrub-like tree native to Venezuela and other tropical areas of South America. It grows 4-5 meters high and has smooth, gray bark ( 18 ). The bark is used in medicine ( 31308 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, angostura is used for fever, diarrhea, preventing recurrence of malaria, and as an antispasmodic. Large doses of angostura can be cathartic and emetic. In foods, angostura is used in alcoholic beverages. However, angostura bitters no longer contain angostura, but gentian and other bitters.

Likely Safe when used orally in amounts commonly found in foods. Angostura has Generally Recognized As Safe status (GRAS) for use in foods in the US ( 4912 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the use of angostura in medicinal amounts.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available.

#### General

Orally, angostura is well tolerated when used in amounts commonly found in foods ( 4912 ). Large doses of angostura may cause nausea and vomiting ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, large doses of angostura may cause nausea and vomiting ( 18 ).

#### General

The applicable part of angostura is the bark. The bark contains angostura bitters 1 and 2, alkaloids, including cuspareine, cusparine, and galipine, and a volatile oil ( 11 , 30308 , 31304 , 31305 , 31306 ).

#### Antimalarial effects

Angostura is traditionally used to prevent a recurrence of malaria. Galipinine and other alkaloids in angostura have antimalarial effects in vitro ( 31307 ).

#### Antispasmodic effects

Angostura is traditionally used as an antispasmodic. The alkaloids, cusparine and galipine, have antispasmodic effects in vitro ( 11 ).
