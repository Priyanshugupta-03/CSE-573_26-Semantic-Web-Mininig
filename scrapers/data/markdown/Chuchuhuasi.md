|

---

## Overview

Chuchuhuasi is a tree that is native to South America ( 90456 ). It can be found in tropical rainforests of Bolivia, Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru ( 90456 , 90458 ). It can grow to be 30 meters in height and has reddish-brown bark that is tough and heavy ( 90457 ). The bark, roots, and leaves of chuchuhuasi have been used as medicine ( 90456 , 90457 ).

## History

Chuchuhuasi has been used as a medicine for centuries by indigenous people of the Amazon rainforest ( 90456 ). The name chuchuhuasi, which means "trembling back," originates from the tree's traditional use as a treatment for joint and back pain ( 90459 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, chuchuhuasi is used for arthritis, joint pain, back pain, broken bones, diarrhea, postpartum complications, and sexual arousal. It is also used as a tonic and as a flavoring agent.

#### General

The applicable parts of chuchuhuasi are the bark, root, and leaf ( 90456 , 90457 ). Some constituents of chuchuhuasi include sesquiterpenes, triterpenoids, flavonoids, and alkaloids ( 26266 , 26267 , 26268 , 26269 , 26270 , 26271 , 26272 , 26273 , 26274 ) ( 26276 , 26277 , 26278 ).

#### Anti-cancer effects

Chuchuhuasi is of interest for skin cancer. Antineoplastic effects of some chuchuhuasi constituents, such as the sesquiterpenes, have been demonstrated in vitro ( 26274 , 26265 , 26267 , 26277 ).
