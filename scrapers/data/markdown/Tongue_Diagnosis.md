## Overview

Tongue diagnosis is a technique used in traditional Asian systems of medicine including traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) and traditional Indian medicine (Ayurveda). It is often used in conjunction with pulse diagnosis. The color and other qualities of the tongue are correlated with various illnesses ( 99487 ).

There is insufficient reliable information about the safety of tongue diagnosis. Although tongue diagnosis is a non-invasive procedure, and it has been used with apparent safety in two small clinical trials ( 99487 , 99488 ), this technique should not be relied upon to identify or diagnose medical conditions in place of modern diagnostic techniques.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### General

Tongue diagnosis is a non-invasive procedure and not be expected to cause harm. No adverse effects have been reported in clinical research ( 99487 , 99488 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of tongue diagnosis for identifying or diagnosing medical conditions.

Tongue diagnosis is a technique used in traditional Asian systems of medicine including traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) and traditional Indian medicine (Ayurveda). Practitioners of these systems of medicine assess the tongue for color and other qualities. A normal tongue would be elastic, have a light red color with a thin white coating, no cracks, and have normal sublingual veins. Abnormalities on the tongue signify certain ailments ( 15913 , 99487 ). Practitioners of these systems of medicine assess the tongue for color, swelling, teeth-marks, and other qualities. In some cases, a digital imaging device has been tried to aid in tongue diagnosis ( 99487 , 99488 ), although there is debate over whether digital imaging devices improve accuracy or reliability.

A fundamental belief of tongue diagnosis is that all organs are interconnected via a network of channels called the meridians.  It is  believed that certain tongue qualities correlate with energy imbalances or humoral imbalances in specific organs or areas of the body. Therapies are often guided based on these tongue qualities. However, there is no reliable scientific evidence that tongue diagnosis is an accurate indicator of medical conditions or disease ( 15913 , 99487 ).
