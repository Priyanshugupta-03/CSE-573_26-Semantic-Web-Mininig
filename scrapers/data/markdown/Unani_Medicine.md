## Overview

Unani literally means "Greek." Unani medicine is a medical system that developed in Greece during a time of Islamic influence. It resembles traditional Greek and Ayurvedic medical systems ( 99721 ). Unani medicine is not recognized in North America but is currently practiced in Asia and is especially popular in India ( 99722 ). Unani medicine may include herbs, meditation, bodywork, cupping, sweating, and leeches or bloodletting ( 99721 ). Unani medicine is often credited to Hakim Ibn Sina (Avicenna) who enriched the system by compiling observations and information about the practice into a compendium known as Al-Qanoon-fit-Tibb (Canon of Medicine) around 1000 AD ( 99721 ). However, the system includes concepts and practices from many other scholars, including Hippocrates, Pythagoras, Aristotle, Discorides, Galen, and Rhazes. Unani medicine was introduced in India in 1351 AD ( 99721 ).

Unani medicine is a system of medicine that was developed in Greece during a time of Islamic influence. It is practiced throughout Asia and India ( 15989 ). Unani medicine has many similarities to both the traditional Greek and Ayurvedic systems of medicine ( 99721 , 99723 ). A practitioner of Unani medicine is referred to as a hakim ( 99723 ). Unani medicine includes similar theories and principles to Ayurvedic medicine regarding temperament. Temperament is based on the four key elements, specifically air, earth, water, and fire ( 99721 , 99723 ). Every organism and form of matter is considered to have a temperament that is affected by the actions and reactions of the elements. Medications and foods are classified according to temperament ( 99721 ). The temperament of the human body, however, is defined by principles that draw from traditional Greek medicine. The human body is thought to be composed of three parts - solid, known as organs; liquid, known as humors; and gas, known as pneuma ( 99721 , 99723 ). The four major "humors" define the temperament of the body and include Dam (blood), Balgham (phlegm), Safra (yellow bile) and Sauda (black bile) ( 99722 , 99723 ). In a healthy individual, these humors are in a state of equilibrium. Imbalance is thought to alter the physiological functions of the human body due to abnormal temperament of the affected organ or system, resulting in disease ( 99722 ). Diagnosis in Unani medicine, similar to Ayurveda and traditional Chinese medicine, involves pulse diagnosis. Unani medicine also utilizes tests of the stool and urine ( 15989 ). One difference between Unani medicine and Western medicine is that a patient is diagnosed and treated based on their temperament in addition to their physical illness. Treatments are focused on the rebalancing of the elements and humors ( 99721 , 99722 ) Therapies in Unani medicine have similarities to those in Ayurveda and often include various foods and herbs ( 15989 ). Additional therapies utilized in Unani medicine include cupping, diaphoresis, diuresis, Turkish bath, massage, counterirritation, leeching, bloodletting, purging, and exercise ( 99722 , 99723 ).
