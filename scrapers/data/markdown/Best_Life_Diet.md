## Overview

The Best Life Diet is a 3-phase diet and exercise plan developed by exercise physiologist and certified personal trainer Bob Greene. The diet was popularized after the publication of a book by the same name in 2006. The Best Life Diet focuses on implementing gradual lifestyle changes to encourage long-term weight maintenance. Some brand name foods carry a Best Life seal of approval to indicate that they are approved as part of the Best Life Diet ( 102310 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of the Best Life Diet. However, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; however, there is no reason to expect safety issues as long as nutritional needs are met.

#### General

A thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted; however, safety concerns are not expected as long as proper nutrition is maintained.

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Obesity.

Although there has been interest in using the Best Life Diet for weight loss, there is insufficient reliable information about the clinical effects of the Best Life Diet for this purpose.

More evidence is needed to rate the Best Life Diet for this use.

The Best Life Diet is divided into 3 phases that describe a gradual process of increasing fiber intake and exercise as well as decreasing fat and sugar intake. Prior to starting the first phase, adherents must analyze their psychological associations with food, eating, and weight loss in order to eliminate negative eating habits. Adherents are also expected to increase physical activity ( 102310 ). Phase 1 lasts for approximately 4 weeks and includes general eating and exercise guidelines. Food is limited to 3 meals and 1 snack per day and should only be eaten until slightly full. Each meal should be consumed with a multivitamin, an omega-3 supplement, and a calcium supplement. Individuals are advised to avoid alcohol and to avoid eating less than 2 hours before bed. Phase 1 is only completed once an individual loses at least 4 pounds ( 102310 ). Phase 2 involves the elimination of foods such as soda, trans fats, fried foods, products made of white flour, regular pasta, and high-fat dairy. Some of these foods may be substituted with low-fat and whole grain versions. In addition, individuals are instructed to exercise more frequently. Phase 2 lasts at least 4 weeks and ends when the individual achieves target weight ( 102310 ). Phase 3 is intended to last indefinitely and involves continuing healthier eating habits and regular physical activity to maintain target weight ( 102310 ).

None known.

The Best Life Diet focuses on the use of psychological analysis to allow individuals to examine their eating motivations and associations with food. The creator states that individuals who are not fully aware of their psychological associations with eating are less likely to succeed in following the diet. This analysis is intended to allow for examination of perceived weight problems, motivations to lose weight, and reasons that weight loss has not been maintained in the past. Theoretically, this analysis and a gradual transition to healthier eating rituals and behaviors would help individuals to achieve and maintain weight loss ( 102310 ).
