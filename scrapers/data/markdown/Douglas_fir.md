## Overview

Douglas fir is a tree that grows up to 60 meters in height. It is native to western North America, particularly the Pacific Coast. Douglas fir essential oil is an oleoresin collected from the trunk of the Douglas fir tree ( 11 ). This essential oil has traditionally been applied topically for wound healing.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Douglas fir.

#### General

The applicable part of Douglas fir is the resin obtained from the trunk of the tree ( 11 ). Major constituents of Douglas fir essential oil include sabinene, alpha-pinene, beta-pinene, alpha-terpinolene, gamma-terpinene, and te erpinene-ol ( 109507 ).

#### Antibacterial effects

Douglas fir essential oil has demonstrated in vitro activity against Staphylococcus aureus and Pseudomonas aeruginosa ( 109507 ).

#### Antifungal effects

Douglas fir essential oil has demonstrated in vitro activity against Candida albicans ( 109507 ).

#### Insecticide effects

Douglas fir essential oil has demonstrated insecticidal activity against Drosophila melanogaster larvae ( 109507 ).
