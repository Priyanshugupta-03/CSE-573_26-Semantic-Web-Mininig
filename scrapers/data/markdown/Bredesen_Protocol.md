## Overview

The Bredesen protocol is a therapeutic program of specific dietary and lifestyle changes designed by Dale Bredesen, a physician and researcher specializing in neurodegenerative diseases. The protocol is intended to optimize metabolic parameters in Alzheimer disease and reverse or stop its advancement ( 93534 ).

## People Use This For

The Bredesen Protocol is used for Alzheimer disease, mild cognitive impairment, and neurodegenerative disorders.

Alzheimer disease.

Case reports in patients with Alzheimer disease suggest that using the Bredesen protocol for 3-6 months might improve cognition and increase ability to return to work. In one patient, the improvement was sustained for 2.5 years ( 93534 ). Prospective, controlled clinical trials are needed to confirm these findings.

More evidence is needed to rate the effectiveness of the Bredesen protocol for this use.

The Bredesen protocol uses multiple therapies to optimize metabolic parameters in Alzheimer disease in order to balance plasticity network components. Dr. Bredesen theorizes that Alzheimer disease results from an imbalance in endogenous plasticity signaling. This involves development, maintenance, repair, and remodeling of neuronal synapses. Physiological mediators involved in these processes include the beta-amyloid precursor protein, Apolipoprotein E (ApoE), tau, and other peptides. Individualized supplementation and dietary and lifestyle interventions aim to optimize or decrease these mediators in the body. The goal is to impact enough network components so that the pathogenic process of Alzheimer disease is halted or reversed ( 93534 ). To accomplish optimization, the goals of therapy are intensive. For example, the desired vitamin D level is higher than normal levels and the desired C-reactive protein level is lower than normal levels. Goals of therapy include reducing inflammation, stimulating the brain, correcting hormone imbalance, optimizing oxygenation, optimizing mitochondrial function, and others. The protocol is personalized and continuously changed over time based on individual laboratory values pertaining to the plasticity network ( 93534 ).
