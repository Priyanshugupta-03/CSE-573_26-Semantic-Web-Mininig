## Overview

Atlantic cedar is a type of tree. The oil is used in medicine ( 5177 ).

## People Use This For

Topically, Atlantic cedar is used for alopecia areata. It is also used as an insect repellent. In manufacturing, Atlantic cedar oils are used as fragrance or fixatives in cosmetics, soaps, and perfumes.

Possibly Safe when the oil is used topically and appropriately. Atlantic cedar oil has been used with apparent safety for up to 7 months ( 5177 ).

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of Atlantic cedar when used orally.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

|

#### General

Topically, Atlantic cedar oil seems to be generally well tolerated. However, there is some evidence that Atlantic cedar oil is allergenic and a local irritant ( 11 ).

#### Dermatologic

Topically, Atlantic cedar oil is a local irritant ( 11 ).

#### Immunologic

Topically, Atlantic cedar oil can cause allergic reactions in sensitive individuals ( 11 ).

Insufficient Reliable Evidence to Rate
Alopecia areata.

There is some evidence that topical application of a combination oil containing lavender, thyme, rosemary, and Atlantic cedar essential oils improves hair growth in up to 44% of patients after 7 months of treatment ( 5177 ). The validity of this finding is limited by the lack of a comparator group. Additionally, it is not clear if this effect is due to Atlantic cedar, the other ingredients, or the combination.

More evidence is needed to rate Atlantic cedar for this use.

#### Adult

Alopecia areata : A combination of essential oils, including Atlantic cedar 2 drops or 94 mg, rosemary 3 drops or 114 mg, thyme 2 drops or 88 mg, and lavender 3 drops or 108 mg, mixed with 3 mL jojoba oil and 20 mL grapeseed oil, has been massaged into the scalp for 2 minutes each night with a warm towel placed around the head to increase absorption ( 5177 ).

#### General

The applicable part of Atlantic cedar is the essential oil ( 5177 ). The main components of the oil are gamma-cardinene, beta-farnescene, viridiflorol, and himachala-2,4-diene ( 111194 ).

#### Antimicrobial effects

In vitro, Atlantic cedar essential oil has antibacterial activity against Gram positive organisms, and weaker activity against Gram negative organisms and biofilm-forming bacteria. It also has activity against fungi and yeasts. The vapor phase of the oil can inhibit the growth of filamentous fungi, such as Penicillium species, on food ( 111194 ).

#### Antioxidant effects

Atlantic cedar essential oil is reported to have strong antioxidant effects ( 111194 ).

#### Insecticidal activity

The vapor phase of Atlantic cedar has insecticidal activity against the European firebug (Pyrrhocoris apterus). The constituents responsible are thought to be himachalol and beta-himachalene ( 111194 ).
