## Overview

Holly is a flowering evergreen tree or shrub from the genus Ilex which grows in temperate and subtropical climates and can reach up to 7 meters in height ( 107872 ). Many Ilex species are referred to as holly. English holly (Ilex aquifolium) and American holly (Ilex opaca) are used as ornamental Christmas holly ( 6 ). Yaupon (Ilex vomitoria), Appalachian tea (Ilex glabra), and cassena (Ilex cassine) may also be included in discussions of holly ( 6 ).

## History

Historically, American holly (Ilex opaca) fruit tea was used as a cardiac stimulant by American Indians. Yaupon (Ilex vomitoria) was used as an emetic, and Yaupon was used as a ceremonial "cleanser" in South America.

## People Use This For

Orally, preparations of holly leaf are used for congestive heart failure (CHF), constipation, coronary heart disease (CHD), cough, digestive disorders, fever, jaundice, hypertension, rheumatoid arthritis (RA), and vertigo.

#### General

Orally, ingestion of holly leaf can cause GI irritation, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting ( 6 , 18 ). Holly leaf spines may tear or puncture skin or mucous membranes ( 6 ). Ingestion of as few as 2 holly berries can cause vomiting and diarrhea in small children ( 6 ). Ingestion of more than 5 holly berries can cause nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea ( 6 , 18 ). Ingestion of 20-30 holly berries can cause death ( 18 ).

#### Gastrointestinal

Orally, ingestion of holly leaf can cause GI irritation, diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting ( 6 , 18 ). Holly leaf spines may tear or puncture skin or mucous membranes ( 6 ). #### General

The applicable parts of holly are the leaf and berry. Constituents of holly include saponins and a cyanogenic glycoside ( 4 , 6 ). An aqueous extract of the leaves contains terpenoids, including amyrin, lupeol, ursolic acid, and ursolic aldehyde; sugars including sucrose, maltose, and cellobiose; and phenolic acids including caffeic acid, malic acid, quinic acid, and ferulic acid.

#### Gastrointestinal effects

Saponin constituents of holly are thought to cause GI irritation and emetic effects ( 6 ).

#### Hypocholesterolemic effects

In rats fed a high-cholesterol diet, giving holly (specifically Ilex aquifolium) leaf extract attenuates increases in total cholesterol, low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, triglycerides, and weight when compared with the high-cholesterol diet alone. It also reduces oxidative lipid degradation and hepatic steatosis ( 107872 ).
