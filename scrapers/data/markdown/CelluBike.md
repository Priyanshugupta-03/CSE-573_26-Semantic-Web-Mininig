## Overview

The CelluBike is an exercise bike that combines pedaling exercise with infrared light. The CelluBike looks like a small vessel with a bicycle inside. A person is intended to exercise on the reclining bicycle while 27 high-intensity infrared lamps shine on the person's thighs ( 102924 ). Despite a lack of supporting evidence, the manufacturer of the CelluBike technology promotes CelluBike for reducing cellulite. There have also been a number of unsupported claims for other therapeutic uses of this device ( 102924 ).

## People Use This For

CelluBike has been promoted for reducing cellulite.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of CelluBike.

#### General

No adverse effects have been reported. However, a thorough evaluation of safety outcomes has not been conducted.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the effectiveness of CelluBike.

CelluBike technicians generally recommend three 40-minute sessions per week for a total of five weeks. During the sessions, the user is instructed to pedal on the CelluBike at about 75% of his or her maximum heart rate. A technician then adjusts the angle and intensity of the 27 infrared lights surrounding the person, which may vary every session ( 102924 ). In addition to receiving Cellubike sessions, individuals are advised to eat a healthy diet, drink large quantities of water, and to avoid smoking and drinking alcohol ( 102924 ).

Cellulite forms when fat cells accumulate inside the spaces of the connective network of septa fibers below the skin. Normally, the septa fibers anchor fat tissue to the deep muscles. However, if the fat grows, the septa may not be able to anchor it as effectively, so the fat comes closer to the skin surface and forms cellulite. Cellulite does not have any negative health repercussions and commonly occurs on the hips, thighs, and buttocks. The CelluBike is promoted for burning fat, softening cellulite, repairing the septa, and increasing blood circulation. It proposes to do this through the use of infrared light combined with physical exertion. This is said to increase circulation and collagen production to help repair the septa and reduce cellulite in the body ( 102924 ).
