## Overview

Cheken is an evergreen shrub or small tree. It is native to Chile and cultivated in Bolivia and Peru. The leaf and leaf oil are used in medicine ( 18 ). KEY HIGHLIGHTS

- Traditionally used for diarrhea, fever, gout, and other conditions, but there is no strong evidence to support any use.
- There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of oral use.
- No known major interactions.

## People Use This For

Orally, cheken leaf preparations are used as a tonic, diuretic, and digestive aid, and for cough, diarrhea, fever, gout, and hypertension. Preparations of the leaf oil are used orally for hyperlipoproteinemia.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the safety of cheken.

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Insufficient reliable information available; avoid using.

#### Adult
Oral:

General : Cheken is typically used as a liquid extract or as tea. The leaves are boiled in water for 10-15 minutes and then strained ( 18 ).
