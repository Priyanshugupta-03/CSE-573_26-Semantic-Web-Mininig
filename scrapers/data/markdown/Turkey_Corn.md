## Overview

Turkey corn is a plant native to the United States and Canada ( 106513 ). The dried tuber of turkey corn has been traditionally used for digestive complaints, menstrual disorders, urinary tract diseases, and skin rashes ( 18 ).

## People Use This For

Orally, turkey corn is used for digestive and menstrual disorders, urinary tract diseases, and skin rashes.

Possibly Unsafe when used orally. Theoretically, the constituent bicuculline, an antagonist of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), could cause poisoning ( 18 ).

PREGNANCY AND LACTATION: Possibly Unsafe when used orally ( 18 ); avoid using.

#### General

The applicable part of turkey corn is the dried tuber. It contains various isoquinoline alkaloids, including bicuculline, corlumine, protopine, cryptopine, and cularine ( 18 ).

#### Diuretic effects

Turkey corn is thought to have diuretic properties ( 18 ).

#### GABAergic effects

Bicuculline, a constituent of turkey corn, is a centrally-acting, spasmogenic antagonist of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) ( 18 ).
