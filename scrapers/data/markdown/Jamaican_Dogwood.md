# Jamaican Dogwood
Scientific Name Piscidia communis, Piscidia piscipula (synonyms: Erythrina piscipula, Ichthyomethia piscipula, Piscidia erythrina) Family Fabaceae/Leguminosae Other Common Names Chijol, Cornouiller de Jamaïque, Dogwood Jamaica, Fish Poison Bark, Fishfuddle, Fishfudle, Fish-Poison Tree, Florida Fishpoison Tree, Jabín, Jamaican Cornouiller, Piscidia, West Indian Dogwood
|

---

Caution

Jamaican Dogwood should not be confused with the similarly named American Dogwood .

## Overview

Jamaican dogwood is a tree. The root bark is used in medicine. However, it has no documented effectiveness and is considered to be toxic ( 4 , 11 , 18 ).

Jamaican dogwood is considered to be toxic ( 4 , 11 , 18 ).

Likely Unsafe when the root bark is used orally for self-medication because it is considered toxic ( 4 ). The elderly are particularly sensitive to the potent neuro-muscular depressant effects of Jamaican dogwood root bark ( 19 ).

CHILDREN: Likely Unsafe when used orally; avoid using. Children are particularly sensitive to the potent, neuro-muscular depressant effects of Jamaican dogwood ( 19 ).

PREGNANCY: Likely Unsafe when used orally; avoid using due to possible uterine depressant effects ( 4 ).

LACTATION: Likely Unsafe when used orally ( 4 ); avoid using.

#### General

Orally, Jamaican dogwood is an irritant and toxic to humans ( 4 ).

#### Serious Adverse Effects (Rare)

Orally : Numbness, salivation, sweating, tremors.

#### Neurologic/CNS

Orally, Jamaican dogwood root bark can cause neuro-muscular depressant effects, numbness, tremors, salivation, sedation, and sweating ( 4 , 19 ).

#### CNS DEPRESSANTS
Interaction Rating Moderate Be cautious with this combination.
Severity HIGH Occurrence POSSIBLE Level of Evidence D (Theoretical based on pharmacology)

Theoretically, taking Jamaican dogwood with drugs with sedative effects may increase the risk of sedation.

Jamaican dogwood has sedative effects ( 4 , 11 ).

#### HERBS AND SUPPLEMENTS WITH SEDATIVE PROPERTIES

Jamaican dogwood has sedative effects.

Theoretically, concomitant use with supplements with sedative activity might have additive effects ( 4 ). See other products with sedative-hypnotic activity here .

#### PERIOPERATIVE

Jamaican dogwood has CNS depressant effects. Theoretically, it might cause additive CNS depression when combined with anesthesia and other medications during and after surgical procedures. Tell patients to discontinue Jamaican dogwood at least 2 weeks before elective surgical procedures.

None known.

#### Presentation

Overdose with Jamaican dogwood may present with numbness, tremors, salivation, and sweating ( 4 , 19 ).

#### Treatment

There is insufficient reliable information available about the treatment of overdose with Jamaican dogwood.

There is insufficient reliable information available about the pharmacokinetics of Jamaican dogwood.

#### General

The applicable part of Jamaican dogwood is the root bark. Constituents include rotenone, ichtynone, and isoflavones ( 4 , 11 , 56909 ).

#### Anti-cancer effects

The Jamaican dogwood constituent rotenone has shown some anticancer activity towards lymphocytic leukemia and human epidermoid carcinoma of the nasopharynx; yet, paradoxically, rotenone is also documented to be carcinogenic ( 4 ).

#### Sedative effects

Animal studies have shown that an extract of Jamaican dogwood has sedative effects ( 4 , 11 ).

#### Spasmolytic effects

Animal studies have shown that an extract of Jamaican dogwood has antispasmodic action on smooth muscles ( 4 , 11 ). The antispasmodic effects of Jamaican dogwood extract have been at least as strong as the effects of papaverine in vitro ( 4 ). The spasmolytic effects of Jamaican dogwood may be attributable to the isoflavone constituents ( 56909 ).
